# BB Bio Chapter 9 — Digestive System

Scope: Anatomy of digestive system (alimentary canal, oral cavity to anus); ingestion & digestion (mechanical and chemical, by location); accessory organs (liver, gallbladder, pancreas); absorption & defecation.

AAMC FC mapping: FC3B (digestive portion)

Kaplan: Biology Ch 9

---

ORAL CAVITY:
- Mechanical: mastication (increases SA)
- Chemical: salivary amylase (ptyalin) starts starch digestion (alpha-1,4 bonds, pH ~7); lingual lipase (works in stomach low pH)

ESOPHAGUS:
- Peristalsis; LES prevents reflux (GERD if dysfunctional)

ANS-GI REGULATION:
- Parasympathetic (vagus): increased motility/secretions ("rest and digest")
- Sympathetic (splanchnic): decreased motility/secretions, blood shunted away

STOMACH:
- Mechanical: churning -> chyme
- Chief cells: pepsinogen -> pepsin (activated by HCl, pH ~2; endopeptidase, starts protein digestion)
- Parietal cells: HCl (H+/K+ ATPase, target of PPIs) + intrinsic factor (B12 absorption in ileum)
- G cells: gastrin (hormone) -> stim parietal HCl
- Mucous cells: mucus + bicarbonate (protect from autodigestion)
- D cells: somatostatin (inhibits gastrin)
- Limited absorption (water, alcohol, aspirin)

SMALL INTESTINE (major digestion + absorption):
- Duodenum (most digestion), jejunum (most absorption), ileum (B12 + bile salts)

Pancreatic enzymes (released to duodenum):
- Trypsinogen -> trypsin by enterokinase (brush border) -> trypsin activates other zymogens (chymotrypsinogen, procarboxypeptidase, proelastase) — prevents autodigestion (pancreatitis)
- Pancreatic lipase (TG -> 2FA + monoglyceride; needs colipase + bile salts for emulsification)
- Pancreatic amylase
- Nucleases
- Sodium bicarbonate (neutralizes chyme, pH ~7-8 for pancreatic enzymes)

Brush border enzymes (microvilli):
- Disaccharidases: maltase, sucrase, lactase
- Peptidases: aminopeptidase, dipeptidase
- Enterokinase

LARGE INTESTINE:
- Absorbs water, electrolytes (Na+), vitamins (K, some B from bacteria)
- Bacteria ferment undigested material
- Forms/stores feces

DIGESTIVE HORMONES:
- Gastrin (G cells, stomach): stim parietal HCl, motility, mucosal growth; triggered by peptides/AAs, stretch, vagal
- Secretin (S cells, duodenum): stim pancreatic BICARBONATE, inhibits gastric acid, stim bile from liver; triggered by acidic chyme
- CCK (I cells, duodenum/jejunum): stim pancreatic ENZYMES, gallbladder contraction, sphincter of Oddi relax, inhibits gastric emptying, satiety; triggered by FA/AAs
- GIP (K cells): inhibits gastric motility, stim insulin

LIVER:
- Bile production (cholesterol-derived bile salts emulsify fat); stored/concentrated in gallbladder
- Detoxification (cytochrome P450); urea cycle (NH3 -> urea)
- Gluconeogenesis, glycogen storage/lysis
- Plasma protein synthesis (albumin, clotting factors, complement)
- Cholesterol/lipoprotein synthesis (VLDL); cholesterol -> bile salts
- First-pass metabolism via hepatic portal vein
- Bilirubin conjugation
- Storage: vitamins A, D, B12, iron

ABSORPTION (SA: plicae circulares > villi > microvilli):
- Carbohydrates: monosaccharides; glucose/galactose via Na+-cotransport SGLT1; fructose via GLUT5; exit basolateral via GLUT2
- Proteins: AAs, dipeptides, tripeptides; Na+-cotransport
- Fats: FA + monoglyceride enter by simple diffusion -> re-esterified in enterocyte -> chylomicrons -> lacteals (lymph) -> thoracic duct -> blood
- Water: osmosis throughout small + large intestines
- Iron: duodenum; ferritin storage; transferrin transport
- B12: needs intrinsic factor; absorbed in terminal ileum
