# BB Chapter 5 — Lipid Structure & Function

Scope: Structural lipids, signaling lipids, energy storage lipids.

AAMC FC mapping: FC1B / FC1D (Biomolecule structure)

Kaplan: Biochem Ch 5

---

STRUCTURAL LIPIDS:
- Phospholipids (amphipathic): glycerophospholipids (phosphatidylcholine, phosphatidylserine, phosphatidylethanolamine, phosphatidylinositol), sphingomyelin
- Form the bilayer of all cellular membranes → see Ch 8
- Cholesterol: steroid nucleus (4 fused rings: 3 six-membered + 1 five-membered), membrane fluidity buffer, precursor to steroid hormones, bile salts, vitamin D
- Waxes (long-chain fatty acid + long-chain alcohol; waterproofing)

SIGNALING LIPIDS:
- Steroid hormones (cortisol, aldosterone, estrogen, testosterone, progesterone) — lipophilic, cross membrane, bind intracellular receptors
- Prostaglandins, thromboxanes, leukotrienes (derived from arachidonic acid via cyclooxygenase / lipoxygenase); inflammation, platelet aggregation, bronchoconstriction
- Fat-soluble vitamins (A, D, E, K)
- Sphingolipids (ceramide signaling; gangliosides in neuronal membranes)
- Phosphoinositides (PIP2 → IP3 + DAG in Gq pathway)

ENERGY STORAGE:
- Triacylglycerols (TAGs/triglycerides): 3 fatty acids esterified to glycerol
- Stored in adipose tissue (white fat)
- Saturated vs unsaturated fatty acids (cis double bonds introduce kinks, lower melting points, liquid at room temp = oils)
- Essential fatty acids: linoleic acid (ω-6), α-linolenic acid (ω-3)
- Lipid mobilization via hormone-sensitive lipase (→ see Ch 11)

FATTY ACID NOMENCLATURE:
- Numbering from carboxyl (C1) or from the methyl end (ω-numbering)
- Saturated: no double bonds (e.g., palmitic acid 16:0, stearic acid 18:0)
- Unsaturated: cis or trans (cis natural; trans from partial hydrogenation, cardiovascular risk)
- Trans fats pack like saturated fats (straight), unlike natural cis fats (kinked)
