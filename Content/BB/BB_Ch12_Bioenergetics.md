# BB Chapter 12 — Bioenergetics & Regulation of Metabolism

Scope: Thermodynamics/bioenergetics, role of ATP, biological oxidation/reduction, metabolic states (fed/fasting/starvation), hormonal regulation, tissue-specific metabolism, integrative metabolism.

AAMC FC mapping: FC1D (Metabolism)

Kaplan: Biochem Ch 12

---

THERMODYNAMICS / BIOENERGETICS:
- ΔG = ΔH - TΔS; spontaneity
- Coupling of exergonic to endergonic reactions via shared intermediates (ATP)
- Standard free energy (ΔG°') vs actual (depends on concentrations)

ROLE OF ATP:
- High-energy phosphoanhydride bonds; ΔG° hydrolysis ≈ -7.3 kcal/mol
- ATP / ADP / AMP ratio as energy signal
- Substrate-level vs oxidative phosphorylation

BIOLOGICAL OXIDATION / REDUCTION:
- NAD+/NADH (catabolic carrier, to ETC)
- FAD/FADH2 (catabolic carrier, to ETC)
- NADP+/NADPH (anabolic/biosynthetic reducing power; from PPP)

METABOLIC STATES:
- Fed state (insulin): glycolysis ON, glycogenesis ON, FA synthesis ON, protein synthesis ON; gluconeogenesis/glycogenolysis/β-oxidation/lipolysis OFF
- Fasting (glucagon): glycogenolysis ON, gluconeogenesis ON, beta-oxidation ON, ketogenesis begins
- Starvation (cortisol + glucagon): protein catabolism, gluconeogenesis from AAs, ketone bodies fuel brain (~60-75% after 3-5 days)

HORMONAL REGULATION:
- Insulin (β-cells): storage — glycogenesis, lipogenesis, protein synthesis, glucose uptake (GLUT4)
- Glucagon (α-cells): mobilization — glycogenolysis, gluconeogenesis, β-ox, ketogenesis
- Epinephrine (adrenal medulla): like glucagon + acts on muscle
- Cortisol (adrenal cortex): proteolysis, gluconeogenesis, lipolysis
- Thyroid hormones (T3/T4): increase BMR, O2 consumption

TISSUE-SPECIFIC METABOLISM:
- Liver (metabolic hub, all pathways; makes but cannot use ketones)
- Muscle (glycogen + protein, cannot export glucose — lacks G6Pase; uses Cori + alanine cycles)
- Brain (glucose-dependent normally; ketone-adapted during starvation; cannot use FAs)
- Adipose (TAG storage/lipolysis; HSL activated by glucagon/epi)
- RBCs (glycolysis only; lack mitochondria; produce lactate)
- Heart (aerobic; preferred fuels: FAs > ketones > lactate > glucose)

INTEGRATIVE METABOLISM:
- Malonyl-CoA = metabolic switch between FA synthesis and β-oxidation
- F-2,6-BP = metabolic switch between glycolysis and gluconeogenesis (via PFK-2, insulin/glucagon ratio)
- Citrate = crossover signal (inhibits PFK-1 in mito, activates ACC in cytoplasm)
- Urea cycle ↔ TCA linked via fumarate ("Krebs bicycle")
