# BB Chapter 4 — Carbohydrate Structure & Function

Scope: Classification of carbohydrates, cyclic sugar forms, monosaccharide properties, complex carbohydrates (di-, oligo-, polysaccharides).

AAMC FC mapping: FC1B / FC1D (Biomolecule structure)

Kaplan: Biochem Ch 4

---

CLASSIFICATION:
- By number of units: monosaccharides, disaccharides, oligosaccharides, polysaccharides
- By carbonyl: aldoses (aldehyde) vs ketoses (ketone)
- By carbon count: trioses (3C), tetroses (4C), pentoses (5C, e.g., ribose), hexoses (6C, e.g., glucose)
- D vs L sugars (D is biological; determined by OH on the chiral carbon farthest from the carbonyl)

MONOSACCHARIDES:
- Glucose, galactose, fructose, mannose (6C)
- Ribose, deoxyribose (5C; in nucleotides)
- Epimers (differ at one chiral center; e.g., glucose vs galactose at C4, glucose vs mannose at C2)
- Anomers (differ at the anomeric carbon after cyclization: α vs β)
- Mutarotation: interconversion between α and β forms in solution

CYCLIC SUGARS:
- Hemiacetal (aldose) / hemiketal (ketose) formation between carbonyl and OH
- Pyranose (6-membered ring) vs furanose (5-membered ring)
- Anomeric carbon = former carbonyl carbon; α (OH down in D-sugar) vs β (OH up)
- Haworth projections

COMPLEX CARBOHYDRATES:
- Disaccharides: maltose (α-1,4 glucose-glucose), lactose (β-1,4 galactose-glucose), sucrose (α,β-1,2 glucose-fructose)
- Glycosidic bonds: N-linked (to Asn) vs O-linked (to Ser/Thr); α vs β
- Polysaccharides:
  - Starch (α-1,4 and α-1,6, plant storage): amylose (linear), amylopectin (branched)
  - Glycogen (α-1,4 and α-1,6, animal storage; more branched than starch) — see Ch 9
  - Cellulose (β-1,4 glucose; plant structural; humans cannot digest)
  - Chitin (β-1,4 N-acetylglucosamine; arthropod/fungal cell walls)
- Reducing vs non-reducing sugars: free anomeric carbon can reduce (Tollens, Benedict's, Fehling's tests)
