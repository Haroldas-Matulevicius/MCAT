# BB Chapter 11 — Lipid & Amino Acid Metabolism

Scope: Lipid digestion/absorption, lipid mobilization, lipid transport, cholesterol metabolism, fatty acids & triacylglycerols, ketone bodies, protein catabolism.

AAMC FC mapping: FC1D (Metabolism)

Kaplan: Biochem Ch 11

---

LIPID METABOLISM:
- Lipid digestion/absorption: bile salts emulsify; pancreatic lipase → monoglycerides + free FAs; micelles → enterocytes; chylomicrons exit via lymph
- Beta-oxidation: activation (acyl-CoA, outer membrane, -2 ATP equiv), carnitine shuttle into matrix, each cycle: 1 FADH2 + 1 NADH + 1 acetyl-CoA
- CPT-I rate-limiting; inhibited by malonyl-CoA
- Odd-chain FAs → propionyl-CoA → succinyl-CoA (vitamin B12)
- FA synthesis: cytoplasm, acetyl-CoA carboxylase (rate-limiting, +insulin, -glucagon), citrate shuttle, requires NADPH
- Ketogenesis: liver, produces acetoacetate, beta-hydroxybutyrate, acetone; used by brain/heart during starvation
- Liver makes but cannot use ketone bodies (lacks thiophorase)

LIPID TRANSPORT (LIPOPROTEINS):
- Chylomicrons (dietary TAG from gut)
- VLDL (liver-made TAG export)
- LDL ("bad" — delivers cholesterol to tissues)
- HDL ("good" — reverse cholesterol transport back to liver)

CHOLESTEROL METABOLISM:
- HMG-CoA reductase = rate-limiting; target of statins
- Cholesterol → bile salts (liver), steroid hormones (adrenal, gonads), vitamin D (skin + liver + kidney)

AMINO ACID METABOLISM:
- Transamination: aminotransferases (require PLP/B6); ALT liver-specific, AST liver + heart + muscle
- Oxidative deamination: glutamate dehydrogenase -> NH4+
- Urea cycle: liver, 2 ATP per urea (3 ATP if counting 4 high-energy bonds), detoxifies NH3; CPS-I rate-limiting
- Glucogenic AAs -> pyruvate or TCA intermediates -> glucose
- Ketogenic AAs -> acetyl-CoA or acetoacetyl-CoA -> ketone bodies
- Both: Ile, Leu, Phe, Trp, Tyr, Thr (only Leu and Lys are purely ketogenic)
- Alanine cycle (muscle alanine → liver pyruvate + glutamate → glucose + urea)
