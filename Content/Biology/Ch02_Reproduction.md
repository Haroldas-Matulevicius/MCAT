# BB Bio Chapter 2 — Reproduction (Cell Cycle, Meiosis, Reproductive System)

Scope: Cell cycle & mitosis; checkpoints, cyclins/CDKs, p53; apoptosis; cancer biology; stem cells & differentiation; meiosis (incl. nondisjunction); male and female reproductive systems and gametogenesis.

AAMC FC mapping: FC2C (Cell division & differentiation), FC3B (reproductive system portion)

Kaplan: Biology Ch 2

THE CELL CYCLE:
- G1 (growth, organelle production, variable length), S (DNA replication; 2C -> 4C; centrosomes duplicate), G2 (preparation, error-checking), M (mitosis + cytokinesis)
- G0: quiescence (neurons, cardiomyocytes permanent; hepatocytes can re-enter)
- Ploidy vs DNA content: 2n stays through S/G2

CHECKPOINTS:
- G1/S (restriction point): growth factors -> Cyclin D-CDK4/6 -> phosphorylates Rb -> releases E2F -> Cyclin E-CDK2 -> commit to S
- G2/M: DNA replication complete? p53 -> p21 -> CDK inhibition; Cyclin B-CDK1 = MPF (master switch)
- Spindle assembly checkpoint: Mad2 inhibits APC/C until proper attachment

CYCLINS/CDKs:
- D/CDK4-6 (G1 progression); E/CDK2 (G1->S); A/CDK2 (S); B/CDK1 (G2->M, MPF)
- CKI brakes: p21, p27, p16

p53 — GUARDIAN OF GENOME:
- Activated by DNA damage (ATM/ATR phosphorylates, prevents MDM2 ubiquitination)
- Outputs: cell cycle arrest (p21), DNA repair, apoptosis (Bax, PUMA)
- Mutated in ~50% of cancers; Li-Fraumeni

MITOSIS (PMAT):
- Prophase: chromatin condenses, spindle forms, nucleolus disappears
- Prometaphase: nuclear envelope breaks (lamin phosphorylation), kinetochores attach
- Metaphase: alignment at metaphase plate (best for karyotype)
- Anaphase: APC/C ubiquitinates securin -> separase cleaves cohesin -> sister chromatids to poles (A); polar MTs slide, cell elongates (B)
- Telophase: nuclear envelopes reform, chromosomes decondense
- Cytokinesis: animal = actin/myosin contractile ring -> cleavage furrow; plant = cell plate from Golgi vesicles

APOPTOSIS vs NECROSIS:
- Apoptosis: orderly, ATP-dependent, cell shrinks, no inflammation, DNA ladder
- Necrosis: uncontrolled, cell swells, ruptures, inflammation, DNA smear
- Caspases (cysteine-aspartic proteases): initiator (8, 9) -> executioner (3, 6, 7)
- Intrinsic (mitochondrial): stress -> Bax/Bak pores -> cytochrome c release -> Apaf-1 + cyt c = apoptosome -> caspase-9 -> caspase-3; Bcl-2/Bcl-xL inhibit
- Extrinsic (death receptor): FasL/Fas, TNF, TRAIL -> FADD -> DISC -> caspase-8 -> caspase-3; Bid->tBid amplifies via intrinsic

CANCER:
- Oncogenes: gain-of-function, dominant, one allele (Ras stuck-on, Myc overexpression, HER2 amplification, Bcl-2 anti-apoptotic, Src)
- Tumor suppressors: loss-of-function, recessive, two-hit (Knudson) -- p53, Rb, BRCA1/2, APC, p16
- Hallmarks: sustained proliferation, evade growth suppressors, resist death, replicative immortality (telomerase), angiogenesis (VEGF), invasion/metastasis (EMT, MMPs, E-cadherin loss)

STEM CELLS:
- Potency: totipotent (zygote, +placenta) > pluripotent (ICM, 3 germ layers) > multipotent (HSCs) > oligopotent > unipotent
- ESCs from inner cell mass; adult stem cells in niches
- iPSCs: Yamanaka factors (Oct4, Sox2, Klf4, c-Myc) -- differentiation is reversible (epigenetic)
- Asymmetric division, determination, induction, epigenetic regulation (DNA methylation, histone acetylation)

MEIOSIS:
- Produces 4 haploid (n) gametes from 1 diploid (2n) cell; two divisions
- Meiosis I (reduction): prophase I (synapsis -> tetrads, crossing over at chiasmata), metaphase I (independent assortment), anaphase I (homologs separate), telophase I
- Meiosis II (mitosis-like): sister chromatids separate
- Sources of variation: crossing over (PI), independent assortment (MI), random fertilization
- Mitosis vs meiosis: 1 vs 2 divisions; 2 vs 4 daughter cells; identical vs unique; 2n vs n
- Nondisjunction: MI failure = all 4 gametes abnormal; MII failure = 2 normal + 2 abnormal -> aneuploidy (trisomy 21, 13, 18; Turner 45,X)

MALE REPRODUCTIVE SYSTEM:
- Spermatogenesis: spermatogonia (2n) -> primary spermatocytes (2n) -[MI]-> secondary spermatocytes (n) -[MII]-> spermatids (n) -> spermatozoa; 1->4 sperm; ~64-72 days
- Sertoli cells: nurse cells, blood-testis barrier, inhibin (FSH neg feedback), ABP, respond to FSH
- Leydig cells: testosterone in response to LH
- Pathway: seminiferous tubules -> epididymis -> vas deferens -> ejaculatory duct -> urethra
- Accessory glands: seminal vesicles (fructose, ~60% volume), prostate (alkaline), bulbourethral

FEMALE REPRODUCTIVE SYSTEM:
- Oogenesis: oogonia (fetal) -> primary oocytes (arrest prophase I until puberty) -> secondary oocyte + 1st polar body (arrest metaphase II) -> ovum (only if fertilized) + 2nd polar body; 1->1 ovum
- Menstrual cycle (~28d):
  - Ovarian: follicular (FSH, estrogen, neg feedback) -> ovulation (estrogen positive feedback -> LH surge ~day 14) -> luteal (corpus luteum -> progesterone)
  - Uterine: menstrual (1-5) -> proliferative (6-14, estrogen) -> secretory (15-28, progesterone)
  - hCG from embryo maintains corpus luteum if fertilized
- Birth control: synthetic estrogen+progesterone -> constant neg feedback -> no ovulation
