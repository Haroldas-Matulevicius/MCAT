# BB Bio Chapter 3 — Embryogenesis

Scope: Early development stages (fertilization, cleavage, blastulation, gastrulation, neurulation); mechanisms of development (germ layers, induction, neural crest); fetal circulation; gestation and birth.

AAMC FC mapping: FC3B (development portion)

Kaplan: Biology Ch 3

---

FERTILIZATION TO IMPLANTATION:
- Fertilization: occurs in ampulla of fallopian tube; acrosome reaction (sperm enzymes penetrate zona pellucida); cortical reaction (cortical granules harden zona, prevent polyspermy); meiosis II of oocyte completed
- Cleavage: rapid mitotic divisions without growth; total embryo size stays ~constant
- Morula (~day 3-4): solid ball of ~16 cells
- Blastocyst (~day 5): hollow ball with inner cell mass (embryoblast -> embryo), trophoblast (-> placenta, secretes hCG), blastocoel
- Implantation (~day 6-7): blastocyst implants in endometrium

GASTRULATION:
- Inner cell mass reorganizes into 3 germ layers via invagination through primitive streak

GERM LAYERS:
- Ectoderm: epidermis, hair, nails, nervous system (CNS + PNS), neural crest, lens, inner ear, tooth enamel, anterior pituitary, adrenal medulla
- Mesoderm: muscle (all types), bone, cartilage, connective tissue, cardiovascular system, kidneys, gonads, adrenal cortex, dermis, spleen, notochord
- Endoderm: GI tract lining, respiratory tract lining, bladder, liver, pancreas, thyroid, parathyroid, thymus, tonsils

NEURULATION:
- Notochord (mesoderm) induces overlying ectoderm -> neural plate
- Neural folds rise around neural groove -> fuse -> neural tube (-> brain + spinal cord); rostral closes first, caudal last (~day 28)
- Neural crest cells pinch off from junction: PNS ganglia, Schwann cells, melanocytes, adrenal medulla, facial bone/cartilage, odontoblasts
- Neural tube defects: anencephaly (rostral, lethal), spina bifida (caudal); folate prevents
- Alar plate (dorsal) = sensory; basal plate (ventral) = motor; sulcus limitans separates -- basis for Bell-Magendie law
- TRAP: notochord is mesoderm, but it INDUCES ectodermal neural plate

ORGANOGENESIS:
- Weeks 3-8 (most critical for teratogen exposure)
- Three germ layers differentiate into specific organs

FETAL CIRCULATION:
- Placenta serves as gas exchange organ (lungs not used)
- Umbilical vein: oxygenated blood from placenta TO fetus (counterintuitive)
- Umbilical arteries (2): deoxygenated FROM fetus TO placenta
- Ductus venosus: shunts umbilical blood past liver into IVC
- Foramen ovale: R atrium -> L atrium (bypasses lungs)
- Ductus arteriosus: pulmonary artery -> aorta (bypasses lungs)
- At birth: lung expansion -> pulmonary resistance drops -> L atrial pressure rises -> foramen ovale closes (-> fossa ovalis); rising O2 + falling prostaglandins -> ductus arteriosus closes (-> ligamentum arteriosum)

GESTATION/BIRTH:
- hCG maintains corpus luteum until placenta takes over (weeks 8-12)
- Labor: oxytocin positive feedback (cervical stretch -> more oxytocin -> stronger contractions)
