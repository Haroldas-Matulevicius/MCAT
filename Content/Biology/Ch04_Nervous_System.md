# BB Bio Chapter 4 — Nervous System

Scope: Cells of the nervous system (neurons, glia); transmission of neural impulses (resting potential, action potential, synaptic transmission); organization of human nervous system (CNS/PNS, somatic/autonomic, brain anatomy).

AAMC FC mapping: FC3A (Nervous system)

Kaplan: Biology Ch 4

---

NERVOUS SYSTEM ORGANIZATION:
- CNS: brain + spinal cord; protected by bone, meninges (dura, arachnoid, pia), CSF
- PNS: 12 cranial + 31 spinal nerves, ganglia, receptors
- Spinal cord: gray INSIDE (butterfly/H), white OUTSIDE — opposite of brain
- CSF flow: choroid plexus -> lateral ventricles -> 3rd (foramen of Monro) -> 4th (aqueduct of Sylvius) -> subarachnoid -> arachnoid granulations
- BBB: tight junctions + astrocyte foot processes; lipophilic molecules cross; L-DOPA crosses (dopamine doesn't)

PNS DIVISIONS:
- By direction: afferent (sensory, "Arrive"), efferent (motor, "Exit")
- Somatic: voluntary, skeletal muscle, ACh on nicotinic receptors at NMJ
- Autonomic: 2-neuron chain (preganglionic synapse in ganglion -> postganglionic to target)

SYMPATHETIC vs PARASYMPATHETIC:
- Sympathetic (fight-or-flight): thoracolumbar T1-L2; short pre, long post; ACh pre (nicotinic) -> NE post (adrenergic); HR up, bronchodil, mydriasis, GI down
- Parasympathetic (rest-and-digest): craniosacral; long pre, short post; ACh pre -> ACh post (muscarinic); HR down, bronchoconstrict, miosis, GI up
- Vagus = CN X = ~75% of parasympathetic (heart, lungs, abdominal organs)
- Adrenal medulla exception: sympathetic preganglionic -> chromaffin cells -> Epi (80%) + NE (20%) into blood; neuroendocrine bridge
- Most organs dual innervation; blood vessels mostly sympathetic only

NEURONS:
- Soma (Nissl bodies), dendrites (input), axon hillock (trigger zone, lowest threshold), axon, myelin sheath, nodes of Ranvier, synaptic terminal
- Sensory (often pseudounipolar), motor (multipolar), interneurons (CNS only)
- Reflex arcs: sensory -> interneuron(s) -> motor; patellar = monosynaptic; withdrawal/crossed extensor = polysynaptic
- Bell-Magendie: dorsal root = sensory IN; ventral root = motor OUT

MYELINATION:
- Schwann cells (PNS): one cell wraps one segment; PNS regenerates
- Oligodendrocytes (CNS): one cell myelinates multiple axons; CNS does NOT regenerate
- MS = CNS demyelination; Guillain-Barre = PNS

GLIAL CELLS:
- Astrocytes: form BBB, K+/NT homeostasis, metabolic support
- Microglia: brain macrophages
- Ependymal: line ventricles, CSF
- Oligodendrocytes / Schwann cells: myelinate
- Satellite cells: PNS ganglia support

RESTING POTENTIAL ~ -70 mV:
- Na+/K+ ATPase: 3 Na+ out, 2 K+ in per ATP (electrogenic)
- K+ leak channels (membrane ~50-100x more permeable to K+)
- Large intracellular anions (proteins, organic phosphates)
- Closer to E_K (~-90 mV) than E_Na (~+60 mV)

NERNST EQUATION:
- E = (61.5/z) log([out]/[in]) at body temp
- E_K ~ -90, E_Na ~ +60, E_Cl ~ -70 to -80
- More permeable to ion X -> Vm moves toward E_X

ACTION POTENTIAL:
- Threshold: ~-55 mV; all-or-nothing; intensity coded by frequency
- Depolarization: voltage-gated Na+ open -> Na+ in -> peak ~+30 to +40 mV
- Repolarization: Na+ inactivate, voltage-gated K+ open (delayed rectifier) -> K+ out
- Hyperpolarization (undershoot): K+ slow to close -> ~-80 to -90 mV
- Refractory: absolute (Na+ inactivated, no AP possible) ensures one-way propagation; relative (hyperpolarized, needs stronger stimulus)

SALTATORY CONDUCTION:
- Myelin insulates; AP regenerates only at nodes; faster, less ATP
- Speed depends on myelination, axon diameter, temperature

SYNAPTIC TRANSMISSION:
- AP -> presynaptic depolarization -> voltage-gated Ca2+ open -> Ca2+ in -> SNARE-mediated vesicle fusion -> NT release -> binds postsyn receptor -> EPSP or IPSP
- NT removal: enzymatic (AChE), reuptake (SSRIs block), diffusion
- Botulinum toxin cleaves SNAREs (flaccid paralysis); tetanus blocks inhibitory NT release (spastic)
- Summation: temporal (rapid same neuron) vs spatial (multiple neurons); axon hillock integrates

NEUROTRANSMITTERS:
- ACh: NMJ (nicotinic, fast), parasympathetic (muscarinic), basal forebrain (memory). AChE breaks down. Alzheimer's (low), myasthenia gravis (autoimmune NMJ), nerve gas/organophosphates (AChE inhibitors)
- NE/Epi: sympathetic post, locus coeruleus (arousal); alpha/beta receptors; synthesis: Tyr -> L-DOPA -> DA -> NE -> Epi
- Dopamine: mesolimbic (reward; high in schizophrenia +sx), nigrostriatal (motor; low in Parkinson's), mesocortical (executive; low in schiz -sx), tuberoinfundibular (inhibits prolactin); cocaine blocks reuptake
- Serotonin: raphe nuclei; mood/sleep/appetite; SSRIs; from tryptophan -> melatonin
- GABA: main inhibitory; opens Cl- channels -> hyperpol; benzos, barbs, alcohol potentiate; Huntington's (loss in caudate)
- Glutamate: main excitatory; NMDA (Mg2+ block, Ca2+, learning/LTP), AMPA, kainate; excitotoxicity in stroke
- Endorphins/enkephalins: endogenous opioids; mu/delta/kappa receptors

BRAIN ANATOMY:
- Frontal lobe: prefrontal (executive), primary motor (precentral gyrus, contralateral), Broca's area (LEFT inferior frontal -- speech production; "Broken speech")
- Parietal: primary somatosensory (postcentral); R parietal lesion -> hemispatial neglect
- Temporal: primary auditory; Wernicke's area (LEFT superior temporal -- comprehension; "Wordy but wrong"); hippocampus, amygdala (medial)
- Occipital: primary visual cortex
- Lateralization: L = language, logic; R = spatial, faces, prosody; corpus callosum
- Limbic: amygdala (fear, emotional memory), hippocampus (declarative memory consolidation; H.M. anterograde amnesia), hypothalamus (homeostasis, drives, ANS, links to endocrine)
- Thalamus: sensory relay (ALL except smell)
- Cerebellum: motor coordination, balance, procedural memory; ataxia if damaged
- Brainstem: midbrain (colliculi, RAS, substantia nigra), pons (cerebrum-cerebellum relay, breathing modulation), medulla (vital functions: HR, BP, breathing rate)
- Basal ganglia: caudate, putamen, globus pallidus; motor initiation; Parkinson's (loss of nigrostriatal DA), Huntington's (loss of caudate GABA, AD CAG repeat chr 4)
