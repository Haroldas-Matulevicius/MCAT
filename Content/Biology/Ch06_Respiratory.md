# BB Bio Chapter 6 — Respiratory System

Scope: Anatomy and mechanism of breathing (conducting/respiratory zones, ventilation mechanics, lung volumes); functions of the respiratory system (gas exchange, O2-Hb dissociation, CO2 transport, acid-base contribution).

AAMC FC mapping: FC3B (respiratory portion)

Kaplan: Biology Ch 6

---

ANATOMY:

Conducting zone (no gas exchange):
- Nasal cavity (warm, humidify, filter via turbinates) -> pharynx (shared with food) -> larynx (vocal cords, epiglottis) -> trachea (C-shaped cartilage open posteriorly; mucociliary escalator) -> bronchi (R is shorter/wider/more vertical -- aspirations lodge here) -> bronchioles (smooth muscle, no cartilage; parasymp constricts, symp dilates) -> terminal bronchioles

Respiratory zone (gas exchange):
- Respiratory bronchioles -> alveolar ducts/sacs -> alveoli (~300 million, ~70 m^2)
- Type I pneumocytes: thin squamous, gas exchange
- Type II pneumocytes: cuboidal, secrete surfactant (DPPC); reduces surface tension (LaPlace P=2T/r); prevents alveolar collapse; matures ~35 weeks gestation (NRDS in preemies)

VENTILATION MECHANICS (Boyle's law: P1V1=P2V2):
- Inspiration (active): diaphragm contracts (flattens), external intercostals lift rib cage -> thoracic volume up -> alveolar P drops -> air in
- Expiration (passive at rest): elastic recoil; forced expiration uses internal intercostals + abdominals
- Pressures: intra-alveolar fluctuates; intrapleural always negative at rest (keeps lungs inflated; pneumothorax = collapse); transpulmonary = alveolar - pleural

LUNG VOLUMES & CAPACITIES:
- TV ~500 mL; IRV ~3000; ERV ~1200; RV ~1200 (cannot measure by spirometry)
- VC = TV+IRV+ERV ~4700; TLC = VC+RV ~5900; FRC = ERV+RV ~2400; IC = TV+IRV ~3500
- Obstructive (asthma, COPD): RV/FRC up (air trapping)
- Restrictive (fibrosis): all volumes down

GAS EXCHANGE (Dalton's law; Fick's law):
- External (alveoli): PO2 100 vs 40 mmHg blood -> O2 in; PCO2 46 vs 40 -> CO2 out
- Internal (tissues): PO2 100 vs 40 tissue -> O2 out; PCO2 46 vs 40 -> CO2 in
- CO2 ~20x more soluble than O2

O2-HEMOGLOBIN DISSOCIATION CURVE (very high yield):
- Hb tetramer (2 alpha + 2 beta in HbA), 4 heme Fe2+, 4 O2 max
- Sigmoidal = cooperative binding (T-state low affinity -> R-state high affinity)
- P50 ~ 26 mmHg
- RIGHT SHIFT (decreased affinity, MORE unloading) — exercising muscle:
  - Increased temp, CO2, H+ (decreased pH = Bohr effect), 2,3-BPG (high altitude)
- LEFT SHIFT (increased affinity, LESS unloading):
  - Fetal Hb (gamma binds 2,3-BPG less) — extracts O2 from maternal blood
  - CO poisoning (200x Hb affinity, locks R-state)
  - Decreased temp, CO2, H+, 2,3-BPG

CO2 TRANSPORT:
- Dissolved (~7%, = PCO2)
- Carbaminohemoglobin (~23%, on Hb amino groups not heme; Haldane effect: deoxy-Hb loads CO2 more)
- Bicarbonate (~70%): CO2 + H2O <-> H2CO3 <-> H+ + HCO3- catalyzed by carbonic anhydrase in RBCs
- Chloride shift: HCO3- exits RBC via band 3 antiporter, Cl- in (reverses at lung)
- Bohr + Haldane synergize: tissue (CO2 loading promotes O2 unloading; O2 unloading promotes CO2 loading); lung (opposite)

ACID-BASE: lungs adjust CO2 (acute compensation); kidneys adjust HCO3- (chronic)
