SARCOMERE STRUCTURE (Z-line to Z-line):
- Z-line: anchor for thin filaments; Z-lines move closer in contraction
- I-band: thin filaments only (light); SHORTENS
- A-band: thick filaments full length (dark); STAYS SAME
- H-zone: thick only (center A-band); SHORTENS
- M-line: center, anchors thick filaments
- "HI shrinks; A stays"
- Thin filaments: actin + tropomyosin + troponin (TnC, TnI, TnT)
- Thick filaments: myosin (head = ATPase)
- Titin: giant elastic Z to M; passive elasticity, prevents over-stretch

SLIDING FILAMENT THEORY (cross-bridge cycle):
1. ATP binds myosin -> detaches from actin (rigor mortis: no ATP, locked)
2. ATP hydrolyzed -> myosin head cocked
3. Myosin binds actin (when tropomyosin moved off binding sites)
4. Power stroke: Pi released, then ADP -> head pivots, pulls thin filament toward M
5. New ATP -> cycle repeats

TROPOMYOSIN-TROPONIN REGULATION:
- At rest: tropomyosin covers myosin-binding sites
- Ca2+ binds TnC -> conformational change -> tropomyosin moves -> binding sites exposed

EXCITATION-CONTRACTION COUPLING:
1. Motor neuron releases ACh at NMJ
2. ACh -> nicotinic receptors -> Na+ in -> sarcolemma depolarization
3. AP propagates along sarcolemma down T-tubules
4. T-tubule DHP receptors mechanically coupled to ryanodine receptors on SR
5. Ryanodine receptors open -> Ca2+ flood from SR
6. Ca2+ binds TnC -> contraction
7. Relaxation: SERCA pumps Ca2+ back into SR

FIBER TYPES:
- Type I (slow oxidative): slow, fatigue-resistant, aerobic, high myoglobin (red), many mitochondria; postural, marathon
- Type IIa (fast oxidative): fast, moderate fatigue, mixed
- Type IIb/IIx (fast glycolytic): fast, low fatigue resistance, anaerobic, low myoglobin (white); sprint, power

MUSCLE TYPE COMPARISON:
- Skeletal: striated, multinucleate (syncytium), voluntary, troponin-tropomyosin, T-tubules, no gap junctions, SR Ca2+
- Cardiac: striated, 1-2 central nuclei, involuntary, troponin-tropomyosin, intercalated discs (gap junctions = electrical syncytium), autorhythmic, SR + extracellular Ca2+ (L-type)
- Smooth: non-striated, 1 central nucleus, involuntary, calmodulin-MLCK (NOT troponin) -- Ca2+-calmodulin activates MLCK -> phosphorylates myosin; gap junctions in single-unit; no T-tubules, no sarcomeres; extracellular Ca2+ primary + IP3-mediated SR release

BONE:

Types:
- Compact (cortical): dense outer layer; osteons (Haversian systems) — concentric lamellae around central canal
- Spongy (cancellous/trabecular): inner mesh; red bone marrow (hematopoiesis)

Cells:
- Osteoblasts: build (osteoid -> mineralizes)
- Osteoclasts: break down (multinucleate, from monocytes, use acid + enzymes)
- Osteocytes: mature (trapped osteoblasts), mechanosensors, maintain matrix, communicate via canaliculi

Bone remodeling:
- PTH: stim osteoclasts (indirectly via RANKL on osteoblasts) -> raise blood Ca2+
- Calcitonin: inhibits osteoclasts -> lower blood Ca2+
- Vitamin D: increases gut Ca2+ absorption + works with PTH

OSSIFICATION:
- Intramembranous: directly from mesenchyme; flat bones (skull, clavicle)
- Endochondral: replaces hyaline cartilage template; long bones (femur, humerus); growth at epiphyseal plate until ossification
