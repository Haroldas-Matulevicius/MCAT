# CP Gen Chem Chapter 3 — Bonding & IMFs

Scope: Ionic vs covalent; Lewis structures; formal charge; resonance; octet exceptions; VSEPR; bond polarity & dipole moments; IMFs.

AAMC FC mapping: FC5B

Kaplan: General Chemistry Ch 3

Note: Hybridization, σ/π, MOs → `CP_OC_Ch03_Bonding.md` (orgo side).

---

BOND TYPES:
- Ionic: e- transfer, ΔEN > ~1.7, lattice, brittle, conducts molten/aqueous
- Covalent: e- sharing; nonpolar (ΔEN <0.4), polar (0.5-1.7)
- Coordinate (dative): both shared e- from one atom (e.g., NH3→BF3)
- Metallic: delocalized "sea of electrons", explains conductivity/malleability

LEWIS STRUCTURES (procedure):
1. Count total valence e-
2. Pick central atom (least EN, never H)
3. Single bonds from center
4. Distribute lone pairs to outer atoms (octets/duet for H)
5. Check central octet; convert lone pairs to multiple bonds if needed
6. Verify with formal charge

FORMAL CHARGE:
- FC = valence e- - lone pair e- - 1/2(bonding e-)
- Best structure minimizes |FC|; negative FC goes on more EN atom

RESONANCE:
- Multiple Lewis structures, electrons differ in position only
- Real molecule = hybrid of contributors
- Most-contributing: fewest FCs, neg FC on EN atom, complete octets
- Stabilizes carboxylate, benzene, allylic/benzylic carbocations
- Explains acidity differences (conjugate base stabilization)

OCTET EXCEPTIONS:
- Incomplete: B (BF3), Be (BeH2)
- Expanded: P, S, Cl and below (PCl5, SF6, SO4^2-)
- Odd-electron radicals: NO, NO2

VSEPR:
- Electron groups = bond pairs + lone pairs
- Electron geometry vs molecular geometry (lone pairs invisible to MG name)
- Repulsion: LP-LP > LP-BP > BP-BP
- 2 groups: linear (180°)
- 3 groups: trig planar (120°); 1 LP → bent (~117°)
- 4 groups: tetrahedral (109.5°); 1 LP → trig pyramidal (NH3, 107°); 2 LP → bent (H2O, 104.5°)
- 5 groups: trig bipyramidal (90/120°); 1 LP → seesaw; 2 LP → T-shape; 3 LP → linear (XeF2). LPs go equatorial.
- 6 groups: octahedral (90°); 1 LP → sq pyramidal; 2 LP → sq planar (XeF4)

BOND POLARITY & DIPOLES:
- Polar bond: ΔEN ≠ 0, partial charges
- Molecular dipole = vector sum of bond dipoles
- Symmetric molecules with identical bonds: net dipole = 0 (CO2, BF3, CCl4, SF6, XeF4)
- Polar bonds + asymmetric geometry → polar molecule (H2O, NH3, CHCl3)

INTERMOLECULAR FORCES (weakest → strongest):
- LDF (London dispersion): ALL molecules; instantaneous dipoles; depends on polarizability, MW, surface area
- Dipole-dipole: polar molecules
- Hydrogen bonding: H bonded to N, O, or F (donor) + lone pair on N/O/F (acceptor); special strong dipole-dipole
- Ion-dipole: strongest; ions in polar solvents (Na+ in H2O)

PROPERTIES & IMFS:
- Stronger IMFs → higher BP, MP, viscosity, surface tension; lower vapor pressure
- "Like dissolves like" (polar in polar, nonpolar in nonpolar)
- BP comparison: butane (LDF) < propanal (dipole) < propanol (H-bond)
