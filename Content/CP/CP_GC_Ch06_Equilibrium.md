# CP Gen Chem Chapter 6 — Equilibrium

Scope: Keq; Q; Le Chatelier; kinetic vs thermodynamic control; ΔG°-Keq relationship.

AAMC FC mapping: FC5E (equilibrium)

Kaplan: General Chemistry Ch 6

---

EQUILIBRIUM CONSTANT (Keq):
- aA + bB ↔ cC + dD: Keq = [C]^c[D]^d / [A]^a[B]^b
- EXCLUDE pure solids and liquids
- Unitless by convention; depends ONLY on T
- Keq >> 1: products favored; << 1: reactants favored
- Kp vs Kc (gases): Kp = Kc(RT)^Δn

REACTION QUOTIENT (Q):
- Same formula as Keq, but with CURRENT (non-eq) concentrations
- Q < K → forward shift (more products)
- Q > K → reverse shift (more reactants)
- Q = K → at equilibrium

LE CHATELIER'S PRINCIPLE:
- Add reactant → shift RIGHT
- Add product → shift LEFT
- Increase P (decrease V): shift to fewer mol gas
- Decrease P (increase V): shift to more mol gas
- Equal mol gas on both sides → no shift
- Temperature: treat heat as reactant (endo) or product (exo)
  - Exo + heat T: shift LEFT, ↓Keq
  - Endo + heat T: shift RIGHT, ↑Keq
  - T is the ONLY factor that changes Keq value
- Catalyst: NO shift (equilibrium reached faster, position unchanged)
- Inert gas at const V: no effect on partial pressures, no shift

ΔG° AND Keq:
- ΔG° = -RT·ln(Keq) [master bridge]
- Keq > 1 ↔ ΔG° < 0
- Keq < 1 ↔ ΔG° > 0
- ΔG = ΔG° + RT·ln(Q): tells direction at non-standard conditions
- At equilibrium: Q = Keq, ΔG = 0

KINETIC vs THERMODYNAMIC CONTROL:
- Kinetic (low T, short time): product via lower Ea path; less stable but faster
- Thermodynamic (high T, long time, reversible): most stable product wins
- Classic: 1,2- vs 1,4-addition to conjugated diene
