IDEAL GAS LAW:
- PV = nRT
- R = 0.0821 L·atm/(mol·K) or 8.314 J/(mol·K)
- T MUST be in Kelvin (K = °C + 273)

INDIVIDUAL GAS LAWS:
- Boyle (n,T const): P1V1 = P2V2 (P↔V inverse)
- Charles (n,P const): V1/T1 = V2/T2 (V↔T direct)
- Gay-Lussac (n,V const): P1/T1 = P2/T2 (P↔T direct)
- Avogadro (P,T const): V1/n1 = V2/n2 (V↔n direct)
- Combined: P1V1/T1 = P2V2/T2

STP:
- 0°C = 273 K, 1 atm
- 1 mol ideal gas = 22.4 L

DALTON'S LAW (partial pressures):
- P_total = P_A + P_B + P_C + ...
- P_A = X_A · P_total (X = mole fraction)
- Drives alveolar gas exchange:
  - Atm: N2 78% (593 mmHg), O2 21% (160), CO2 0.04% (0.3)
  - Alveoli: P_O2 ~100 (vs blood ~40), P_CO2 ~40 (vs blood ~46)
  - Diffusion down partial pressure gradient, no active transport

KMT (ideal gas assumptions):
1. Negligible particle volume
2. No intermolecular forces
3. Elastic collisions
4. Random straight-line motion
5. KE_avg ∝ T

Quantitative:
- KE_avg = (3/2)k_B·T (same for all gases at same T)
- v_rms = √(3RT/M) (lighter = faster)
- Graham's law: rate1/rate2 = √(M2/M1)
  - H2 vs O2: √(32/2) = 4× faster

REAL GASES (van der Waals):
- (P + an²/V²)(V - nb) = nRT
- a corrects intermolecular attractions
- b corrects molecular volume
- Deviate MOST: high P, low T
- Most ideal: low P, high T
- He behaves most ideally (small, nonpolar)
- NH3, H2O vapor deviate more (polar, H-bonding)

HENRY'S LAW:
- [gas]_dissolved = k_H · P_gas
- Explains CO2 in soda, decompression sickness ("bends"), O2 delivery to tissues
