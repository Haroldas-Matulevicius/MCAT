# CP Gen Chem Chapter 9 — Solutions

Scope: Solutions; concentration units; dilution; solubility & Ksp; common-ion effect; selective precipitation; complex ions; colligative properties.

AAMC FC mapping: FC5A (solutions portion)

Kaplan: General Chemistry Ch 9

---

NATURE OF SOLUTIONS:
- Homogeneous mixture: solute + solvent
- "Like dissolves like" (polar↔polar, nonpolar↔nonpolar) — IMF compatibility
- Saturation: unsat (Q<Ksp) → sat (Q=Ksp) → supersat (metastable, Q>Ksp briefly)

CONCENTRATION UNITS:
- Molarity M: mol solute/L solution (default)
- Molality m: mol solute/kg solvent (T-independent, used for colligative)
- Mole fraction X: mol component / total mol
- Mass percent (w/w): mass solute / mass solution × 100
- ppm/ppb: trace concentrations
- Normality N: equivalents/L = M × n (polyprotic, multi-e- redox)

DILUTION:
- M1V1 = M2V2 (moles conserved)
- ALWAYS add acid to water (exothermic, prevent boil/spatter)

SOLUBILITY & KSP:
- AgCl ↔ Ag+ + Cl-: Ksp = [Ag+][Cl-] (exclude solid)
- Ca3(PO4)2 ↔ 3Ca²⁺ + 2PO4³⁻: Ksp = [Ca²⁺]³[PO4³⁻]²
- Solubility from Ksp: AgCl, s = √Ksp; PbI2, s = (Ksp/4)^(1/3)
- Q_sp < Ksp: dissolves; = Ksp: saturated; > Ksp: precipitates
- Compare Ksp directly only if same stoichiometry

COMMON-ION EFFECT:
- Adding common ion decreases solubility (Le Chatelier)
- AgCl in 0.1 M NaCl: s = Ksp/0.1, ~7000× less soluble

SELECTIVE PRECIPITATION:
- Smallest Ksp precipitates first
- AgI (Ksp=8.5e-17) before AgCl (Ksp=1.8e-10)

COMPLEX ION FORMATION:
- Ligands (NH3, CN-, OH-) bind metal ions, increase solubility
- AgCl + 2NH3 → [Ag(NH3)2]+ + Cl-

COLLIGATIVE PROPERTIES (depend on # particles, not identity):
- van't Hoff factor i: particles per formula unit
  - Glucose: 1; NaCl: 2; CaCl2: 3; Al2(SO4)3: 5
  - Weak electrolytes: ~1 (barely dissociate)
- BP elevation: ΔT_b = i·K_b·m (water K_b = 0.512 °C/m)
- FP depression: ΔT_f = i·K_f·m (water K_f = 1.86 °C/m); 0.5 m NaCl → -1.86°C
- Osmotic pressure: π = iMRT
  - 0.15 M NaCl @37°C: π ≈ 7.6 atm
  - Iso/hyper/hypotonic for cells
- Vapor pressure (Raoult): P_soln = X_solvent · P°_solvent
- Volatile mixtures: P_total = X_A·P°_A + X_B·P°_B
- Positive deviation (weak A-B IMFs): min-BP azeotrope (ethanol/water 95.6%)
- Negative deviation (strong A-B IMFs): max-BP azeotrope (acetone/chloroform)
- Azeotropes can't be separated by simple/fractional distillation
