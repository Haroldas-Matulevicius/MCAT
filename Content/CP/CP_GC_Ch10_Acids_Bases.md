# CP Gen Chem Chapter 10 — Acids & Bases

Scope: Bronsted-Lowry & Lewis; strong vs weak; pH calc; H-H; buffers; titrations (strong/strong, weak/strong, polyprotic); indicators.

AAMC FC mapping: FC5A (acid-base)

Kaplan: General Chemistry Ch 10

---

DEFINITIONS:
- Bronsted-Lowry (default): acid = H+ donor; base = H+ acceptor
- Lewis (broader): acid = e- pair acceptor; base = e- pair donor
- Use Lewis for: metal coord chem, BF3/AlCl3, nucleophile/electrophile in orgo
- Conjugate pairs differ by 1 H+; stronger acid → weaker conjugate base

STRONG ACIDS (memorize 6):
- HCl, HBr, HI, H2SO4, HNO3, HClO4 (100% dissociation)
- H2SO4: 1st H strong, 2nd H weak (Ka2 ~ 0.012); on MCAT treat as 2 H+ per molecule

STRONG BASES:
- Group 1 hydroxides: NaOH, KOH, LiOH
- Group 2 (with limits): Ba(OH)2, Ca(OH)2

WEAK ACIDS/BASES:
- HA ↔ H+ + A-: Ka = [H+][A-]/[HA]
- B + H2O ↔ BH+ + OH-: Kb = [BH+][OH-]/[B]
- Ka × Kb = Kw = 1e-14; pKa + pKb = 14
- Larger Ka = smaller pKa = stronger acid

pH CALCULATIONS:
- pH = -log[H+]; pOH = -log[OH-]; pH + pOH = 14
- Log approximations: log2=0.3, log3=0.5, log4=0.6, log5=0.7, log6=0.8, log8=0.9
- pH = -log(A × 10^-n) = n - log(A)
- 10^-0.3 ≈ 0.5
- Strong acid: [H+] = concentration directly
- Weak acid (5% rule, C/Ka > 100): [H+] = √(Ka·C)
- Polyprotic: only Ka1 matters for pH of original solution
- Amphoteric intermediate: pH = (pKa1 + pKa2)/2 (also AA pI formula)

HENDERSON-HASSELBALCH:
- pH = pKa + log([A-]/[HA])
- [A-] = [HA] → pH = pKa (half-equivalence)
- [A-]/[HA] = 10 → pH = pKa + 1
- [A-]/[HA] = 0.1 → pH = pKa - 1

BUFFERS:
- Weak acid + conjugate base resists pH change
- Effective range: pKa ± 1
- Pick buffer with pKa near target pH (pH 7.4 needs pKa near 7.4)
- Bicarbonate (H2CO3/HCO3-) buffers blood at 7.4 despite pKa1 = 6.1 (open system, CO2 exhaled)
- Adding strong acid/base: do stoichiometry FIRST, then H-H

TITRATIONS:

Strong acid + strong base:
- Equivalence pH = 7
- Sharp vertical jump at equivalence

Weak acid + strong base (highest yield):
- Start: use √(Ka·C)
- Buffer region: gradual rise; use H-H
- Half-equivalence: pH = pKa (find pKa from this)
- Equivalence pH > 7 (A- hydrolyzes); for acetic acid ~8.7-8.9
- After: excess base

Weak base + strong acid (mirror):
- Equivalence pH < 7
- Half-equiv: pOH = pKb, pH = 14 - pKb

Polyprotic curves:
- Diprotic: 2 equivalence points + 2 half-equiv
- Triprotic: 3 equivalence points
- Amino acids: pI = (pKa1+pKa2)/2 (or flank zero-charge form)

Reading curve elements:
- Starting pH, buffer region (flat), half-eq (middle of buffer), eq (center of steep rise)
- Eq pH = 7 (strong/strong); >7 (weak acid); <7 (weak base)
- Ma·Va = Mb·Vb at equivalence (monoprotic)

INDICATORS:
- Weak acid HIn with different colored forms
- Color change at pH ≈ pKa
- Strong/strong (pH 7): phenol red, bromothymol blue
- WA/SB (pH 8-9): phenolphthalein

POLYVALENCE/NORMALITY (see Ch04 for fundamentals):
- N_a·V_a = N_b·V_b (cleaner for polyprotic)
- 1 M H2SO4 = 2 N H2SO4 (n=2)
