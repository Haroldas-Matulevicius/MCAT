# CP Orgo Chapter 8 — Carboxylic Acids

Scope: Properties; acidity (pKa, resonance, inductive); reactions (form derivatives, decarboxylation, reduction); dicarboxylic acids; lactones.

AAMC FC mapping: FC5D (carb acid chem)

Kaplan: Organic Chemistry Ch 8

---

PROPERTIES:
- R-COOH: carbonyl + hydroxyl on same C
- High BP (dimer H-bonding)
- Small chain water-soluble
- IR: VERY broad O-H 2500-3300 (swallows C-H) + sharp C=O 1700-1725

ACIDITY:
- pKa ~4-5 (acetic 4.76, formic 3.75, benzoic 4.20)
- Why acidic: carboxylate (RCOO-) resonance-stabilized over 2 equivalent O's
- vs alcohol (~16): ~12 pKa units more acidic = pure resonance argument

INDUCTIVE EFFECTS (EWG → more acidic):
- Acetic 4.76 → chloroacetic 2.86 → dichloroacetic 1.29 → trichloroacetic 0.65 → TFA 0.23
- Distance matters: α-EWG >> β
- EDGs (alkyl): less acidic

NaHCO3 TEST (high-yield):
- Carb acid (pKa 4) dissolves in 5% NaHCO3 (pKa H2CO3 = 6.4): bubbling CO2
- Phenol (pKa 10): doesn't dissolve in bicarb (need NaOH)
- Used in acid-base extraction to differentiate

REACTIONS:

CONVERSION TO DERIVATIVES (see Ch09 for derivative reactions):
- Acyl chloride: R-COOH + SOCl2 → R-COCl + SO2 + HCl (standard activation)
- Ester: Fischer (R-COOH + R'-OH + H+ + heat, remove water) — reversible; or via acyl chloride
- Amide: harsh conditions; usually via acyl chloride or coupling reagent (DCC, EDC)
- Anhydride: 2 R-COOH → R-CO-O-CO-R + H2O

DECARBOXYLATION (β-keto acids + 1,3-diacids on heating):
- R-CO-CH2-COOH → R-CO-CH3 + CO2
- Cyclic 6-membered transition state; via enol intermediate
- BIOLOGY:
  - Acetoacetic acid (ketone body) — heat-sensitive
  - Malonyl-CoA → fatty acid synthesis (drives C-C bond formation)
  - TCA: isocitrate DH, α-KG DH steps
- Simple R-COOH (no β-carbonyl) is stable

REDUCTION:
- LAH: R-COOH → R-CH2OH (primary alcohol; can't stop at aldehyde)
- NaBH4: does NOT reduce carb acid
- DIBAL-H at low T: ester → aldehyde (lower-yield)

α-HALOGENATION (Hell-Volhard-Zelinsky):
- R-CH2-COOH + Br2/PBr3 → R-CHBr-COOH
- α-H pKa ~25 (less acidic than ketone α-H)

DICARBOXYLIC ACIDS:
- Oxalic (HOOC-COOH): pKa1 = 1.27
- Malonic (HOOC-CH2-COOH): pKa1 = 2.83; decarboxylates on heating
- Succinic (4C): TCA intermediate
- Glutaric (5C); Adipic (6C, nylon)
- pKa1 << pKa2 (after one ionization, harder to lose 2nd)

LACTONES (cyclic esters):
- β-, γ-, δ-hydroxy acids cyclize on warming
- 5-membered (γ-butyrolactone) and 6-membered (δ-valerolactone): stable
- β-lactone (4-mem): strained
- β-lactam = 4-mem cyclic amide → penicillin, cephalosporin (mechanism = acylate transpeptidase)
