# CP Orgo Chapter 12 — Separations & Purifications

Scope: Extraction (incl acid-base); distillation; chromatography (TLC, column, HPLC, GC, size-exclusion, ion-exchange, affinity); electrophoresis (agarose, native PAGE, SDS-PAGE, IEF, 2D); centrifugation (differential, density gradient); recrystallization; filtration; dialysis.

AAMC FC mapping: FC5C (separations)

Kaplan: Organic Chemistry Ch 12

EXTRACTION:

LIQUID-LIQUID:
- Two immiscible solvents; partition coefficient K = [organic]/[aqueous]
- Separatory funnel; drain bottom layer
- Organic usually on TOP; DCM, chloroform DENSER than water (bottom)

ACID-BASE EXTRACTION:
- Charged → water; uncharged → organic
- Carb acid: NaOH → COO- (water); HCl → COOH (organic)
- Amine: HCl → NH3+ (water); NaOH → NH2 (organic)
- Neutral compound: pH-independent, stays in organic
- Order: HCl wash first (pulls out base), then NaOH (pulls out acid), neutral stays

NaHCO3 (5%) DIFFERENTIATION:
- Carb acid (pKa 4) dissolves (CO2 bubbles)
- Phenol (pKa 10) does NOT (need NaOH)

DISTILLATION:

SIMPLE: ΔBP > 25°C; one cycle
FRACTIONAL: ΔBP < 25°C; column with theoretical plates
VACUUM: heat-sensitive or very high BP compounds (low P → low BP)

VAPOR PRESSURE LINK:
- Raoult: P_A = X_A · P°_A
- Higher pure VP (lower BP) dominates vapor → distillate
- Positive deviation (weak A-B IMF): min-BP azeotrope (95.6% ethanol/water; can't separate further)
- Negative: max-BP azeotrope

CHROMATOGRAPHY (general):
- Mobile phase carries; stationary phase retains
- Stronger interaction with stationary → slower → elutes LATER

TLC:
- Stationary: silica/alumina (POLAR); plate
- Solvent rises by capillary action
- Rf = distance(compound)/distance(solvent front), 0-1
- Normal phase: higher Rf = LESS polar
- Reversed phase (C18 nonpolar stationary): everything flips
- More polar mobile phase → higher Rf

COLUMN CHROMATOGRAPHY:
- Preparative; collect fractions
- Normal: silica + nonpolar/moderate mobile; less polar elutes first
- Reversed: C18 + polar mobile; polar elutes first
- Gradient elution: increase polarity over time (normal); decrease (reversed)

HPLC:
- High pressure, small particles, high resolution
- Detector: UV-Vis, fluor, MS
- Quantitative (peak area)

GC:
- Volatile compounds; carrier gas (He, N2)
- Liquid stationary phase coating in capillary column
- Separation by BP + polarity
- Often GC-MS

SIZE-EXCLUSION (GEL FILTRATION):
- Porous beads
- Big molecules can't enter pores → travel around → elute FIRST
- Small molecules enter pores → longer path → elute LAST
- OPPOSITE of gel electrophoresis (where small goes fastest)
- Uses: protein MW, buffer exchange/desalting

ION-EXCHANGE:
- Cation exchanger: NEGATIVE beads (-SO3-) bind cations
- Anion exchanger: POSITIVE beads (-NR3+) bind anions
- Elute: salt gradient (compete) or pH change (alter charge)
- pH > pI: protein neg → anion exchanger
- pH < pI: protein pos → cation exchanger
- pH = pI: no binding either

AFFINITY:
- Specific ligand on beads (lock-and-key)
- Highest specificity = one-step high purity
- Examples: antibody, Ni-NTA (His-tag), substrate analog, lectin
- Elute: competing ligand or change pH/ionic strength

ELECTROPHORESIS:

AGAROSE (DNA/RNA):
- Uniform charge/mass (phosphate backbone) → size only
- Smaller migrates FASTER (toward anode +)
- Loading at cathode (-); ladder for size; EtBr or SYBR Green visualization

NATIVE PAGE (proteins):
- Non-denaturing; native fold
- Separates by charge + size + shape
- Preserves activity

SDS-PAGE (workhorse):
- SDS denatures + uniform negative coat (~1/2 AAs)
- Separates by MW ONLY
- Smaller migrates faster
- Reducing agents (BME, DTT) break disulfides → reveal subunits
- log(MW) vs migration ~linear

IEF (isoelectric focusing):
- Immobilized pH gradient gel
- Protein migrates until pH = pI (zero charge), STOPS
- Sharp band at pI

2D GEL: IEF (horiz) → SDS-PAGE (vert) = pI × MW = thousands of spots (proteomics)

CENTRIFUGATION:

DIFFERENTIAL (sequential spins):
- 1st (~1,000g): cells, nuclei, debris
- 2nd (~10,000g): mitochondria, lysosomes, peroxisomes
- 3rd (~100,000g): ER (microsomes), PM fragments
- 4th (>100,000g): ribosomes, viruses
- Pour off supernatant, spin again

DENSITY GRADIENT ULTRA:
- Rate-zonal (sucrose): particles sediment by S value (size+shape); stop before bottom
- Isopycnic (CsCl): spin to equilibrium where particle density = medium density; separation by buoyant density
- Meselson-Stahl: 14N vs 15N DNA in CsCl

OTHER:

RECRYSTALLIZATION (solids):
1. Dissolve in MINIMUM hot solvent
2. Hot-filter if insoluble impurities
3. Slow cool → pure crystals
4. Impurities stay in mother liquor (low conc)
5. Filter, wash cold

FILTRATION:
- Gravity (filter paper, funnel) — remove insoluble
- Vacuum (Buchner) — collect product solid quickly

DIALYSIS:
- Semipermeable membrane, MWCO
- Small molecules (salt) pass; large (protein, DNA) retained
- Buffer exchange, desalting

KEY DON'T-CONFUSE:
- Size-exclusion: BIG elutes FIRST (excluded from pores)
- Gel electrophoresis: SMALL migrates fastest
- Normal vs reversed phase: opposite polarity preferences
- SDS-PAGE: MW only; native PAGE: charge+size+shape
