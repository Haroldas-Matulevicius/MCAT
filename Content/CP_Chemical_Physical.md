## FOUNDATIONAL CONCEPT 4: Physical processes underlie biological systems

### 4A -- Translational Motion, Forces, Work, Energy, Equilibrium (Physics)

- Kinematics: displacement, velocity (average and instantaneous), acceleration
- Big 5 equations: v=v0+at, x=x0+v0t+1/2at^2, v^2=v0^2+2a*delta_x
- Free fall (g=9.8 m/s^2)
- Projectile motion: horizontal (constant v) and vertical (constant a=g) are independent
- Newton's laws: 1st (inertia), 2nd (F=ma), 3rd (action-reaction)
- Friction: static (fs<=us*N) and kinetic (fk=uk*N)
- Free body diagrams
- Inclined planes: mg sin(theta) parallel, mg cos(theta) perpendicular
- Circular motion and centripetal force/acceleration (F=mv^2/r)
- Work: W=Fd cos(theta) (positive, negative, zero)
- Work-energy theorem: Wnet=delta_KE
- Kinetic energy: KE=1/2mv^2
- Potential energy: gravitational (PE=mgh), elastic (PE=1/2kx^2)
- Conservation of energy
- Power: P=W/t=Fv
- Mechanical advantage (pulleys, levers, inclined planes)
- Center of mass
- Torque: tau=rF sin(theta)
- Rotational equilibrium: sum(tau)=0
- Static equilibrium: sum(F)=0 AND sum(tau)=0
- Linear momentum: p=mv
- Impulse: J=F*delta_t=delta_p
- Conservation of momentum
- Collisions: elastic (KE conserved) vs inelastic (KE not conserved)

Kaplan: Physics Ch 1 (Kinematics/Dynamics), Ch 2 (Work/Energy)

### 4B -- Fluids, Gas Movement, Gas Exchange (Physics, Gen Chem, Bio)

- Density (rho=m/V), specific gravity (rho/rho_water)
- Pressure: P=F/A, gauge vs absolute, hydrostatic (P=rho*g*h)
- Pascal's law: pressure transmitted equally in enclosed fluid
- Archimedes' principle: buoyant force = weight of displaced fluid (Fb=rho_fluid * V_submerged * g)
- Float/sink: compare object density to fluid density
- Continuity equation: A1*v1 = A2*v2
- Bernoulli's equation: P + 1/2*rho*v^2 + rho*g*h = constant (faster flow = lower pressure)
- Poiseuille's law: Q = pi*r^4*delta_P/(8*eta*L) (flow proportional to r^4, critical for blood flow)
- Surface tension, capillary action
- Viscosity
- Reynolds number (laminar vs turbulent flow)
- Ideal gas law: PV=nRT
- Dalton's law of partial pressures
- Kinetic molecular theory
- Real gases: van der Waals equation, deviations from ideal behavior
- Starling forces: hydrostatic vs oncotic pressure in capillary exchange (Kaplan gap)

Kaplan: Physics Ch 4 (Fluids), Gen Chem Ch 8 (Gas Phase). Starling forces and Poiseuille's in biological context often need supplementation.

### 4C -- Electrochemistry and Electrical Circuits (Physics, Gen Chem)

ELECTROSTATICS:
- Charge: quantized (e=1.6e-19 C), conservation, conductors vs insulators
- Coulomb's law: F=kq1q2/r^2
- Electric field: E=F/q=kQ/r^2 (points away from positive)
- Electric potential: V=kQ/r
- Potential energy: U=kq1q2/r
- Equipotential lines (perpendicular to field lines)
- Capacitors: C=epsilon0*A/d (parallel plate), Q=CV, energy=1/2CV^2, dielectrics increase C

MAGNETISM:
- Force on moving charge: F=qvB sin(theta) (right-hand rule)
- Force on current-carrying wire: F=BIL sin(theta)
- Magnetic field from wire: B=mu0*I/(2*pi*r)

CIRCUITS:
- Current: I=Q/t
- Resistance: R=rho*L/A
- Ohm's law: V=IR
- Power: P=IV=I^2R=V^2/R
- Series: R_total=R1+R2 (same current, voltage divides)
- Parallel: 1/R_total=1/R1+1/R2 (same voltage, current divides)
- Kirchhoff's junction rule: sum(I_in) = sum(I_out)
- Kirchhoff's loop rule: sum(V)=0
- Batteries, EMF, internal resistance
- RC circuits: time constant tau=RC
- Ammeters (in series, low R) vs voltmeters (in parallel, high R)

ELECTROCHEMISTRY:
- Galvanic/voltaic cells: spontaneous (delta_G<0, E_cell>0), anode=oxidation, cathode=reduction, salt bridge
- Electrolytic cells: non-spontaneous (require external voltage)
- Standard reduction potentials (E0)
- Cell potential: E0_cell = E0_cathode - E0_anode
- Nernst equation: E = E0 - (RT/nF)lnQ
- Relationship: delta_G0 = -nFE0
- Faraday's law of electrolysis: moles = It/nF
- Concentration cells

Kaplan: Physics Ch 5 (Electrostatics/Magnetism), Ch 6 (Circuits), Gen Chem Ch 11 (Redox), Ch 12 (Electrochemistry)

### 4D -- Light and Sound (Physics, Orgo)

WAVES:
- Properties: wavelength (lambda), frequency (f), amplitude, period (T=1/f), velocity (v=f*lambda)
- Transverse (displacement perpendicular to propagation) vs longitudinal (displacement parallel)
- Superposition and interference (constructive/destructive)
- Standing waves: nodes and antinodes
- Harmonics: string fixed both ends lambda_n=2L/n; pipe open both ends same; pipe closed one end lambda_n=4L/n (odd harmonics only)

SOUND:
- Intensity and decibels: beta=10 log(I/I0)
- Inverse square law
- Resonance, beats (f_beat=|f1-f2|)
- Doppler effect: approaching -> higher f, receding -> lower f
- Ultrasound applications

LIGHT AND OPTICS:
- EM spectrum: radio->microwave->IR->visible->UV->X-ray->gamma
- Photon energy: E=hf=hc/lambda
- Reflection: angle incidence = angle reflection
- Refraction: Snell's law (n1*sin(theta1)=n2*sin(theta2))
- Total internal reflection (critical angle)
- Dispersion
- Mirrors: concave (converging, real images when d>f), convex (diverging, always virtual)
- Lenses: converging (convex), diverging (concave)
- Thin lens/mirror equation: 1/f=1/d_o+1/d_i
- Magnification: m=-d_i/d_o (negative=inverted)
- Sign conventions: real image d_i>0, virtual d_i<0
- Lens power: P=1/f (diopters)
- Diffraction: single-slit, Young's double-slit (d sin(theta)=m*lambda) (Kaplan gap)
- Thin-film interference (constructive/destructive, depends on path difference and phase shifts) (Kaplan gap)
- Polarization
- Photoelectric effect: KE_max=hf-phi

Kaplan: Physics Ch 7 (Waves/Sound), Ch 8 (Light/Optics). Thin-film interference and diffraction details are known Kaplan gaps.

### 4E -- Atoms, Nuclear Decay, Electronic Structure (Physics, Gen Chem)

- Atomic structure: subatomic particles, atomic number, mass number, isotopes
- Bohr model, quantum mechanical model
- Quantum numbers: n, l, ml, ms
- Electron configurations: aufbau, Hund's rule, Pauli exclusion
- Orbital shapes: s, p, d, f
- Periodic trends: electronegativity (up and right), ionization energy (same), electron affinity, atomic radius (down and left), metallic character
- Shielding and effective nuclear charge
- Nuclear decay types: alpha (4_2He, Z-2, A-4), beta-minus (Z+1, A same), beta-plus/positron (Z-1), gamma (no change), electron capture (Z-1)
- Half-life: N=N0*(1/2)^(t/t_half)
- Mass defect and nuclear binding energy
- Fission (heavy->light) and fusion (light->heavy)
- Medical applications: PET scans, radiation therapy
- Stoichiometry: molecular weight, empirical/molecular formulas, mole concept, Avogadro's number, balancing equations, limiting reagents, theoretical yield, percent composition
- Oxidation numbers

Kaplan: Gen Chem Ch 1 (Atomic Structure), Ch 2 (Periodic Table), Ch 4 (Stoichiometry), Physics Ch 9 (Atomic/Nuclear)

## FOUNDATIONAL CONCEPT 5: Chemical principles govern interactions

### 5A -- Acid-Base Chemistry (Gen Chem, Biochem)

THE SINGLE HIGHEST-YIELD GEN CHEM TOPIC.

- Bronsted-Lowry (H+ donor/acceptor) and Lewis (electron pair acceptor/donor) definitions
- Conjugate acid-base pairs
- Strong acids: HCl, HBr, HI, H2SO4, HNO3, HClO4
- Strong bases: group 1 hydroxides (NaOH, KOH)
- Weak acid/base equilibria: Ka, Kb, Ka*Kb=Kw=1e-14
- pH = -log[H+], pOH, pH+pOH=14, pKa=-logKa
- Polyprotic acids (H3PO4, H2CO3)
- Henderson-Hasselbalch: pH = pKa + log([A-]/[HA])
- Buffer capacity, optimal buffering at pH=pKa +/- 1
- Titration types: strong/strong, weak acid+strong base, weak base+strong acid
- Equivalence point pH: =7 (strong/strong), >7 (weak acid), <7 (weak base)
- Half-equivalence point: pH=pKa
- Interpreting titration curves
- Indicators
- Solutions: molarity, molality, mole fraction, percent composition
- Solubility: Ksp, common-ion effect, complex ion formation
- Colligative properties: BP elevation (delta_Tb=i*Kb*m), FP depression (delta_Tf=i*Kf*m), osmotic pressure (pi=iMRT), vapor pressure lowering (Raoult's law)
- van't Hoff factor (i)

Kaplan: Gen Chem Ch 9 (Solutions), Ch 10 (Acids and Bases)

### 5B -- Molecular Structure and IMFs (Gen Chem, Orgo)

- Lewis structures, resonance, formal charge
- VSEPR: molecular geometry and bond angles
- Hybridization: sp (linear, 180), sp2 (trigonal planar, 120), sp3 (tetrahedral, 109.5)
- Sigma and pi bonds
- Bond polarity, dipole moments
- IMFs: London dispersion, dipole-dipole, hydrogen bonding (N-H, O-H, F-H), ion-dipole
- IMF strength -> physical properties (BP, solubility)
- Stereochemistry: constitutional isomers, enantiomers, diastereomers, meso compounds, cis-trans/E-Z
- Chirality, optical activity, specific rotation
- R/S configuration (Cahn-Ingold-Prelog)
- Fischer projections
- Racemic mixtures

Kaplan: Gen Chem Ch 3, Orgo Ch 1-3

### 5C -- Separation and Purification (Orgo, Biochem)

- Extraction (liquid-liquid, acid-base)
- Distillation (simple, fractional, vacuum)
- Chromatography: TLC (Rf values), column, HPLC, gas chromatography
- Size-exclusion chromatography (larger elute first)
- Ion-exchange chromatography
- Affinity chromatography (ligand-specific, highest purity)
- Electrophoresis: agarose gel, native PAGE, SDS-PAGE (separates by size -- SDS denatures + uniform negative charge), isoelectric focusing, 2D gel
- Centrifugation: differential, density gradient/ultracentrifugation
- Recrystallization
- Filtration, dialysis

### 5E -- Thermodynamics, Kinetics, Enzymes (Gen Chem, Biochem)

THERMODYNAMICS:
- State functions (delta_H, delta_S, delta_G, delta_U) vs path functions (q, w)
- Zeroth, first, second laws
- Enthalpy: endo/exothermic, Hess's law, bond dissociation energy
- Entropy: delta_S>0 for more disorder
- Gibbs free energy: delta_G=delta_H-T*delta_S, spontaneity, delta_G0=-RT*lnKeq, delta_G=delta_G0+RT*lnQ
- Calorimetry: q=mc*delta_T, bomb calorimeter
- Phase diagrams: triple point, critical point

KINETICS:
- Rate laws: rate=k[A]^n[B]^m, reaction orders
- Integrated rate laws, half-life (1st order: t_half=0.693/k)
- Rate-determining step
- Energy diagrams: Ea, transition state, intermediate
- Arrhenius equation: k=Ae^(-Ea/RT)
- Catalysts: lower Ea, homogeneous vs heterogeneous

EQUILIBRIUM:
- Keq, Q vs K, Le Chatelier's principle
- delta_G0=-RT*lnKeq relationship

ENZYMES:
- Active-site model, induced fit
- Cofactors, coenzymes, vitamins
- Michaelis-Menten: Km (affinity, lower=tighter), Vmax
- Lineweaver-Burk plots
- Inhibition types and effects on Km/Vmax:
  - Competitive: increased apparent Km, same Vmax
  - Noncompetitive: same Km, decreased Vmax
  - Uncompetitive: decreased both Km and Vmax
  - Mixed: increased Km, decreased Vmax (or variable)
- Allosteric regulation, cooperativity
- Covalent modification (phosphorylation)
- Zymogens/proenzymes

BIOENERGETICS:
- ATP hydrolysis, group transfer potential
- Biological oxidation-reduction
- Electron carriers: NAD+/NADH, FAD/FADH2
- Coupled reactions
- Chemiosmotic theory

Kaplan: Biochem Ch 2, 12; Gen Chem Ch 5 (Kinetics), Ch 6 (Equilibrium), Ch 7 (Thermo); Physics Ch 3

## SPECTROSCOPY (Orgo)

IR SPECTROSCOPY -- key absorptions:
- Broad O-H: ~3200-3550 (alcohol), ~2500-3300 (carboxylic acid)
- Sharp C=O: ~1700-1750
- N-H: ~3300-3500 (1 peak 2nd amine, 2 peaks 1st amine)
- C-O: ~1000-1250

1H NMR:
- Chemical shift (delta, ppm), TMS reference at 0
- Shielding/deshielding
- Splitting: n+1 rule
- Integration: area proportional to number of protons
- D2O shake removes O-H and N-H peaks

13C NMR: number of unique carbon environments, no splitting

MASS SPECTROMETRY:
- Molecular ion peak (M+)
- Common fragment losses: 15 (CH3), 17 (OH), 18 (H2O), 28 (CO), 29 (CHO), 45 (OEt)
- Base peak = tallest

UV-Vis:
- Beer-Lambert law: A=epsilon*l*c
- Conjugation -> longer lambda_max

Kaplan: Orgo Ch 11
