SUBATOMIC PARTICLES:
- Proton (+1, ~1 amu, nucleus), neutron (0, ~1 amu, nucleus), electron (-1, ~1/1836 amu, orbitals)
- Atomic number Z = # protons (defines element); mass number A = p + n
- Isotopes: same Z, different A (e.g., C-12 vs C-14)
- Ions: atoms with gained/lost electrons; nucleus unchanged

ATOMIC MASS vs ATOMIC WEIGHT:
- Atomic mass = mass of single atom (specific isotope, in amu)
- Atomic weight = weighted average of natural isotope abundance (periodic table value)

BOHR MODEL (works for H, He+, Li2+):
- Electrons in fixed energy levels n = 1, 2, 3...
- E_n = -13.6 eV / n^2 (most important equation)
- Ground state vs excited state; emission (drop) vs absorption (jump)
- ΔE = -13.6(1/n_f^2 - 1/n_i^2) eV; |ΔE| = hf = hc/λ
- Spectral series: Lyman (→n=1, UV), Balmer (→n=2, visible), Paschen (→n=3, IR)

QUANTUM MECHANICAL MODEL:
- Orbitals = probability distributions (~90% confidence region)
- Heisenberg uncertainty: cannot know position + momentum exactly

QUANTUM NUMBERS:
- n (principal): 1, 2, 3...; max electrons = 2n^2
- l (angular momentum): 0 to n-1; s/p/d/f shapes
- m_l (magnetic): -l to +l; orientation; orbitals = 2l+1
- m_s (spin): ±1/2; Pauli exclusion (no two electrons same 4 quantum numbers)

ELECTRON CONFIGURATION RULES:
- Aufbau: fill lowest energy first (1s, 2s, 2p, 3s, 3p, 4s, 3d, 4p, 5s, 4d, 5p, 6s, 4f, 5d, 6p...)
- Hund's: fill orbitals singly with parallel spins before pairing
- Pauli exclusion: max 2 electrons per orbital, opposite spins
- Noble gas shorthand: [Ar] 4s^2 3d^6 for Fe

EXCEPTIONS:
- Cr (Z=24): [Ar] 4s^1 3d^5 (half-filled d stability)
- Cu (Z=29): [Ar] 4s^1 3d^10 (full d stability)

ELECTRON CONFIG OF IONS:
- Remove from highest n FIRST (4s before 3d for transition metals)
- Fe^2+ = [Ar] 3d^6, Fe^3+ = [Ar] 3d^5

VALENCE ELECTRONS:
- Main group: highest n shell only
- Transition metals: ns + (n-1)d both available → multiple oxidation states

PARAMAGNETISM vs DIAMAGNETISM:
- Paramagnetic: unpaired electrons, weakly attracted to magnets
- Diamagnetic: all paired, weakly repelled
