# CP Gen Chem Chapter 2 — The Periodic Table

Scope: Periodic table organization; types of elements; periodic trends (atomic radius, IE, EA, EN, metallic character); chemistry of major groups.

AAMC FC mapping: FC4E (periodic trends portion)

Kaplan: General Chemistry Ch 2

---

EFFECTIVE NUCLEAR CHARGE (master concept):
- Z_eff = Z - S (nuclear charge minus shielding by inner electrons)
- Across period: Z_eff INCREASES (same shielding, more protons)
- Down group: Z_eff roughly constant; new shell, more shielding, electrons farther

ATOMIC RADIUS:
- Decreases L→R; Increases top→bottom
- Cations < parent atom; anions > parent atom
- Isoelectronic series: more protons = smaller (O^2- > F^- > Na+ > Mg^2+)

IONIZATION ENERGY (IE):
- Energy to remove outermost e- from gaseous atom
- Increases L→R; bottom→top (same as EN)
- Successive IEs: huge jump after valence electrons exhausted (reveals valence count)
- Exceptions: Be > B (s vs p); N > O (half-filled p^3 stability)

ELECTRON AFFINITY (EA):
- Energy released when gaseous atom gains e-
- More negative L→R, bottom→top (less smooth than IE)
- Halogens: most negative EA; noble gases: ~zero/positive

ELECTRONEGATIVITY (EN, Pauling):
- Atom's pull on bonding electrons
- Increases L→R, bottom→top
- F = 4.0 (highest); Fr = lowest
- ΔEN determines bond polarity (ionic vs polar covalent vs nonpolar)

METALLIC CHARACTER:
- Opposite of EN; increases down and left
- Metals: low IE/EN, form cations
- Nonmetals: high IE/EN/EA, form anions

ELEMENT TYPES:
- Metals (left/center): lustrous, conductive, malleable
- Nonmetals (upper right): dull, brittle, insulating
- Metalloids (staircase B/Si/Ge/As/Sb/Te): semiconductors
- Transition metals (d-block): variable oxidation states, colored complexes
- Noble gases (Group 18): full shells, inert

GROUP CHEMISTRY:
- Group 1 (alkali): ns^1, +1 cations, very reactive (vigorous w/ H2O)
- Group 2 (alkaline earth): ns^2, +2 cations
- Group 17 (halogens): ns^2 np^5, -1 anions, F2 > Cl2 > Br2 > I2 reactivity
- Group 18 (noble): full octet, inert
