## SECTION 1: CP — CHEMICAL AND PHYSICAL FOUNDATIONS

Discipline mix: ~30% general chemistry, 25% physics, 25% biochemistry, 15% organic chemistry, 5% biology.

### FOUNDATIONAL CONCEPT 4: Physical processes underlie biological systems

#### 4A — Translational Motion, Forces, Work, Energy, Equilibrium (Physics)

- Kinematics: displacement, velocity (average and instantaneous), acceleration
- Big 5 equations: v=v₀+at, x=x₀+v₀t+½at², v²=v₀²+2aΔx
- Free fall (g=9.8 m/s²)
- Projectile motion: horizontal (constant v) and vertical (constant a=g) are independent
- Newton's laws: 1st (inertia), 2nd (F=ma), 3rd (action-reaction)
- Friction: static (fs≤μsN) and kinetic (fk=μkN)
- Free body diagrams
- Inclined planes: mg sinθ parallel, mg cosθ perpendicular
- Circular motion and centripetal force/acceleration (F=mv²/r)
- Work: W=Fd cosθ (positive, negative, zero)
- Work-energy theorem: Wnet=ΔKE
- Kinetic energy: KE=½mv²
- Potential energy: gravitational (PE=mgh), elastic (PE=½kx²)
- Conservation of energy
- Power: P=W/t=Fv
- Mechanical advantage (pulleys, levers, inclined planes)
- Center of mass
- Torque: τ=rF sinθ
- Rotational equilibrium: Στ=0
- Static equilibrium: ΣF=0 AND Στ=0
- Linear momentum: p=mv
- Impulse: J=FΔt=Δp
- Conservation of momentum
- Collisions: elastic (KE conserved) vs inelastic (KE not conserved)

Kaplan: Physics Ch 1 (Kinematics/Dynamics), Ch 2 (Work/Energy)

#### 4B — Fluids, Gas Movement, Gas Exchange ⭐ (Physics, Gen Chem, Bio)

- Density (ρ=m/V), specific gravity (ρ/ρ_water)
- Pressure: P=F/A, gauge vs absolute, hydrostatic (P=ρgh)
- Pascal's law: pressure transmitted equally in enclosed fluid
- Archimedes' principle: buoyant force = weight of displaced fluid (Fb=ρ_fluid × V_submerged × g)
- Float/sink: compare object density to fluid density
- Continuity equation: A₁v₁ = A₂v₂
- Bernoulli's equation: P + ½ρv² + ρgh = constant (faster flow = lower pressure) ⭐
- Poiseuille's law: Q = πr⁴ΔP/8ηL (flow ∝ r⁴, critical for blood flow applications) ⭐
- Surface tension, capillary action
- Viscosity
- Reynolds number (laminar vs turbulent flow)
- Ideal gas law: PV=nRT
- Dalton's law of partial pressures
- Kinetic molecular theory
- Real gases: van der Waals equation, deviations from ideal behavior
- Starling forces: hydrostatic vs oncotic pressure in capillary exchange ⚠️

Kaplan: Physics Ch 4 (Fluids), Gen Chem Ch 8 (Gas Phase). Starling forces and Poiseuille's in biological context often need supplementation.

#### 4C — Electrochemistry and Electrical Circuits ⭐ (Physics, Gen Chem)

ELECTROSTATICS:
- Charge: quantized (e=1.6×10⁻¹⁹ C), conservation, conductors vs insulators
- Coulomb's law: F=kq₁q₂/r²
- Electric field: E=F/q=kQ/r² (points away from positive)
- Electric potential: V=kQ/r
- Potential energy: U=kq₁q₂/r
- Equipotential lines (perpendicular to field lines)
- Capacitors: C=ε₀A/d (parallel plate), Q=CV, energy=½CV², dielectrics increase C

MAGNETISM:
- Force on moving charge: F=qvBsinθ (right-hand rule)
- Force on current-carrying wire: F=BILsinθ
- Magnetic field from wire: B=μ₀I/2πr

CIRCUITS: ⭐
- Current: I=Q/t
- Resistance: R=ρL/A
- Ohm's law: V=IR
- Power: P=IV=I²R=V²/R
- Series: R_total=R₁+R₂ (same current, voltage divides)
- Parallel: 1/R_total=1/R₁+1/R₂ (same voltage, current divides)
- Kirchhoff's junction rule: ΣI_in = ΣI_out
- Kirchhoff's loop rule: ΣV=0
- Batteries, EMF, internal resistance
- RC circuits: time constant τ=RC
- Ammeters (in series, low R) vs voltmeters (in parallel, high R)

ELECTROCHEMISTRY: ⭐
- Galvanic/voltaic cells: spontaneous (ΔG<0, E°>0), anode=oxidation, cathode=reduction, salt bridge
- Electrolytic cells: non-spontaneous (require external voltage)
- Standard reduction potentials (E°)
- Cell potential: E°_cell = E°_cathode − E°_anode
- Nernst equation: E = E° − (RT/nF)lnQ
- Relationship: ΔG° = −nFE°
- Faraday's law of electrolysis: moles = It/nF
- Concentration cells

Kaplan: Physics Ch 5 (Electrostatics/Magnetism), Ch 6 (Circuits), Gen Chem Ch 11 (Redox), Ch 12 (Electrochemistry)

#### 4D — Light and Sound (Physics, Orgo)

WAVES:
- Properties: wavelength (λ), frequency (f), amplitude, period (T=1/f), velocity (v=fλ)
- Transverse (displacement ⊥ propagation) vs longitudinal (displacement ∥)
- Superposition and interference (constructive/destructive)
- Standing waves: nodes and antinodes
- Harmonics: string fixed both ends λn=2L/n; pipe open both ends same; pipe closed one end λn=4L/n (odd harmonics only)

SOUND:
- Intensity and decibels: β=10 log(I/I₀)
- Inverse square law
- Resonance, beats (f_beat=|f₁−f₂|)
- Doppler effect: approaching → higher f, receding → lower f
- Ultrasound applications

LIGHT AND OPTICS: ⭐
- EM spectrum: radio→microwave→IR→visible→UV→X-ray→gamma
- Photon energy: E=hf=hc/λ
- Reflection: angle incidence = angle reflection
- Refraction: Snell's law (n₁sinθ₁=n₂sinθ₂)
- Total internal reflection (critical angle)
- Dispersion
- Mirrors: concave (converging, real images when d>f), convex (diverging, always virtual)
- Lenses: converging (convex), diverging (concave)
- Thin lens/mirror equation: 1/f=1/d_o+1/d_i
- Magnification: m=−d_i/d_o (negative=inverted)
- Sign conventions: real image d_i>0, virtual d_i<0
- Lens power: P=1/f (diopters)
- Diffraction: single-slit, Young's double-slit (d sinθ=mλ) ⚠️
- Thin-film interference (constructive/destructive, depends on path difference and phase shifts) ⚠️
- Polarization
- Photoelectric effect: KE_max=hf−φ

Kaplan: Physics Ch 7 (Waves/Sound), Ch 8 (Light/Optics). Thin-film interference and diffraction details are known Kaplan gaps.

#### 4E — Atoms, Nuclear Decay, Electronic Structure (Physics, Gen Chem)

- Atomic structure: subatomic particles, atomic number, mass number, isotopes
- Bohr model, quantum mechanical model
- Quantum numbers: n, l, ml, ms
- Electron configurations: aufbau, Hund's rule, Pauli exclusion
- Orbital shapes: s, p, d, f
- Periodic trends ⭐: electronegativity (↑ up and right), ionization energy (same), electron affinity, atomic radius (↓ up and right), metallic character
- Shielding and effective nuclear charge
- Nuclear decay types: alpha (⁴₂He, Z−2, A−4), beta-minus (Z+1, A same), beta-plus/positron (Z−1), gamma (no change), electron capture (Z−1)
- Half-life: N=N₀(½)^(t/t₁/₂)
- Mass defect and nuclear binding energy
- Fission (heavy→light) and fusion (light→heavy)
- Medical applications: PET scans, radiation therapy
- Stoichiometry: molecular weight, empirical/molecular formulas, mole concept, Avogadro's number, balancing equations, limiting reagents, theoretical yield, percent composition
- Oxidation numbers

Kaplan: Gen Chem Ch 1 (Atomic Structure), Ch 2 (Periodic Table), Ch 4 (Stoichiometry), Physics Ch 9 (Atomic/Nuclear)

### FOUNDATIONAL CONCEPT 5: Chemical principles govern interactions

#### 5A — Acid-Base Chemistry ⭐⭐ (Gen Chem, Biochem)

THE SINGLE HIGHEST-YIELD GEN CHEM TOPIC.

- Brønsted-Lowry (H⁺ donor/acceptor) and Lewis (electron pair acceptor/donor) definitions
- Conjugate acid-base pairs
- Strong acids: HCl, HBr, HI, H₂SO₄, HNO₃, HClO₄
- Strong bases: group 1 hydroxides (NaOH, KOH)
- Weak acid/base equilibria: Ka, Kb, Ka×Kb=Kw=10⁻¹⁴
- pH = −log[H⁺], pOH, pH+pOH=14, pKa=−logKa
- Polyprotic acids (H₃PO₄, H₂CO₃)
- Henderson-Hasselbalch: pH = pKa + log([A⁻]/[HA]) ⭐
- Buffer capacity, optimal buffering at pH=pKa±1
- Titration types: strong/strong, weak acid+strong base, weak base+strong acid
- Equivalence point pH: =7 (strong/strong), >7 (weak acid), <7 (weak base)
- Half-equivalence point: pH=pKa ⭐
- Interpreting titration curves ⭐
- Indicators
- Solutions: molarity, molality, mole fraction, percent composition
- Solubility: Ksp, common-ion effect, complex ion formation
- Colligative properties: BP elevation (ΔTb=iKbm), FP depression (ΔTf=iKfm), osmotic pressure (π=iMRT), vapor pressure lowering (Raoult's law)
- van't Hoff factor (i)

Kaplan: Gen Chem Ch 9 (Solutions), Ch 10 (Acids and Bases)

#### 5B — Molecular Structure and IMFs (Gen Chem, Orgo)

- Lewis structures, resonance, formal charge
- VSEPR: molecular geometry and bond angles
- Hybridization: sp (linear, 180°), sp² (trigonal planar, 120°), sp³ (tetrahedral, 109.5°)
- Sigma and pi bonds
- Bond polarity, dipole moments
- IMFs: London dispersion, dipole-dipole, hydrogen bonding (N-H, O-H, F-H), ion-dipole
- IMF strength → physical properties (BP, solubility)
- Stereochemistry ⭐: constitutional isomers, enantiomers, diastereomers, meso compounds, cis-trans/E-Z
- Chirality, optical activity, specific rotation
- R/S configuration (Cahn-Ingold-Prelog)
- Fischer projections
- Racemic mixtures

Kaplan: Gen Chem Ch 3, Orgo Ch 1-3

#### 5C — Separation and Purification ⭐ (Orgo, Biochem)

#### 5E — Thermodynamics, Kinetics, Enzymes ⭐ (Gen Chem, Biochem)

THERMODYNAMICS:
- State functions (ΔH, ΔS, ΔG, ΔU) vs path functions (q, w)
- Zeroth, first, second laws
- Enthalpy: endo/exothermic, Hess's law, bond dissociation energy
- Entropy: ΔS>0 for more disorder
- Gibbs free energy: ΔG=ΔH−TΔS, spontaneity, ΔG°=−RTlnKeq, ΔG=ΔG°+RTlnQ
- Calorimetry: q=mcΔT, bomb calorimeter
- Phase diagrams: triple point, critical point

KINETICS:
- Rate laws: rate=k[A]ⁿ[B]ᵐ, reaction orders
- Integrated rate laws, half-life (1st order: t₁/₂=0.693/k)
- Rate-determining step
- Energy diagrams: Ea, transition state, intermediate
- Arrhenius equation: k=Ae^(−Ea/RT)
- Catalysts: lower Ea, homogeneous vs heterogeneous

EQUILIBRIUM:
- Keq, Q vs K, Le Châtelier's principle
- ΔG°=−RTlnKeq relationship

ENZYMES: ⭐⭐
- Active-site model, induced fit
- Cofactors, coenzymes, vitamins
- Michaelis-Menten: Km (affinity, lower=tighter), Vmax
- Lineweaver-Burk plots ⭐
- Inhibition types and effects on Km/Vmax:
  - Competitive: ↑apparent Km, same Vmax
  - Noncompetitive: same Km, ↓Vmax
  - Uncompetitive: ↓both Km and Vmax
  - Mixed: ↑Km, ↓Vmax (or variable)
- Allosteric regulation, cooperativity
- Covalent modification (phosphorylation)
- Zymogens/proenzymes

BIOENERGETICS:
- ATP hydrolysis, group transfer potential
- Biological oxidation-reduction
- Electron carriers: NAD⁺/NADH, FAD/FADH₂
- Coupled reactions
- Chemiosmotic theory

Kaplan: Biochem Ch 2, 12; Gen Chem Ch 5 (Kinetics), Ch 6 (Equilibrium), Ch 7 (Thermo); Physics Ch 3

### FOUNDATIONAL CONCEPT 1: Biomolecules have unique properties

#### 1A — Protein Structure and Function ⭐ (Biochem)

AMINO ACIDS — HIGHEST-YIELD BB TOPIC:
- All 20 structures, 1-letter and 3-letter codes
- Classifications: nonpolar (G,A,V,L,I,P,F,W,M), polar uncharged (S,T,C,N,Q), positive at pH 7 (K,R,H), negative at pH 7 (D,E)
- Special properties: Gly (smallest, achiral), Pro (cyclic, disrupts α-helix), Cys (disulfide bonds), His (pKa ~6, enzyme active sites), Trp (largest, UV 280nm), Met (start codon)
- Acid-base behavior: pKa values, pI calculations
- L vs D stereochemistry

PROTEIN STRUCTURE:
- Primary: amino acid sequence
- Secondary: α-helix (H-bonds i to i+4, right-handed), β-sheet (parallel/antiparallel, H-bonds between strands), turns (Pro, Gly)
- Tertiary: hydrophobic interactions, disulfide bonds, salt bridges, H-bonds
- Quaternary: subunit assembly, cooperativity
- Denaturation (heat, pH, detergent, urea)
- Chaperones

ENZYME KINETICS: see FC5E above (tested in both CP and BB)

NON-ENZYMATIC PROTEIN FUNCTIONS:
- Structural: collagen, keratin, elastin
- Motor: myosin, kinesin, dynein
- Cell adhesion: cadherins, integrins
- Immune: antibodies, complement
- Signaling: receptors, G-proteins

SIGNAL TRANSDUCTION: ⭐
- GPCRs: Gαs (stimulatory → adenylyl cyclase → ↑cAMP → PKA), Gαi (inhibitory → ↓cAMP), Gαq (→ PLC → IP₃ + DAG → Ca²⁺ release)
- Receptor tyrosine kinases (RTKs): dimerization → autophosphorylation → Ras-MAPK cascade
- Second messengers: cAMP, IP₃, DAG, Ca²⁺ (calmodulin)
- Ligand-gated ion channels
- Intracellular receptors (steroid hormones — lipophilic, cross membrane)
- Kinase cascades and signal amplification

Kaplan: Biochem Ch 1-3

DNA:
- Double helix, base pairing (A-T 2 H-bonds, G-C 3 H-bonds), antiparallel

#### 1D — Metabolism ⭐ (Biochem)

GLYCOLYSIS (cytoplasm):
- 10 steps — key regulated/irreversible enzymes:
  - Hexokinase/glucokinase: glucose → G6P (−1 ATP)
  - PFK-1: F6P → F1,6BP (−1 ATP) — RATE-LIMITING STEP ⭐
  - Pyruvate kinase: PEP → pyruvate (+1 ATP)
- Net yield: 2 ATP, 2 NADH, 2 pyruvate per glucose
- Substrate-level phosphorylation
- PFK-1 regulation: activated by AMP, fructose-2,6-BP; inhibited by ATP, citrate

FERMENTATION (anaerobic):
- Lactate dehydrogenase: pyruvate → lactate (regenerates NAD⁺)
- Alcohol fermentation: pyruvate → acetaldehyde → ethanol

PYRUVATE DEHYDROGENASE COMPLEX:
- Pyruvate → acetyl-CoA + CO₂ + NADH (irreversible)
- Requires 5 cofactors: TPP, lipoic acid, CoA, FAD, NAD⁺
- Links glycolysis to TCA

TCA CYCLE (mitochondrial matrix):
- Per acetyl-CoA: 3 NADH, 1 FADH₂, 1 GTP, 2 CO₂
- Regulated enzymes: citrate synthase, isocitrate dehydrogenase, α-ketoglutarate dehydrogenase
- Regulation: inhibited by ATP, NADH; activated by ADP

ELECTRON TRANSPORT CHAIN (inner mitochondrial membrane): ⭐
- Complex I: NADH → CoQ (pumps 4H⁺)
- Complex II: FADH₂ → CoQ (NO pumping — this is why FADH₂ yields less ATP)
- Complex III: CoQ → Cyt c (pumps 4H⁺)
- Complex IV: Cyt c → O₂ → H₂O (pumps 2H⁺)
- Inhibitors: rotenone (I), antimycin A (III), cyanide/CO (IV), oligomycin (ATP synthase) ⭐
- Uncouplers: DNP, thermogenin/UCP1 (brown fat — heat, no ATP)

OXIDATIVE PHOSPHORYLATION:
- Chemiosmotic theory: proton gradient across inner membrane drives ATP synthase
- P/O ratios: ~2.5 ATP/NADH, ~1.5 ATP/FADH₂
- Total: ~30-32 ATP per glucose

GLUCONEOGENESIS:
- 3 bypass enzymes: pyruvate carboxylase, PEP carboxykinase, fructose-1,6-bisphosphatase, glucose-6-phosphatase
- Occurs in liver (and kidney)
- Cori cycle: muscle lactate → liver → glucose → muscle

GLYCOGEN METABOLISM:
- Glycogenesis: glycogen synthase (α-1,4 bonds), branching enzyme (α-1,6)
- Glycogenolysis: glycogen phosphorylase, debranching enzyme
- Hormonal control: insulin → synthase ON; glucagon/epinephrine → phosphorylase ON (via cAMP → PKA cascade)

PENTOSE PHOSPHATE PATHWAY:
- Oxidative phase: G6P → NADPH + ribulose-5-P (G6P dehydrogenase, rate-limiting)
- Non-oxidative phase: ribose-5-P (for nucleotide synthesis)
- NADPH uses: fatty acid synthesis, steroid synthesis, glutathione reduction (ROS defense)

LIPID METABOLISM:
- β-oxidation: activation (acyl-CoA, outer membrane, −2 ATP equiv), carnitine shuttle into matrix, each cycle: 1 FADH₂ + 1 NADH + 1 acetyl-CoA
- FA synthesis: cytoplasm, acetyl-CoA carboxylase (rate-limiting, +insulin, −glucagon), citrate shuttle, requires NADPH
- Ketogenesis: liver, produces acetoacetate, β-hydroxybutyrate, acetone; used by brain/heart during starvation

AMINO ACID METABOLISM:
- Transamination: aminotransferases (require PLP/B₆)
- Oxidative deamination: glutamate dehydrogenase → NH₄⁺
- Urea cycle: liver, 2 ATP per urea molecule, detoxifies NH₃
- Glucogenic AAs → pyruvate or TCA intermediates → glucose
- Ketogenic AAs → acetyl-CoA or acetoacetyl-CoA → ketone bodies
- Both: Ile, Leu, Phe, Trp, Tyr (only Leu and Lys are purely ketogenic)

METABOLIC INTEGRATION: ⭐
- Fed state (insulin): glycolysis ON, glycogenesis ON, FA synthesis ON, protein synthesis ON
- Fasting (glucagon): glycogenolysis ON, gluconeogenesis ON, β-oxidation ON, ketogenesis begins
- Starvation (cortisol + glucagon): protein catabolism, gluconeogenesis from AAs, ketone bodies fuel brain
- Tissue-specific: liver (metabolic hub, all pathways), muscle (glycogen + protein, cannot export glucose), brain (glucose-dependent → ketone-adapted), adipose (TAG storage/lipolysis)

Kaplan: Biochem Ch 9-12

### FOUNDATIONAL CONCEPT 2: Cells and cellular assemblies

#### 2A — Cell Structure and Membranes (Bio, Biochem)

ORGANELLES:
- Nucleus: nuclear envelope (double membrane), nuclear pores, nucleolus (rRNA)
- Mitochondria: double membrane, own circular DNA, matrix (TCA), inner membrane (ETC), IMS (proton gradient)
- Rough ER: ribosomes, secretory protein synthesis
- Smooth ER: lipid synthesis, detoxification, Ca²⁺ storage
- Golgi: cis (receiving) → trans (shipping), modification (glycosylation), sorting, packaging
- Lysosomes: acid hydrolases (pH ~5), autophagy, phagocytic digestion
- Peroxisomes: H₂O₂ metabolism, catalase, β-oxidation of very long chain FA

CYTOSKELETON:
- Microfilaments: actin, 7nm, muscle contraction, cell motility, cytokinesis (cleavage furrow)
- Intermediate filaments: structural support, keratin, vimentin, neurofilaments, lamins
- Microtubules: tubulin (α/β dimers), 25nm, cilia, flagella, centrioles, mitotic spindle, intracellular transport (kinesin toward +end, dynein toward −end)

MEMBRANE: ⭐
- Fluid mosaic model, phospholipid bilayer
- Cholesterol: fluidity buffer (prevents extremes)
- Integral (transmembrane) vs peripheral proteins
- Membrane fluidity factors: unsaturated FA (↑), shorter chains (↑), cholesterol (buffers)

TRANSPORT: ⭐
- Passive: simple diffusion, facilitated (channels, carriers), osmosis
- Tonicity: hypertonic (cell shrinks), hypotonic (cell swells/lyses), isotonic
- Active primary: Na⁺/K⁺ ATPase (3Na⁺ out, 2K⁺ in, 1 ATP)
- Active secondary: symport (same direction), antiport (opposite)
- Endocytosis: phagocytosis, pinocytosis, receptor-mediated
- Exocytosis

CELL JUNCTIONS:
- Tight junctions: seal, prevent paracellular transport
- Gap junctions: communication (connexons)
- Desmosomes: adhesion (anchoring)

#### 2B — Prokaryotes and Viruses (Bio)

PROKARYOTES:
- Cell wall: peptidoglycan — Gram+ (thick wall, crystal violet) vs Gram− (thin wall + outer membrane + LPS)
- Ribosomes: 70S (30S+50S)
- Flagella, pili (attachment, conjugation), plasmids
- Nucleoid region (no membrane-bound nucleus)
- Endospores, biofilms
- Binary fission
- Genetic transfer: transformation (uptake from environment), transduction (phage-mediated: generalized/specialized), conjugation (F plasmid, pilus)

VIRUSES:
- Structure: nucleic acid core (DNA or RNA, ss or ds) + capsid ± envelope
- Lytic cycle: attachment → injection → replication → assembly → lysis
- Lysogenic cycle: integration as prophage → latency → can switch to lytic
- Retroviruses: RNA → reverse transcriptase → DNA → integrase → host genome
- Bacteriophages
- Prions: misfolded proteins (PrP^Sc), no nucleic acid ⚠️
- Fungi basics: chitin cell wall, ergosterol membrane ⚠️

#### 2C — Cell Division and Differentiation (Bio)

CELL CYCLE:
- G₁ (growth, organelle production), S (DNA replication), G₂ (preparation), M (mitosis)
- G₀: quiescent state (non-dividing)
- Checkpoints: G₁/S (restriction point), G₂/M, spindle assembly
- Cyclins + CDKs drive progression ⭐

MITOSIS: PMAT
- Prophase: chromatin condenses, nuclear envelope breaks down, spindle forms
- Metaphase: chromosomes align at metaphase plate
- Anaphase: sister chromatids separate, move to poles
- Telophase: nuclear envelopes reform, chromosomes decondense

APOPTOSIS:
- Programmed cell death, caspases (executioner enzymes)
- Intrinsic pathway (mitochondrial — cytochrome c release)
- Extrinsic pathway (death receptor — Fas, TNF)

CANCER:
- Loss of cell cycle control
- Oncogenes: gain-of-function mutations (e.g., Ras, Myc, Src)
- Tumor suppressors: loss-of-function (p53 — guardian of genome, Rb, BRCA)
- Metastasis

STEM CELLS: totipotent → pluripotent → multipotent → unipotent

### FOUNDATIONAL CONCEPT 3: Organ systems provide coordination

#### 3A — Nervous and Endocrine Systems ⭐ (Bio)

NERVOUS SYSTEM ORGANIZATION:
- CNS: brain + spinal cord
- PNS: somatic (voluntary) + autonomic (involuntary)
- Autonomic: sympathetic (fight-or-flight) vs parasympathetic (rest-and-digest)

NEURONS:
- Structure: cell body (soma), dendrites (receive), axon (transmit), myelin sheath (Schwann cells in PNS, oligodendrocytes in CNS), nodes of Ranvier, synaptic terminal
- Types: sensory (afferent), motor (efferent), interneurons

NEURAL SIGNALING: ⭐
- Resting potential: −70mV (K⁺ leak channels, Na⁺/K⁺ ATPase)
- Action potential: depolarization (Na⁺ influx), repolarization (K⁺ efflux), hyperpolarization
- Threshold: ~−55mV, all-or-nothing
- Refractory periods: absolute (no new AP possible), relative (stronger stimulus needed)
- Saltatory conduction (myelinated = faster, jumps between nodes)
- Synaptic transmission: AP → Ca²⁺ influx → vesicle fusion → neurotransmitter release → receptor binding → post-synaptic potential

NEUROTRANSMITTERS: ⭐
- Acetylcholine (ACh): NMJ, parasympathetic, memory
- Norepinephrine (NE): sympathetic, arousal, attention
- Dopamine: reward, motivation, motor control (substantia nigra → basal ganglia)
- Serotonin (5-HT): mood, sleep, appetite
- GABA: main inhibitory NT in CNS
- Glutamate: main excitatory NT in CNS
- Endorphins: pain modulation, opioid receptors

BRAIN ANATOMY:
- Cerebral cortex: frontal (executive function, motor cortex, Broca's area), parietal (somatosensory), temporal (auditory, Wernicke's area), occipital (vision)
- Limbic system: amygdala (fear/emotion), hippocampus (memory consolidation), hypothalamus (homeostasis, drives)
- Thalamus: sensory relay station (all senses except smell)
- Cerebellum: coordination, balance
- Brainstem: medulla (vital functions — breathing, HR, BP), pons, midbrain
- Basal ganglia: motor control (Parkinson's — dopamine loss here)
- Spinal cord: reflex arcs, ascending/descending tracts

ENDOCRINE SYSTEM: ⭐
- Hormone types: peptide (hydrophilic, cell surface receptors, fast) vs steroid (lipophilic, intracellular receptors, slow, gene expression)
- Hypothalamic-pituitary axis
- Anterior pituitary: GH, TSH, ACTH, FSH, LH, prolactin (controlled by hypothalamic releasing/inhibiting hormones)
- Posterior pituitary: ADH (water reabsorption), oxytocin (uterine contraction, milk letdown) — made in hypothalamus, stored in posterior pituitary
- Thyroid: T3/T4 (↑BMR, requires iodine), calcitonin (↓blood Ca²⁺)
- Parathyroid: PTH (↑blood Ca²⁺ — opposes calcitonin) ⭐
- Adrenal cortex: cortisol (stress, gluconeogenesis, anti-inflammatory), aldosterone (Na⁺ reabsorption, K⁺ secretion), DHEA (androgens)
- Adrenal medulla: epinephrine/norepinephrine (fight-or-flight)
- Pancreas: insulin (fed state — ↓blood glucose, glycogenesis, FA synthesis), glucagon (fasting — ↑blood glucose, glycogenolysis, gluconeogenesis), somatostatin
- Gonads: estrogen, progesterone, testosterone
- Pineal: melatonin (circadian rhythm)
- Negative feedback loops (most hormones) vs positive feedback (oxytocin during labor, LH surge)

#### 3B — Organ System Structure and Function (Bio)

RESPIRATORY SYSTEM:
- Anatomy: nasal cavity → pharynx → larynx → trachea → bronchi → bronchioles → alveoli
- Ventilation: diaphragm contracts → intrathoracic pressure drops → air flows in (Boyle's law)
- Lung volumes: tidal, residual, vital capacity, total lung capacity
- Gas exchange: driven by partial pressure gradients (Dalton's law)
- O₂-Hemoglobin dissociation curve: ⭐
  - Sigmoidal shape (cooperative binding)
  - RIGHT SHIFT = ↑O₂ unloading (Bohr effect): ↑temp, ↑CO₂, ↓pH, ↑2,3-BPG
  - LEFT SHIFT = ↑O₂ affinity: opposite conditions, fetal Hb, CO poisoning
- CO₂ transport: dissolved (7%), carbaminohemoglobin (23%), bicarbonate (70%)
  - Carbonic anhydrase: CO₂ + H₂O ↔ H₂CO₃ ↔ H⁺ + HCO₃⁻

CARDIOVASCULAR SYSTEM: ⭐
- Heart: 4 chambers, valves (AV: tricuspid, mitral; semilunar: pulmonary, aortic)
- Cardiac cycle: systole (contraction) and diastole (relaxation)
- Cardiac output: CO = HR × SV
- Blood pressure: systolic/diastolic, regulated by baroreceptors, RAAS, ANP
- Vessel types: arteries (thick, elastic) → arterioles (resistance vessels) → capillaries (exchange) → venules → veins (valves, capacitance vessels)
- Blood: plasma (55%), RBCs (no nucleus, biconcave, 120-day lifespan), WBCs, platelets
- Coagulation cascade, fibrin clot
- Blood types: ABO (codominant I^A, I^B; recessive i) + Rh factor
- Fetal circulation: umbilical vein (oxygenated from placenta), ductus arteriosus (bypasses lungs), foramen ovale (R→L atrial shunt)

IMMUNE SYSTEM: ⭐
- Innate: physical barriers (skin, mucus, low pH, lysozyme), phagocytes (neutrophils — first responders, macrophages, dendritic cells), complement system (MAC), interferons (antiviral), NK cells, inflammation (vasodilation, increased permeability, chemotaxis), fever
- Adaptive — Humoral: B cells → plasma cells (secrete antibodies) + memory B cells
  - Antibody structure: variable region (antigen binding), constant region (effector function)
  - Classes: IgG (most abundant, crosses placenta), IgM (first response, pentamer), IgA (mucosal), IgE (allergies, parasites), IgD (B cell surface)
  - Clonal selection, affinity maturation, class switching
- Adaptive — Cell-mediated: T cells (mature in thymus)
  - CD4⁺ helper T cells: recognize MHC II (on APCs), activate B cells and macrophages
  - CD8⁺ cytotoxic T cells: recognize MHC I (on all nucleated cells), kill infected/cancer cells
  - MHC I: endogenous antigen presentation (all nucleated cells → CD8)
  - MHC II: exogenous antigen presentation (APCs → CD4) ⭐
- Active immunity (your own immune response, memory) vs passive (antibodies transferred, no memory)
- Vaccines: active immunity via weakened/inactivated pathogen
- Autoimmune diseases, immunodeficiency, hypersensitivity (Types I-IV)

DIGESTIVE SYSTEM:
- Mouth: salivary amylase (starch→maltose), lingual lipase
- Esophagus: peristalsis, LES
- Stomach: HCl (parietal cells), pepsinogen→pepsin (chief cells), mucus cells, gastrin (hormone)
- Small intestine: duodenum (receives bile + pancreatic juice), jejunum (main absorption), ileum (B12, bile salt reabsorption)
- Pancreas: trypsinogen→trypsin (activated by enterokinase), chymotrypsin, carboxypeptidase, pancreatic lipase, amylase, bicarbonate
- Liver: bile production (cholesterol derivative, emulsifies fat), detoxification, urea cycle, gluconeogenesis, plasma proteins, first-pass metabolism
- Gallbladder: stores and concentrates bile
- Large intestine: water absorption, bacterial flora (K, biotin production)
- Absorption: villi/microvilli (↑surface area), glucose/AAs via Na⁺ co-transport (secondary active), fats → chylomicrons → lymphatics

EXCRETORY/RENAL SYSTEM: ⭐
- Kidney: cortex, medulla, pelvis → ureter → bladder → urethra
- Nephron: functional unit
  - Glomerulus/Bowman's capsule: filtration (GFR ~120 mL/min, filtered by size and charge)
  - PCT: bulk reabsorption (glucose, AAs, Na⁺, water, HCO₃⁻), secretion (H⁺, drugs)
  - Loop of Henle: descending (water permeable, thin), ascending (Na⁺/K⁺/Cl⁻ pump, impermeable to water)
  - DCT: fine-tuning (aldosterone → Na⁺ reabsorb, PTH → Ca²⁺ reabsorb)
  - Collecting duct: ADH → aquaporin insertion → water reabsorption
- Countercurrent multiplier: creates medullary osmotic gradient (300→1200 mOsm)
- RAAS: ↓BP → renin → angiotensin I → ACE (lungs) → angiotensin II → vasoconstriction + aldosterone release → Na⁺/H₂O retention → ↑BP
- ANP: released by atria when stretched, opposes RAAS
- Acid-base balance: H⁺ secretion, HCO₃⁻ reabsorption
- Clearance: C = (U×V)/P

MUSCULOSKELETAL:
- Skeletal muscle: sarcomere structure (Z-line to Z-line), actin (thin filaments + tropomyosin + troponin), myosin (thick filaments, cross-bridges)
- Sliding filament theory: Ca²⁺ released from SR → binds troponin → tropomyosin moves → myosin binds actin → power stroke (ATP hydrolysis)
- Excitation-contraction coupling: AP → T-tubules → SR Ca²⁺ release
- Muscle fiber types: slow-twitch (type I, oxidative, endurance), fast-twitch (type II, glycolytic, power)
- Smooth muscle: involuntary, single-unit vs multi-unit, calmodulin-mediated
- Cardiac muscle: intercalated discs (gap junctions), autorhythmic, striated
- Bone: compact (osteons/Haversian systems) vs spongy (trabecular)
- Bone cells: osteoblasts (build), osteoclasts (resorb), osteocytes (maintain)
- Endochondral ossification (cartilage → bone) vs intramembranous (direct)

REPRODUCTIVE AND DEVELOPMENT:
- Male: testes → seminiferous tubules (spermatogenesis), Sertoli cells (support), Leydig cells (testosterone)
- Female: ovaries → oogenesis, menstrual cycle (follicular phase → ovulation → luteal phase)
- Hormonal control: GnRH → FSH/LH
- Embryology: fertilization → cleavage → morula → blastula → gastrulation → neurulation → organogenesis
- Germ layers: ectoderm (skin, nervous system), mesoderm (muscle, bone, blood, kidneys), endoderm (gut lining, lungs, liver, pancreas)

INTEGUMENTARY: skin layers (epidermis, dermis, hypodermis), thermoregulation
LYMPHATIC: lymph, lymph nodes, spleen (blood filter), thymus (T cell maturation)

#### 6A — Sensing the Environment

DRUGS AND ADDICTION:
- Stimulants: cocaine, amphetamines (↑dopamine/NE)

- Cannabis

MEMORY: ⭐

- Storage: sensory (iconic ~0.5s, echoic ~3-4s), short-term/working (7±2 items, ~30s, chunking), long-term:
  - Explicit/declarative: episodic (personal events) + semantic (facts)
  - Implicit/procedural: skills, conditioning
- Retrieval: recall vs recognition vs relearning, retrieval cues, context-dependent, state-dependent
- Forgetting: decay, proactive interference (old blocks new), retroactive interference (new blocks old), source monitoring errors, misinformation effect (Loftus)
- Long-term potentiation (LTP), synaptic plasticity
- Memory disorders: Alzheimer's (acetylcholine, amyloid plaques), Korsakoff's (thiamine/B1 deficiency, alcoholism)

LANGUAGE:
- Broca's area: speech production (damage → expressive/non-fluent aphasia)

## RESEARCH METHODS AND BIOSTATISTICS ⭐⚠️

Cross-cutting: tested in all science sections, weighted most heavily in PS (~20%). ONE OF THE MOST COMMONLY UNDERPREPARED AREAS.

### Study Design

EXPERIMENTAL:
- Randomized controlled trial (RCT) — gold standard for causation
- Quasi-experimental (no random assignment)
- Factorial designs

OBSERVATIONAL:
- Cross-sectional: snapshot at one time point, measures prevalence
- Longitudinal/prospective cohort: follows group over time, measures incidence, can determine relative risk
- Retrospective: looks backward
- Case-control: starts with outcome (cases vs controls), looks back at exposure, measures odds ratio
- Naturalistic observation
- Case studies
- Survey methods (Likert scale, open/closed questions)

META-ANALYSIS: statistical combination of multiple studies
TWIN STUDIES / ADOPTION STUDIES: heritability estimates

### Key Concepts

VARIABLES:
- Independent (manipulated), dependent (measured)
- Confounding (alternative explanation), control
- Moderating (affects strength of relationship between IV and DV)
- Mediating (explains HOW IV affects DV, intervening mechanism)
- Operational definitions

CONTROLS:
- Positive control (known to work), negative control (known not to work)
- Placebo group, sham procedures
- Randomization
- Blinding: single (participant), double (participant + researcher), triple (+ data analyst)
- Counterbalancing (order effects)

### Validity and Reliability

STUDY VALIDITY:
- Internal: confidence in cause-effect (threats: confounds, history, maturation, testing effects, attrition)
- External: generalizability to other populations/settings
- Ecological: real-world relevance

MEASUREMENT VALIDITY:
- Face: appears to measure what it claims
- Content: covers full range of construct
- Construct: measures the theoretical construct
- Criterion: predicts related outcomes (predictive = future, concurrent = present)

RELIABILITY:
- Test-retest: consistency over time
- Inter-rater: consistency between observers
- Internal consistency: Cronbach's alpha (items measure same construct)
- Parallel forms: equivalent test versions

### Sampling Methods

PROBABILITY (generalizable):
- Simple random, stratified (subgroups proportionally represented), cluster (random groups), systematic (every nth)

NON-PROBABILITY (not generalizable):
- Convenience (whoever is available), snowball (referrals), purposive (researcher selects), quota

### Statistics

DESCRIPTIVE:
- Central tendency: mean, median (resistant to outliers), mode
- Spread: range, IQR, variance, standard deviation
- Normal distribution: 68-95-99.7 rule (±1,2,3 SD) ⭐
- Skew: positive (tail right, mean > median > mode), negative (tail left, mean < median < mode)

INFERENTIAL: ⭐
- Null hypothesis (H₀: no effect) vs alternative (H₁: effect exists)
- p-value: probability of results given H₀ is true
- Alpha level: significance threshold (usually 0.05)
- Type I error (false positive, α): reject H₀ when it's true
- Type II error (false negative, β): fail to reject H₀ when it's false
- Statistical power (1−β): probability of correctly detecting a real effect
  - Increased by: larger sample size, larger effect size, higher α
- Confidence intervals: range likely containing true parameter
- Effect size (Cohen's d): magnitude of difference

STATISTICAL TESTS: ⚠️
- t-test: compare means of 2 groups
- ANOVA: compare means of 3+ groups
- Chi-square: test association between categorical variables
- Correlation (Pearson's r): strength/direction of linear relationship (−1 to +1)
- Regression: predict DV from IV(s)
- CORRELATION ≠ CAUSATION (third variable problem)

### Epidemiological Measures ⚠️

- Incidence: NEW cases in a time period (rate)
- Prevalence: ALL cases at a point in time (proportion) — prevalence = incidence × duration
- Relative risk (RR): risk in exposed / risk in unexposed (used in cohort studies)
- Odds ratio (OR): odds of exposure in cases / odds in controls (used in case-control)
- Number needed to treat (NNT): 1/absolute risk reduction
- Sensitivity: true positive rate (proportion of actual positives correctly identified) ⭐
- Specificity: true negative rate (proportion of actual negatives correctly identified) ⭐
- Positive predictive value (PPV): probability that positive test = truly positive
- Negative predictive value (NPV): probability that negative test = truly negative
- Sensitivity/specificity are intrinsic to the test; PPV/NPV depend on prevalence

### Bias Taxonomy

RESEARCH BIASES:
- Selection bias, sampling bias
- Attrition/dropout bias
- Volunteer/self-selection bias
- Lead-time bias
- Publication bias

PARTICIPANT BIASES:
- Hawthorne effect (behavior changes when observed) ⭐
- Placebo/nocebo effect
- Social desirability bias
- Demand characteristics
- Recall bias
- Acquiescence bias (tendency to agree)

RESEARCHER BIASES:
- Experimenter expectancy (Rosenthal/Pygmalion effect) ⭐
- Observer bias
- Confirmation bias

### Ethics

- Informed consent
- IRB (Institutional Review Board)
- IACUC (animal research)
- Deception: must be justified, requires debriefing
- Right to withdraw at any time
- Confidentiality
- Minimal risk
- Belmont Report principles: respect for persons (autonomy), beneficence (do good/minimize harm), justice (fair distribution of benefits/burdens)
- Vulnerable populations: extra protections

## LAB TECHNIQUES MASTER LIST

These techniques appear in passage-based questions across BB and CP. Students consistently underestimate their frequency.

### Separation and Purification

| Technique | Principle | When to Use |
|-----------|-----------|-------------|
| SDS-PAGE | Separates proteins by molecular weight (SDS denatures + uniform negative charge) | Determine protein size, purity |
| Native PAGE | Separates by charge, size, and shape (no denaturation) | Preserve protein activity/structure |
| Isoelectric focusing | Separates by isoelectric point (pI) | Determine pI |
| 2D gel | IEF (1st) + SDS-PAGE (2nd) | Resolve complex protein mixtures |
| Agarose gel electrophoresis | Separates DNA/RNA by size (smaller = farther) | DNA fragment analysis |
| Size-exclusion chromatography | Larger molecules elute FIRST (don't enter pores) | Separate by size, desalting |
| Ion-exchange chromatography | Binds by charge; elute by changing pH or salt | Separate by charge |
| Affinity chromatography | Binds specific ligand; elute by competitor or pH | Highest purity, specific target |
| TLC | Compound moves up plate; Rf = distance compound/distance solvent | Quick reaction monitoring |
| Column chromatography | Gravity-driven separation through stationary phase | Bulk purification |
| HPLC | High-pressure column chromatography | High-resolution separation |
| Gas chromatography | Volatile compounds separated in gas phase | Small volatile molecules |
| Distillation | Separates by boiling point differences | Purify liquids |
| Extraction | Separates by solubility in different solvents | Separate organic/aqueous |
| Centrifugation | Separates by density/size; differential = sequential spins | Separate cell components |
| Dialysis | Semipermeable membrane separates by molecular size | Remove small molecules, buffer exchange |
| Recrystallization | Dissolve hot, crystallize cold | Purify solids |

### Molecular Biology Techniques

| Technique | What It Does | Key Details |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| PCR | Amplifies specific DNA sequence | 3 steps per cycle: denature (95°C), anneal (55-65°C), extend (72°C). Taq polymerase. Exponential: 2ⁿ copies after n cycles |
| RT-PCR | Detects RNA via cDNA | Reverse transcriptase → cDNA, then PCR |
| qPCR | Quantifies DNA in real-time | Fluorescent reporters, threshold cycle (Ct) |
| Southern blot | Detects specific DNA sequence | Gel → membrane → DNA probe |
| Northern blot | Detects specific RNA | Gel → membrane → RNA probe |
| Western blot | Detects specific protein | SDS-PAGE → membrane → antibody detection |
| ELISA | Quantifies antigen or antibody | Enzyme-labeled detection. Direct, indirect, sandwich formats |
| Sanger sequencing | Determines DNA sequence | ddNTPs cause chain termination at each base |
| Restriction enzymes | Cut DNA at specific palindromic sequences | Sticky ends (overhangs) vs blunt ends |
| Gene cloning | Insert gene into vector for amplification | Restriction enzymes + ligase + vector + transformation |
| Microarrays | Genome-wide gene expression | Thousands of probes, fluorescent hybridization |
| CRISPR-Cas9 | Gene editing | Guide RNA directs Cas9 nuclease to target sequence |
| Flow cytometry/FACS | Sort cells by fluorescence | Single-cell analysis, surface marker identification |
| Immunoprecipitation | Pull down specific protein + binding partners | Antibody-bead conjugate |
| ChIP | Identify DNA-protein interactions | Crosslink → fragment → immunoprecipitate → sequence |
| Edman degradation | Sequence protein N-terminus | Sequential amino acid removal and identification |
| Bradford assay | Quantify total protein | Coomassie dye binding, measure absorbance |
| Karyotyping | Visualize chromosomes | Identify chromosomal abnormalities |
| FISH | Locate specific DNA sequence on chromosome | Fluorescent probe hybridization |
| Radioactive labeling | Track molecules through pathways | ³H, ¹⁴C, ³²P, ³⁵S + autoradiography |
| X-ray crystallography | Determine 3D protein structure | Requires crystallized protein |

### Spectroscopy Summary

| Method | What It Reveals | Key Details |
|--------|----------------|-------------|
| IR | Functional groups present | O-H broad, C=O sharp ~1700, N-H ~3300 |
| ¹H NMR | Hydrogen environments | Chemical shift, splitting (n+1), integration |
| ¹³C NMR | Carbon environments | No splitting, # unique carbons |
| Mass Spec | Molecular weight, fragments | M⁺ peak, common losses (15, 18, 28, 29) |
| UV-Vis | Conjugation, concentration | Beer-Lambert: A=εlc |
