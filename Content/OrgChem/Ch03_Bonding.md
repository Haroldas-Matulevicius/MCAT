ATOMIC ORBITALS:
- s spherical, p dumbbell, d cloverleaf
- For orgo: s and p of C, N, O, halogens
- Bonding via orbital overlap

MOLECULAR ORBITAL (MO) THEORY:
- 2 atomic orbitals → 2 MOs (bonding lower, antibonding* higher)
- Bond order = (bonding e- - antibonding e-)/2
- BO > 0 → bond exists; BO = 0 → no bond (He2 doesn't exist)
- O2: 2 unpaired e- in π* → paramagnetic, diradical (Lewis can't show)
- Conjugated systems: continuous π above/below plane
- Underlies UV-Vis (electronic transitions between MOs)

HYBRIDIZATION:
- # electron groups = # hybrid orbitals
- 2 groups → sp (1s + 1p), linear, 180°, up to 2π
- 3 groups → sp² (1s + 2p), trigonal planar, 120°, up to 1π
- 4 groups → sp³ (1s + 3p), tetrahedral, 109.5°, no π
- Unhybridized p orbitals form π bonds

SIGMA vs PI BONDS:
- σ: head-on overlap (along axis), 1 per bond, free rotation, stronger; from hybrid orbitals
- π: lateral overlap (above/below axis), restricted rotation, weaker; from unhybridized p
- Single = 1σ; double = 1σ+1π; triple = 1σ+2π
- π rotation barrier ~260 kJ/mol → E/Z isomerism

HYBRIDIZATION IN FUNCTIONAL GROUPS:
- Alkane C-C: sp³
- Alkene C=C: sp²
- Alkyne C≡C: sp
- Carbonyl C=O: sp²
- Aromatic ring C: sp² (delocalized π)
- Nitrile C≡N: sp (linear)
- Carbocation R3C+: sp² (empty p ⊥ plane)
- Carbanion R3C:-: sp³ usually (sp² if stabilized by adjacent π)

NITROGEN/OXYGEN HYBRIDIZATION (count groups):
- Amine N (R-NH2): sp³ (3 bonds + 1 LP = 4 groups)
- Amide N: sp² (LP delocalized into C=O π) → planar, partial double-bond character
- Water O: sp³ (2 bonds + 2 LP)
- Carbonyl O: sp²

AROMATICITY (Hückel):
1. Planar cyclic
2. Continuous p orbitals (every ring atom sp²)
3. (4n+2) π electrons
- Benzene: 6 π, n=1 → aromatic, ~150 kJ/mol stability gain
- Antiaromatic: 4n π electrons, planar (cyclobutadiene) — unstable
- Non-aromatic: doesn't satisfy criteria (cyclohexane sp³ carbons)
- Aromatics undergo SUBSTITUTION not addition (preserve aromaticity)
