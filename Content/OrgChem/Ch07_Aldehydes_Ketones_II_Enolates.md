# CP Orgo Chapter 7 — Aldehydes & Ketones II (Enolates)

Scope: α-H acidity; keto-enol tautomerism; enolate formation; aldol condensation; retro-aldol; α-alkylation; α,β-unsaturated carbonyl (Michael/conjugate addition).

AAMC FC mapping: FC5D (enolate chem)

Kaplan: Organic Chemistry Ch 7

---

α-HYDROGEN ACIDITY:
- Ketone α-H: pKa ~20
- Aldehyde α-H: pKa ~17
- Ester α-H: pKa ~25
- 1,3-dicarbonyl: pKa ~5-13 (much more acidic, doubly resonance-stabilized)

WHY α-H ACIDIC:
- Conjugate base (enolate) resonance-stabilized
- Major resonance contributor: negative charge on O (more EN)
- 1,3-dicarbonyl: negative delocalized over 2 oxygens

KETO-ENOL TAUTOMERISM:
- Keto: C=O + α-CH ↔ Enol: C=C + O-H (proton migration)
- Tautomers ≠ resonance (atoms move)
- Keto strongly favored (~99.99%) for normal carbonyls
- Enol favored: 1,3-dicarbonyls (intramol H-bond), phenol (aromaticity)
- Catalyzed by acid OR base

ENOLATE FORMATION:
- Need base with conjugate acid pKa > α-H pKa
- NaOH (pKa H2O 15.7): partial enolate
- LDA (pKa ~36): clean, quantitative enolate (bulky, doesn't add to carbonyl)
- NaH (pKa H2 36): irreversible

KINETIC vs THERMODYNAMIC ENOLATE:
- Kinetic: less substituted C=C; LDA, low T (-78°C)
- Thermodynamic: more substituted C=C, more stable; weaker base, higher T

ALDOL CONDENSATION (mechanism):
1. Base deprotonates α-H → enolate
2. Enolate attacks another carbonyl C
3. Alkoxide tetrahedral intermediate
4. Protonation → β-hydroxy carbonyl ("aldol product")
5. Heat → dehydration → α,β-unsaturated carbonyl ("aldol condensation product", enone)
- Crossed/mixed aldol: messy (4 products) unless one has no α-H (formaldehyde, benzaldehyde) or use directed LDA strategy

RETRO-ALDOL:
- β-hydroxy carbonyl → 2 smaller carbonyls
- BIOLOGICAL: fructose-1,6-bisphosphate → DHAP + G3P via aldolase (glycolysis)
- Mechanism: aldolase forms Schiff base w/ active-site Lys + C2 carbonyl of F1,6BP, activating retro-aldol

α-ALKYLATION:
- Enolate + R-X → α-alkylated carbonyl (SN2 at alkyl halide)
- Best with 1° alkyl halides
- Acetoacetic ester synthesis: makes methyl ketones
- Malonic ester synthesis: makes carb acids

α,β-UNSATURATED CARBONYL (enone):
- 1,2-addition: nuc to carbonyl C (RMgX, NaBH4 cold, LAH)
- 1,4-addition (Michael/conjugate): soft nuc to β-C (cuprates R2CuLi, enolates, amines, thiols)
- Michael additions in biosynthesis
