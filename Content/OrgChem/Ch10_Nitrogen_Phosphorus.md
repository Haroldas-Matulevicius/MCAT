AMINES:
- 1° R-NH2, 2° R2NH, 3° R3N, 4° R4N+ (charged)
- N lone pair → basic (R-NH3+ pKa ~10)
- sp3 in amine; sp2 in amide (LP delocalized into C=O)
- Aromatic amine (aniline pKa ~4.6) less basic (LP into ring)
- Reactions: imine/enamine (Ch06), amide formation (Ch09), SN2 alkylation, Hofmann elimination

AMINO ACID STRUCTURE:
- α-C bonded to: -NH2, -COOH, H, R group
- α-C chiral except glycine (R = H)
- All biological AAs = L-configuration (S for most; cysteine R due to S priority)

ZWITTERION:
- Physiological pH 7.4: -NH3+ and -COO- both ionized; net charge = 0
- Carboxyl pKa ~2 (deprotonated at 7.4); amino pKa ~9 (protonated at 7.4)

ISOELECTRIC POINT (pI):
- No ionizable side chain: pI = (pKa1 + pKa2)/2
- Acidic side chain (Asp, Glu): pI = (pKa1 + pKaR)/2 (avg of two lowest)
- Basic side chain (Lys, Arg, His): pI = (pKa2 + pKaR)/2 (avg of two highest)
- Rule: avg the 2 pKas flanking the zero-charge form

ELECTROPHORESIS:
- pH < pI: net + → cathode
- pH > pI: net - → anode
- pH = pI: no migration

PEPTIDE BOND:
- Formation: condensation (-H2O) between carboxyl + amino
- ~40% double bond character (N lone pair → C=O resonance)
- PLANAR (6 atoms: Cα-C-O-N-H-Cα)
- Trans favored (proline can be cis)
- ω angle locked ~180°; φ, ψ free
- NOT a true double bond, just partial character

PEPTIDE HYDROLYSIS:
- Reverse of condensation; regenerates free -NH2 + -COOH
- Catalyzed in vivo by proteases (trypsin, chymotrypsin, pepsin)
- Acid: 6M HCl, 110°C, 24h breaks ALL peptide bonds (AA analysis)
- Base: works but can racemize

STRECKER SYNTHESIS (α-amino acid):
- R-CHO + NH3 → imine
- + HCN → α-aminonitrile (R-CH(NH2)(CN))
- + H3O+/heat → α-amino acid (racemic)

GABRIEL SYNTHESIS (primary amine):
1. Phthalimide (NH pKa ~8) + KOH → phthalimide anion
2. + R-X (SN2) → N-alkyl phthalimide
3. + N2H4 (or harsh acid/base) hydrolysis → primary amine
- For α-AA: use diethyl bromomalonate as R-X
- Avoids polyalkylation (vs direct NH3 alkylation)

PHOSPHORUS:

PHOSPHATE GROUP:
- PO4^3-: tetrahedral, P sp3, one P=O + three P-O (resonance equalizes 4 O's)
- H3PO4 pKas: 2.1, 7.2, 12.4 (triprotic)
- Physiological 7.4: H2PO4-/HPO4^2- ~1:1 (pH 7.2)

PHOSPHATE ESTERS:
- R-O-PO(OH)2; biologically negatively charged
- Examples: G6P, glycerol-3-P, phospho-AAs
- Trap molecules in cells (charge); kinase/phosphatase regulation

PHOSPHOANHYDRIDES (ATP):
- Adenine + ribose + 3 phosphates (α, β, γ)
- α-β and β-γ bonds = phosphoanhydrides
- ATP + H2O → ADP + Pi: ΔG° ≈ -30.5 kJ/mol
- ATP + H2O → AMP + PPi: ΔG° ≈ -45 kJ/mol
- Why exergonic: charge repulsion relief, resonance, hydration, entropy

PHOSPHODIESTER (DNA/RNA):
- Phosphate connects 3'-OH to 5'-OH (two ester linkages, one phosphate)
- Backbone negative at physiological pH
- Migrates to anode in gel electrophoresis
- Stabilizes histone interaction (compaction)

PHOSPHOLIPIDS:
- Glycerol + 2 fatty acids + phosphate + headgroup (choline, ethanolamine, serine, inositol)
- Phosphodiester linkage
- Amphipathic → membrane bilayer
