## CHAPTER 1: Biology of Behavior

AAMC FC mapping: 6A (parts of nervous system) + 7A (biological bases of behavior)

HISTORY:
- Phrenology (Gall) → discredited but introduced localization of function
- Flourens — extirpation/ablation in animals (first to experimentally prove localization)
- Wundt (1879) — first psych lab (Leipzig); structuralism via introspection; "father of experimental psychology"
- William James — functionalism (US); *Principles of Psychology* (1890); stream of consciousness
- Dewey — extension to education; 1896 reflex arc paper = formal start of US functionalism
- Broca — speech production localized (left frontal)
- Helmholtz — speed of nerve impulse (psychology as quantifiable)
- Sherrington — inferred synapses

NERVOUS SYSTEM ORGANIZATION:
- CNS (brain + spinal cord) vs PNS (everything else)
- PNS: somatic (voluntary, skeletal muscle) vs autonomic
- ANS: sympathetic (fight/flight, NE) vs parasympathetic (rest/digest, ACh)
- Vagus nerve (CN X) — major parasympathetic nerve, ~75% of parasympathetic fibers

BRAIN ORGANIZATION:
- Hindbrain: medulla (vital reflexes — breathing, HR, BP, vomiting), pons (sensory/motor relay), cerebellum (coordination, balance, motor learning), reticular formation (arousal, sleep-wake)
- Midbrain: superior colliculi (visual reflexes), inferior colliculi (auditory reflexes), substantia nigra (dopamine; Parkinson's)
- Forebrain: thalamus, hypothalamus, limbic, basal ganglia, cerebrum

FOREBRAIN PARTS:
- Thalamus — sensory relay (all senses except olfaction)
- Hypothalamus — homeostasis (Four F's: Feeding, Fighting, Fleeing, Sex)
  - LH = hunger ("Lateral = Lunch")
  - VMH = satiety ("Ventromedial = Very full")
  - SCN = circadian pacemaker
- Limbic system — emotion + memory
  - Amygdala (fear, emotional memory)
  - Hippocampus (new explicit memory; HM case)
  - Fornix (white matter tract)
  - Septal nuclei (pleasure, addiction)
  - Cingulate cortex (emotion regulation, attention)
- Basal ganglia — caudate, putamen, globus pallidus (smooth voluntary movement; Parkinson's, Huntington's)
- Cerebrum — 4 lobes:
  - Frontal: primary motor cortex, executive function, Broca's area (left, speech production), personality
  - Parietal: primary somatosensory cortex, spatial reasoning
  - Temporal: primary auditory cortex, Wernicke's area (left, comprehension), memory
  - Occipital: primary visual cortex (V1)
- Aphasias: Broca's (expressive, "Broken speech"), Wernicke's (receptive, "Wordy but Wrong"), conduction (arcuate fasciculus damage)
- Lateralization: left = language/logic; right = spatial/music/face/emotion
- Corpus callosum — connects hemispheres
- Split-brain (Sperry) — severed corpus callosum studies

INFLUENCES ON BEHAVIOR:
- Heritability — proportion of trait variation due to genes
- Twin studies (MZ vs DZ), adoption studies, family studies
- Gene-environment interaction (epigenetics)
- Endocrine — hypothalamus → pituitary → other glands
- Anterior pituitary mnemonic FLAT PEG: FSH, LH, ACTH, TSH, Prolactin, Endorphins, GH
- Posterior pituitary: oxytocin (bonding, contractions, milk letdown), ADH (water retention)
- HPA axis: hypothalamus → CRH → ant. pituitary → ACTH → adrenal cortex → cortisol
- Adrenal cortex: cortisol, aldosterone, sex hormones; medulla: epinephrine, NE
- Thyroid: T3/T4 (metabolism), calcitonin
- Gonads: testosterone (testes), estrogen/progesterone (ovaries)
- Pineal: melatonin (circadian)

DEVELOPMENT:
- Neural tube → 3 primary vesicles → 5 secondary vesicles → adult brain
- Neural tube defects: spina bifida, anencephaly (folic acid prevents)
- Infant reflexes: rooting, sucking, Moro, Babinski, grasp (palmar)
- Fixed action patterns — innate stereotyped behavior triggered by sign stimulus
- Critical period (narrow, must occur) vs sensitive period (broader, optimal)
- Imprinting (Lorenz) — geese to first moving object (critical period)

METHODS TO STUDY BRAIN:
- EEG — electrical, poor spatial / excellent temporal (ms)
- CT — structure, X-ray
- MRI — structure, magnetic, excellent spatial
- fMRI — function (blood O2), good spatial / moderate temporal
- PET — function (metabolism), moderate spatial / poor temporal
- Lesion studies — behavior after damage
- Tradeoff: high temporal sacrifices spatial and vice versa

NEURONS AND GLIA:
- Neuron: dendrites → soma → axon hillock → axon (myelinated) → terminal
- Glia:
  - Astrocytes (most common; BBB, K+ buffering)
  - Oligodendrocytes (CNS myelination, multiple axons)
  - Schwann cells (PNS myelination, one segment)
  - Microglia (immune, phagocytosis)
  - Ependymal (line ventricles, produce CSF)

ACTION POTENTIALS:
- Resting ~-70 mV; Na+/K+ pump (3 Na+ out / 2 K+ in per ATP)
- Depolarization: Na+ in (voltage-gated channels)
- Repolarization: K+ out
- Hyperpolarization briefly
- Refractory periods: absolute (Na+ inactivated) vs relative

SYNAPTIC TRANSMISSION:
- AP at terminal → Ca2+ influx → vesicle fusion → NT release
- EPSP (depolarizing) vs IPSP (hyperpolarizing)
- Summation at axon hillock (temporal vs spatial)
- NT cleared by reuptake, enzymatic degradation, or diffusion

NEUROTRANSMITTERS (high yield):
- ACh — memory, muscle, parasympathetic; Alzheimer's (low)
- Dopamine — reward, movement; schizophrenia (high mesolimbic), Parkinson's (low nigrostriatal)
- NE — alertness, arousal, fight/flight; depression (low), anxiety (high)
- Serotonin — mood, sleep, appetite; depression (low), OCD
- GABA — main inhibitory; anxiety (low); enhanced by alcohol/benzos
- Glutamate — main excitatory; LTP, learning; NMDA hypofunction in schizophrenia
- Endorphins — pain reduction; opioids mimic

## CHAPTER 2: Sensation and Perception

AAMC FC mapping: 6A

SENSATION VS PERCEPTION:
- Sensation = detection (transduction)
- Perception = interpretation
- Absolute threshold (50% detection)
- Difference threshold = JND
- Weber's law: ΔI/I = k (constant ratio)
- Subliminal stimuli (below absolute threshold)
- Signal Detection Theory (SDT) 2x2: hit, miss, false alarm, correct rejection
  - Sensitivity vs criterion (response bias)
- Sensory adaptation (decreased firing with constant stimulus)
- Pain does NOT adapt (exception)

VISION:
- Anatomy: cornea (most refraction) → pupil/iris → lens (accommodation) → retina
- Rods: dim light, periphery, rhodopsin, scotopic, no color
- Cones: bright light, fovea (highest acuity, 1:1 ganglion ratio), photopic, color
- Trichromatic (Young-Helmholtz, retinal level, R/G/B cones)
- Opponent-process (Hering, ganglion/LGN, R-G/B-Y/B-W)
- Pathway: retina → optic nerve → optic chiasm → LGN (thalamus) → V1 (occipital)
- Feature detectors (Hubel & Wiesel)
- Parallel processing (color, motion, form, depth simultaneously)

HEARING AND VESTIBULAR:
- Outer (pinna, canal, tympanic membrane)
- Middle (ossicles: malleus, incus, stapes; oval window)
- Inner (cochlea, basilar membrane, hair cells)
- Pathway through MGN of thalamus to auditory cortex (temporal lobe)
- Place theory: high freq → base, low freq → apex
- Frequency theory + volley principle (low frequencies)
- Conductive vs sensorineural hearing loss
- Vestibular: semicircular canals (rotation, 3 planes), otolith organs (utricle/saccule, linear/gravity)

OTHER SENSES:
- Somatosensation receptors:
  - Meissner (light touch)
  - Pacinian (deep pressure/vibration)
  - Merkel (sustained pressure)
  - Free nerve endings (pain/temp)
- Gate control theory of pain (Melzack & Wall) — large fibers close gate; endorphins close gate
- Taste: 5 basics (sweet, sour, salty, bitter, umami); CN VII, IX, X
- Olfaction: bypasses thalamus → directly to cortex/limbic (emotional memory connection)

PERCEPTION:
- Bottom-up (data-driven) vs top-down (knowledge-driven)
- Gestalt principles: proximity, similarity, closure, continuity, figure-ground, common fate
- Depth cues:
  - Binocular: convergence, retinal disparity
  - Monocular: relative size, interposition, linear perspective, texture gradient, motion parallax, aerial perspective

## CHAPTER 3: Learning, Memory, and Behavior

AAMC FC mapping: 6B (memory) + 7C (learning)

LEARNING:
- Habituation (decreased response to repeated stimulus)
- Dishabituation (recovery with new stimulus)
- Sensitization (increased response after intense event)

CLASSICAL CONDITIONING (Pavlov):
- UCS → UCR (unlearned)
- CS → CR (learned through pairing)
- Acquisition (CS before UCS)
- Extinction (CS without UCS)
- Spontaneous recovery
- Generalization, discrimination
- Higher-order conditioning
- Garcia effect (taste aversion, biological preparedness, one-trial learning)

OPERANT CONDITIONING (Skinner):
- Pos reinforcement (+ stim, ↑ behavior)
- Neg reinforcement (- stim, ↑ behavior)
- Pos punishment (+ stim, ↓ behavior)
- Neg punishment (- stim, ↓ behavior)
- Schedules:
  - Fixed-ratio: high rate, post-reinforcement pause
  - Variable-ratio: highest rate, most resistant to extinction (gambling)
  - Fixed-interval: scallop pattern
  - Variable-interval: slow steady
- Shaping (successive approximations)
- Escape vs avoidance learning
- Instinctive drift

OBSERVATIONAL LEARNING (Bandura):
- Bobo doll experiment
- Vicarious reinforcement / punishment
- Mirror neurons

MEMORY — ENCODING:
- Automatic vs effortful
- Levels of processing (shallow → deep)
- Elaborative rehearsal > maintenance rehearsal
- Self-reference effect
- Spacing effect (why Anki works)

MEMORY — STORAGE:
- Sensory: iconic ~0.5s, echoic ~3-4s
- STM/working: 7±2 (Miller), 30s, chunking
- Baddeley's working memory: phonological loop, visuospatial sketchpad, central executive, episodic buffer
- LTM:
  - Explicit (declarative): episodic + semantic — requires hippocampus
  - Implicit (non-declarative): procedural, priming, classical conditioning — does NOT require hippocampus

MEMORY — RETRIEVAL:
- Recall vs recognition vs relearning
- Retrieval cues
- Context-dependent, state-dependent, mood-congruent
- Primacy (LTM) vs recency (STM)

FORGETTING:
- Encoding failure
- Decay (Ebbinghaus forgetting curve)
- Proactive interference (old blocks new — "ProOld")
- Retroactive interference (new blocks old — "RetroNew")
- Source monitoring error
- Misinformation effect (Loftus, "smashed" vs "hit")
- Repressed/false memories

MEMORY DISORDERS:
- Alzheimer's: amyloid plaques, tau tangles, ACh deficit; cholinesterase inhibitors
- Korsakoff's: thiamine (B1) deficiency, alcoholism, confabulation

NEUROBIOLOGY OF LEARNING/MEMORY:
- Long-term potentiation (LTP) — NMDA receptors, glutamate
- Synaptic plasticity
- Hippocampus (consolidation, HM case)
- Amygdala (emotional memory)
- Cerebellum (procedural)
- Neural plasticity

## CHAPTER 4: Cognition, Consciousness, and Language

AAMC FC mapping: 6B

COGNITION:
- Information processing model (input → encoding → storage → retrieval → output)
- Concept formation, prototypes, exemplars, mental representations

COGNITIVE DEVELOPMENT (Piaget):
- Sensorimotor (0-2): object permanence (~8 mo)
- Preoperational (2-7): symbolic thought, egocentrism, centration, fails conservation
- Concrete operational (7-11): conservation, logical thought (concrete only)
- Formal operational (12+): abstract/hypothetical reasoning
- Vygotsky: ZPD, scaffolding, more knowledgeable other (sociocultural)

PROBLEM SOLVING / DECISION MAKING:
- Algorithms vs heuristics
- Availability heuristic (ease of recall)
- Representativeness heuristic (resembles prototype; conjunction fallacy)
- Anchoring bias
- Framing effect (90% survival vs 10% mortality)
- Confirmation bias
- Belief perseverance
- Overconfidence
- Hindsight bias
- Functional fixedness
- Mental set
- Dual-process theory (System 1 fast/intuitive vs System 2 slow/deliberate)
- Intuition (pattern matching)

INTELLIGENCE:
- Spearman's g
- Fluid (novel problem-solving) vs crystallized (knowledge) — Cattell
- Gardner multiple intelligences
- Sternberg triarchic (analytical, creative, practical)
- Emotional intelligence (Salovey & Mayer)
- IQ tests (mean 100, SD 15)
- Flynn effect (IQ rising over decades)

CONSCIOUSNESS — STATES:
- Alertness, sleep, dreaming, hypnosis, meditation, drug-altered

SLEEP STAGES:
- Awake/alert: beta
- Awake/relaxed: alpha
- N1: theta, hypnagogic hallucinations, light
- N2: theta + sleep spindles + K-complexes (most time spent here)
- N3: delta, slow-wave, growth hormone, parasomnias (sleepwalking, night terrors)
- REM: beta (paradoxical), atonia, vivid dreams, memory consolidation
- ~90 min cycles; more N3 early, more REM late
- Newborns ~50% REM; adults ~20-25%

CIRCADIAN:
- SCN of hypothalamus (gets light from retina) controls pineal → melatonin
- Disrupted by jet lag, shift work, blue light

SLEEP DISORDERS:
- Insomnia
- Narcolepsy (sudden REM onset, orexin/hypocretin deficiency)
- Sleep apnea (obstructive vs central)
- Parasomnias (N3): sleepwalking, night terrors

DREAMING THEORIES:
- Freud (wish fulfillment, manifest vs latent)
- Activation-synthesis (Hobson & McCarley)
- Information processing (consolidation)

HYPNOSIS / MEDITATION:
- Social influence theory (Spanos)
- Dissociation theory (Hilgard, "hidden observer")
- Meditation: focused attention; ↓ cortisol, ↓ BP

CONSCIOUSNESS-ALTERING DRUGS:
- Stimulants (cocaine, amphetamines, caffeine, nicotine) — ↑ DA/NE
- Depressants (alcohol, barbiturates, benzos) — ↑ GABA
- Opioids (heroin, morphine, oxycodone, fentanyl) — mimic endorphins
- Hallucinogens (LSD, psilocybin, mescaline) — serotonin (5-HT2A)
- Cannabis — cannabinoid receptors (CB1)
- Mesolimbic reward pathway: VTA → nucleus accumbens
- Tolerance, physical vs psychological dependence, withdrawal

ATTENTION:
- Selective (cocktail party effect)
- Divided
- Automaticity
- Inattentional blindness (gorilla experiment)
- Change blindness

LANGUAGE COMPONENTS:
- Phonology (sounds), morphology (meaning units), semantics (word/sentence meaning), syntax (grammar), pragmatics (social context)

LANGUAGE BRAIN AREAS:
- Broca's (frontal): production — Broca's aphasia (expressive/non-fluent, "Broken speech")
- Wernicke's (temporal): comprehension — Wernicke's aphasia (receptive/fluent, "Wordy but Wrong")
- Arcuate fasciculus damage — conduction aphasia (can't repeat)

LANGUAGE ACQUISITION STAGES:
- Cooing (birth-6 mo)
- Babbling (6-12 mo)
- One-word (12-18 mo)
- Telegraphic (18-24 mo)
- Full sentences (24+ mo)

LANGUAGE THEORIES:
- Nativism (Chomsky) — LAD, universal grammar
- Behaviorism (Skinner) — operant conditioning
- Social interactionist (both)
- Sapir-Whorf (linguistic relativity, weak version supported)
- Bilingualism (cognitive advantages, possible delayed dementia)

## CHAPTER 5: Motivation, Emotion, and Stress

AAMC FC mapping: 7A (motivation) + 6C (emotion, stress)

MOTIVATION THEORIES:
- Instinct theory (innate fixed action patterns)
- Drive reduction (homeostasis; primary vs secondary drives)
- Arousal theory + Yerkes-Dodson inverted-U (optimal arousal depends on task difficulty)
- Incentive theory (external pull)
- Expectancy-value theory (motivation = expectancy × value)
- Opponent-process theory
- Cognitive theories
- Need theories:
  - Maslow's hierarchy (5 levels): physiological → safety → love/belonging → esteem → self-actualization
  - Self-determination (Deci & Ryan): autonomy, competence, relatedness
- Intrinsic vs extrinsic motivation
- Overjustification effect (extrinsic reward kills intrinsic motivation)

HUNGER:
- LH = hunger ("Lateral = Lunch")
- VMH = satiety ("Ventromedial = Very full")
- Ghrelin (stomach, hunger; "Growling")
- Leptin (adipose, satiety)
- Insulin (pancreas, glucose uptake)

SEXUAL/SOCIAL MOTIVATION:
- Sexual: hormones, social/cultural, learned
- Social: affiliation, achievement, power (McClelland)

EMOTION COMPONENTS:
- Physiological + behavioral + cognitive

THEORIES OF EMOTION (high yield, every exam):
- James-Lange (body first → emotion: "I tremble therefore afraid")
- Cannon-Bard (body + emotion simultaneous)
- Schachter-Singer two-factor (arousal + cognitive label)
- Lazarus (cognitive appraisal first)

UNIVERSAL EMOTIONS (Ekman):
- Happiness, sadness, fear, anger, surprise, disgust

BRAIN AND EMOTION:
- Amygdala (fear, emotional memory)
- Prefrontal cortex (regulation, impulse control)
- ANS sympathetic activation
- Limbic system

STRESS — APPRAISAL (Lazarus):
- Primary appraisal (threat/challenge/irrelevant)
- Secondary appraisal (cope?)

STRESSOR TYPES:
- Cataclysmic
- Personal
- Daily hassles / background
- Ambient

GAS (Selye):
- Alarm → resistance → exhaustion

HPA AXIS:
- Hypothalamus → CRH → ant. pituitary → ACTH → adrenal cortex → cortisol
- Negative feedback: cortisol inhibits CRH and ACTH

CHRONIC STRESS EFFECTS:
- Immune suppression
- Cardiovascular (↑ BP, atherosclerosis)
- Cognitive (hippocampal damage)
- Mental health (depression, anxiety, PTSD)

COPING:
- Problem-focused (control)
- Emotion-focused (no control)
- Social support (emotional, instrumental, informational, appraisal)
- Learned helplessness (Seligman, model of depression)

## CHAPTER 6: Identity and Personality

AAMC FC mapping: 7A (personality) + 8A (self-identity)

SELF-CONCEPT AND IDENTITY:
- Self-concept (overall understanding of self)
- Self-schema (organized cognitive structure for one aspect)
- Self-esteem (evaluation of self-worth)
- Self-efficacy (Bandura, specific situation)
- Locus of control (Rotter): internal vs external
- Social identity, ethnic identity, national identity, gender/sex identity

FORMATION OF IDENTITY (symbolic interactionism):
- Looking-glass self (Cooley) — identity from how we think others see us

ERIKSON'S 8 STAGES:

7. Generativity vs Stagnation (middle adult)

KOHLBERG MORAL DEVELOPMENT:
- Preconventional (1: punishment; 2: self-interest)

FREUD PSYCHOSEXUAL:

VYGOTSKY (sociocultural):
- ZPD, scaffolding, MKO

PERSONALITY — PSYCHOANALYTIC (Freud):

- Defense mechanisms:
  - Repression
  - Denial

NEO-FREUDIANS:

HUMANISTIC:

- Maslow: self-actualization

TRAIT THEORIES:
- Allport (cardinal/central/secondary)
- Eysenck PEN model
- Big Five OCEAN (Openness, Conscientiousness, Extraversion, Agreeableness, Neuroticism) — factor analysis

SOCIAL COGNITIVE (Bandura):

BIOLOGICAL:
- Temperament (innate behavioral style)

BEHAVIORIST:

## CHAPTER 7: Psychological Disorders

AAMC FC mapping: 7A

UNDERSTANDING DISORDERS:
- DSM-5 classification (descriptive criteria)
- Biomedical vs biopsychosocial (MCAT-preferred) models
- Rates of disorders (~50% lifetime)
- Deinstitutionalization

ANXIETY DISORDERS:
- GAD (6+ months excessive worry)
- Specific phobia
- Social anxiety disorder
- Panic disorder + agoraphobia
- Separation anxiety disorder

OCD (own DSM-5 category, not anxiety):
- Obsessions + compulsions

TRAUMA-RELATED:
- PTSD: intrusion, avoidance, negative cognition/mood, hyperarousal

SOMATIC SYMPTOM:
- Somatic symptom disorder
- Illness anxiety disorder

DEPRESSIVE:
- MDD (5+ symptoms, 2+ weeks): anhedonia, sleep, appetite, concentration, SI
- Persistent depressive disorder (dysthymia, 2+ years)
- Premenstrual dysphoric disorder (PMDD)
- Biology: serotonin/NE deficiency

BIPOLAR:
- Bipolar I (full mania + depression)
- Bipolar II (hypomania + depression)

SCHIZOPHRENIA:
- Positive: hallucinations (usually auditory), delusions (paranoid/grandiose/referential), disorganized speech/behavior
- Negative: flat affect, avolition, alogia, anhedonia, social withdrawal
- Cognitive: working memory, executive function deficits
- Dopamine hypothesis (mesolimbic ↑ for positive; mesocortical ↓ for negative)
- Glutamate hypothesis (NMDA hypofunction; PCP mimics)

DISSOCIATIVE:
- DID
- Dissociative amnesia
- Depersonalization/derealization

PERSONALITY DISORDERS:
- Cluster A (odd/eccentric): paranoid, schizoid, schizotypal
- Cluster B (dramatic/emotional): antisocial, borderline, histrionic, narcissistic
- Cluster C (anxious/fearful): avoidant, dependent, OCPD (≠ OCD)

EATING DISORDERS:
- Anorexia nervosa
- Bulimia nervosa

NEURODEVELOPMENTAL:
- ADHD (stimulants paradoxically calming)
- Autism spectrum

BIOLOGICAL BASIS OF NS DISORDERS:
- Alzheimer's: amyloid plaques, tau tangles, ACh deficit; cholinesterase inhibitors
- Parkinson's: dopamine loss in substantia nigra; tremor/rigidity/bradykinesia; L-DOPA
- Huntington's: autosomal dominant, caudate/putamen degeneration, chorea
- ALS: motor neuron degeneration

TREATMENT:
- Psychotherapy types:
  - CBT (most empirical support)
  - Psychoanalytic / psychodynamic
  - Humanistic (Rogerian)
  - Behavioral therapy (exposure, systematic desensitization)
  - Group therapy
  - Family therapy
- Biomedical:
  - SSRIs (1st line for depression, anxiety)
  - SNRIs
  - Tricyclic antidepressants (TCAs)
  - MAOIs (tyramine restriction)
  - Antipsychotics: typical (D2; EPS) vs atypical (D2 + 5-HT2A; metabolic SE)
  - Mood stabilizers — lithium (bipolar)
  - Anxiolytics — benzodiazepines (GABA enhancement)
  - ECT (treatment-resistant depression, severe mania, catatonia)
- Combination therapy strongest evidence base

SANCTIONS:

ATTITUDES — ABC:

ELABORATION LIKELIHOOD MODEL (Petty & Cacioppo):

COGNITIVE DISSONANCE (Festinger):
- Attitude-behavior conflict → discomfort → attitude change

PERSUASION/COMPLIANCE:
- Foot-in-the-door
