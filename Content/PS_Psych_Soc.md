# PS -- PSYCHOLOGICAL, SOCIAL, AND BIOLOGICAL FOUNDATIONS

Discipline mix: ~65% psychology, 30% sociology, 5% biology.
Skill distribution: 35% Skill 1 (knowledge), 45% Skill 2 (reasoning), 10% Skill 3 (research design), 10% Skill 4 (data/statistics).

## FOUNDATIONAL CONCEPT 6 (25%): Perception, cognition, experience

### 6A -- Sensing the Environment

- Sensation (detection) vs perception (interpretation)
- Absolute threshold (minimum detectable stimulus)
- Difference threshold (JND -- just noticeable difference)
- Weber's law: delta_I/I = constant
- Signal detection theory: hit, miss, false alarm, correct rejection (influenced by response bias/criterion)
- Sensory adaptation (decreased sensitivity to constant stimulus)

VISION:
- Eye anatomy: cornea, pupil (iris controls), lens (accommodation), retina
- Rods: dim light, periphery, rhodopsin
- Cones: color, acuity, fovea (highest cone density)
- Trichromatic theory (3 cone types: R, G, B) vs opponent-process theory (R-G, B-Y, B-W pairs)
- Visual pathway: retina -> optic nerve -> optic chiasm -> LGN (thalamus) -> V1 (occipital)
- Feature detection (Hubel & Wiesel), parallel processing

HEARING:
- Ear: outer (pinna, auditory canal) -> middle (ossicles: malleus, incus, stapes) -> inner (cochlea, basilar membrane, hair cells)
- Place theory (high frequency -- base of cochlea) vs frequency theory (low frequency -- rate of firing)
- Conduction hearing loss (middle ear) vs sensorineural (inner ear/nerve)

OTHER SENSES:
- Somatosensation, pain (gate control theory), proprioception
- Taste (5: sweet, sour, salty, bitter, umami), smell (olfactory cells -> olfactory bulb)
- Vestibular sense (semicircular canals, balance)

PERCEPTION:
- Bottom-up (data-driven) vs top-down (knowledge-driven) processing
- Gestalt principles: proximity, similarity, closure, continuity, figure-ground, common fate
- Depth perception: binocular cues (convergence, retinal disparity) vs monocular cues (relative size, interposition, linear perspective)

Kaplan: Behavioral Sciences Ch 2

### 6B -- Cognition, Consciousness, Memory, Language

ATTENTION:
- Selective attention (cocktail party effect)
- Divided attention
- Inattentional blindness, change blindness

COGNITIVE DEVELOPMENT:
- Piaget's stages: sensorimotor (0-2, object permanence), preoperational (2-7, egocentrism, conservation failure), concrete operational (7-11, conservation, logical thought about concrete), formal operational (12+, abstract/hypothetical reasoning)
- Vygotsky: zone of proximal development (ZPD), scaffolding, sociocultural emphasis

PROBLEM-SOLVING AND BIASES:
- Algorithms vs heuristics
- Availability heuristic (judge likelihood by ease of recall)
- Representativeness heuristic (judge by resemblance to prototype)
- Anchoring bias, framing effect
- Confirmation bias, belief perseverance, overconfidence, hindsight bias
- Functional fixedness, mental set

INTELLIGENCE: Spearman's g, Gardner's multiple intelligences, Sternberg's triarchic (analytical, creative, practical)

CONSCIOUSNESS AND SLEEP (Kaplan gap):
- Sleep stages:
  - N1: light sleep, theta waves, hypnagogic hallucinations
  - N2: sleep spindles, K-complexes
  - N3: deep/slow-wave sleep, delta waves, growth hormone release, hardest to wake
  - REM: dreaming, muscle paralysis (atonia), beta waves, memory consolidation
- 90-minute cycles: more N3 early night, more REM late night
- Circadian rhythms: suprachiasmatic nucleus (SCN), melatonin (pineal)
- Sleep disorders: insomnia, narcolepsy (sudden REM onset), sleep apnea (obstructive/central), parasomnias (sleepwalking/night terrors -- N3 stage)
- Dreaming theories: Freud (wish fulfillment), activation-synthesis, information processing
- Hypnosis: social influence theory vs dissociation theory

DRUGS AND ADDICTION:
- Stimulants: cocaine, amphetamines (increased dopamine/NE)
- Depressants: alcohol, barbiturates, benzodiazepines (increased GABA)
- Opioids: heroin, morphine (mimic endorphins)
- Hallucinogens: LSD, psilocybin (serotonin)
- Cannabis
- Reward pathway: VTA -> nucleus accumbens (mesolimbic dopamine pathway)
- Tolerance, dependence (physical/psychological), withdrawal

MEMORY:
- Encoding: automatic vs effortful, levels of processing (shallow->deep), elaborative rehearsal, self-referencing, spacing effect
- Storage: sensory (iconic ~0.5s, echoic ~3-4s), short-term/working (7+/-2 items, ~30s, chunking), long-term:
  - Explicit/declarative: episodic (personal events) + semantic (facts)
  - Implicit/procedural: skills, conditioning
- Retrieval: recall vs recognition vs relearning, retrieval cues, context-dependent, state-dependent
- Forgetting: decay, proactive interference (old blocks new), retroactive interference (new blocks old), source monitoring errors, misinformation effect (Loftus)
- Long-term potentiation (LTP), synaptic plasticity
- Memory disorders: Alzheimer's (acetylcholine, amyloid plaques), Korsakoff's (thiamine/B1 deficiency, alcoholism)

LANGUAGE:
- Broca's area: speech production (damage -> expressive/non-fluent aphasia)
- Wernicke's area: speech comprehension (damage -> receptive/fluent aphasia)
- Chomsky: nativism, language acquisition device (LAD), universal grammar
- Skinner: language as learned behavior (operant conditioning)
- Sapir-Whorf hypothesis: language shapes thought (linguistic relativity)

Kaplan: Behavioral Sciences Ch 3-4

### 6C -- Emotion and Stress

THEORIES OF EMOTION (nearly guaranteed on every exam):
- James-Lange: stimulus -> body reaction -> emotion ("I'm trembling, so I must be scared")
- Cannon-Bard: stimulus -> simultaneous body reaction AND emotion
- Schachter-Singer two-factor: stimulus -> physiological arousal + cognitive label -> emotion
- Universal emotions (Ekman): happiness, sadness, fear, anger, surprise, disgust

BRAIN AND EMOTION:
- Amygdala: fear processing, emotional memory
- Prefrontal cortex: emotional regulation
- Autonomic nervous system: sympathetic activation during emotion

STRESS:
- Appraisal model (Lazarus): primary (Is this a threat?) -> secondary (Can I cope?)
- Stressor types: cataclysmic events, personal stressors, daily hassles/background
- General adaptation syndrome (Selye): alarm -> resistance -> exhaustion
- HPA axis: hypothalamus -> CRH -> anterior pituitary -> ACTH -> adrenal cortex -> cortisol
- Coping: problem-focused (address the stressor) vs emotion-focused (manage emotional response)

Kaplan: Behavioral Sciences Ch 5

## FOUNDATIONAL CONCEPT 7 (35%): Behavior and behavior change

### 7A -- Biological Bases, Personality, Disorders, Motivation

PERSONALITY THEORIES:
- Psychoanalytic (Freud): id (pleasure principle), ego (reality principle), superego (morality)
- Defense mechanisms: repression, denial, projection, displacement, sublimation, rationalization, reaction formation, regression
- Humanistic: Rogers (unconditional positive regard, self-concept), Maslow (self-actualization)
- Trait: Big Five OCEAN (Openness, Conscientiousness, Extraversion, Agreeableness, Neuroticism)
- Social cognitive: Bandura (self-efficacy, reciprocal determinism)
- Biological: temperament, twin studies, heritability

PSYCHOLOGICAL DISORDERS:
- DSM classification, biomedical vs biopsychosocial models
- Anxiety disorders: GAD, specific phobias, social anxiety, panic disorder, agoraphobia
- OCD: obsessions (intrusive thoughts) + compulsions (repetitive behaviors)
- PTSD
- Somatic symptom disorders (illness anxiety)
- Depressive disorders: MDD (>=2 weeks, 5+ symptoms), persistent depressive disorder
  - Biological: serotonin/NE hypothesis
- Bipolar: mania + depression, I vs II
- Schizophrenia: positive symptoms (hallucinations, delusions, disorganized), negative symptoms (flat affect, avolition, alogia, anhedonia), cognitive symptoms
  - Dopamine hypothesis, glutamate hypothesis
- Dissociative disorders: DID, dissociative amnesia
- Personality disorders: Cluster A (odd/eccentric -- paranoid, schizoid, schizotypal), Cluster B (dramatic -- antisocial, borderline, histrionic, narcissistic), Cluster C (anxious -- avoidant, dependent, OCPD)

MOTIVATION:
- Drive reduction theory (homeostasis)
- Arousal theory, Yerkes-Dodson law (optimal arousal, inverted U)
- Incentive theory (external rewards)
- Maslow's hierarchy: physiological -> safety -> belonging -> esteem -> self-actualization
- Self-determination theory: autonomy, competence, relatedness
- Hunger: lateral hypothalamus (hunger), ventromedial hypothalamus (satiety), ghrelin (hunger), leptin (satiety from adipose)

Kaplan: Behavioral Sciences Ch 1, 5, 6, 7

### 7B -- Social Processes (PSY, SOC)

- Social facilitation (Zajonc: easy tasks improved, hard tasks impaired by others)
- Social loafing (less effort in groups)
- Deindividuation (loss of self-awareness in groups)
- Bystander effect, diffusion of responsibility
- Group polarization (groups become more extreme)
- Groupthink (conformity suppresses dissent)
- Conformity: Asch line experiments (normative vs informational influence)
- Obedience: Milgram experiment (authority -> shocking)
- Norms: folkways (informal customs), mores (moral significance), taboos (strongly prohibited)
- Deviance: differential association (Sutherland), labeling theory (Becker), strain theory (Merton: conformity, innovation, ritualism, retreatism, rebellion) (Kaplan gap)
- Agents of socialization: family, peers, media, education, workplace, religion

Kaplan: Behavioral Sciences Ch 8

### 7C -- Learning (PSY)

CLASSICAL CONDITIONING (Pavlov):
- UCS -> UCR (unlearned), CS -> CR (learned through pairing)
- Acquisition, extinction, spontaneous recovery
- Generalization, discrimination
- Higher-order conditioning
- Biological preparedness (taste aversion -- Garcia effect, one-trial learning)

OPERANT CONDITIONING (Skinner):
- Positive reinforcement (+stimulus, increased behavior)
- Negative reinforcement (-stimulus, increased behavior)
- Positive punishment (+stimulus, decreased behavior)
- Negative punishment (-stimulus, decreased behavior)
- Schedules: fixed-ratio (fast, brief pause), variable-ratio (fastest, most resistant to extinction), fixed-interval (scallop), variable-interval (slow steady)
- Shaping (successive approximations)
- Escape vs avoidance learning
- Instinctive drift

OBSERVATIONAL LEARNING (Bandura):
- Modeling, Bobo doll experiment
- Vicarious reinforcement
- Mirror neurons

ATTITUDE CHANGE:
- Elaboration likelihood model: central route (careful, lasting) vs peripheral route (superficial, temporary)
- Cognitive dissonance (Festinger): attitude-behavior conflict -> discomfort -> attitude change
- Foot-in-the-door, door-in-the-face, lowball technique

Kaplan: Behavioral Sciences Ch 3, 8

## FOUNDATIONAL CONCEPT 8 (20%): Self and social interaction

### 8A -- Self-Identity

- Self-concept, self-identity, social identity
- Self-esteem, self-efficacy (Bandura), locus of control (internal vs external -- Rotter)
- Erikson's 8 psychosocial stages: trust/mistrust (0-1), autonomy/shame (1-3), initiative/guilt (3-6), industry/inferiority (6-12), identity/role confusion (12-18), intimacy/isolation (young adult), generativity/stagnation (middle adult), integrity/despair (late adult)
- Kohlberg's moral development: preconventional (punishment/reward), conventional (social approval/law), postconventional (social contract/universal ethics)
- Freud's psychosexual stages: oral (0-1), anal (1-3), phallic (3-6, Oedipus/Electra), latency (6-12), genital (12+)
- Looking-glass self (Cooley): identity shaped by how we think others see us (Kaplan gap)
- Role-taking (Mead): I (spontaneous self) and Me (socialized self) (Kaplan gap)
- Reference groups

Kaplan: Behavioral Sciences Ch 6

### 8B -- Social Thinking

ATTRIBUTION:
- Fundamental attribution error: overattribute others' behavior to disposition (internal)
- Actor-observer bias: attribute own behavior to situation, others' to disposition
- Self-serving bias: success = internal, failure = external

PREJUDICE AND DISCRIMINATION:
- Stereotype (cognitive), prejudice (affective), discrimination (behavioral)
- Individual vs institutional discrimination
- Stereotype threat (Steele): awareness of stereotype -> performance anxiety
- Self-fulfilling prophecy
- Ethnocentrism vs cultural relativism
- In-group/out-group bias, out-group homogeneity
- Contact hypothesis (reducing prejudice through equal-status contact)

Kaplan: Behavioral Sciences Ch 10

### 8C -- Social Interactions

- Impression management, front-stage/back-stage self (Goffman's dramaturgical approach) (Kaplan gap)
- Status: achieved (earned) vs ascribed (assigned at birth), master status
- Roles: role conflict (between different roles), role strain (within one role), role exit
- Groups: primary (close, intimate) vs secondary (formal, task-oriented)
- In-group/out-group, reference groups
- Networks and social capital
- Organizations: bureaucracy (Weber's ideal type), iron law of oligarchy, McDonaldization (Kaplan gap)
- Attraction: proximity, similarity, reciprocity, physical attractiveness
- Aggression: biological (testosterone, amygdala) vs social (frustration-aggression, social learning)
- Attachment: Bowlby (contact comfort, critical period), Ainsworth Strange Situation:
  - Secure (70%): distressed at separation, comforted by return
  - Avoidant: little distress, ignores caregiver
  - Anxious-ambivalent: very distressed, not easily comforted
  - Disorganized: inconsistent/confused behavior
- Altruism: empathy-altruism hypothesis, kin selection, reciprocal altruism

Kaplan: Behavioral Sciences Ch 9, 10

## FOUNDATIONAL CONCEPT 9 (15%): Culture and social structure

### 9A -- Social Structure (Kaplan gap)

SOCIOLOGICAL THEORIES (tested more than students expect):
- Functionalism (Durkheim): society as organism, parts serve functions
  - Manifest functions (intended) vs latent functions (unintended)
  - Social solidarity: mechanical (similar) vs organic (specialized)
  - Anomie: breakdown of social norms
- Conflict theory (Marx): society driven by class struggle and power
  - Class consciousness vs false consciousness
  - Weber extension: power, authority, status groups
- Symbolic interactionism (Mead, Blumer): meaning through social interaction, symbols
- Social constructionism (Berger & Luckmann): reality is socially constructed (Kaplan gap)
- Feminist theory: gender inequality as structural, intersectionality (Kaplan gap)
- Exchange/rational choice theory: behavior based on cost-benefit analysis

SOCIAL INSTITUTIONS (Kaplan gap):
- Education: hidden curriculum, teacher expectancy (Pygmalion effect), tracking, educational stratification
- Family: kinship, nuclear/extended, endogamy/exogamy
- Religion: types (church, sect, cult), secularization, fundamentalism
- Government: authority types -- traditional, charismatic, rational-legal (Weber)
- Economy: capitalism, socialism, division of labor

HEALTH AND MEDICINE:
- Medicalization: normal conditions reframed as medical problems
- Sick role (Parsons): rights (exempt from normal duties, not blamed) + obligations (seek treatment, try to recover)
- Illness experience
- Social epidemiology
- Delivery of healthcare, access disparities

CULTURE:
- Elements: beliefs, language, rituals, symbols, values, norms
- Material vs symbolic culture
- Culture lag (technology changes faster than norms), culture shock
- Assimilation, multiculturalism
- Subcultures, countercultures

Kaplan: Behavioral Sciences Ch 11. MAJOR KAPLAN GAP -- supplement with Khan Academy and 300-page P/S document.

### 9B -- Demographics (Kaplan gap)

- Demographic transition model (4 stages):
  1. Pre-industrial: high birth, high death
  2. Transitional: high birth, declining death
  3. Industrial: declining birth, low death
  4. Post-industrial: low birth, low death
- Malthusian theory: population grows exponentially, food grows arithmetically
- Population pyramids: expansive, constrictive, stationary
- Fertility rates (total, crude, age-specific), mortality patterns
- Push/pull factors in migration
- Social movements: relative deprivation
- Globalization: contributing factors, social consequences
- Urbanization, suburbanization, gentrification

Kaplan: Behavioral Sciences Ch 11

## FOUNDATIONAL CONCEPT 10 (5%): Stratification

### 10A -- Social Inequality (Kaplan gap)

- Social stratification theories: Davis-Moore (functional necessity) vs conflict (exploitation)
- Social class, socioeconomic status (income + education + occupation)
- Social mobility: intergenerational, intragenerational, vertical, horizontal
- Meritocracy vs structural barriers
- Poverty: absolute vs relative, social exclusion
- Cultural capital (Bourdieu): knowledge, skills, education that confer social advantage (Kaplan gap)
- Social capital: networks and relationships that provide resources (Kaplan gap)
- Class consciousness vs false consciousness (Marx)
- Intersectionality: overlapping identities (race, gender, class) create unique experiences
- Health disparities: class, gender, race -> health outcomes
- Healthcare disparities: unequal access and quality
- Social determinants of health (SES, neighborhood, education, discrimination)
- Environmental justice (Kaplan gap)
