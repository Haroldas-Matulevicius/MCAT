Kaplan Physics Ch 1 | FC4A

UNITS & VECTORS:
- SI base units: kg, m, s, A, K, mol
- Scalar (magnitude only): mass, speed, distance, time, energy, temperature
- Vector (magnitude + direction): displacement, velocity, acceleration, force, momentum
- Derived units: N = kg·m/s² | J = kg·m²/s² | Pa = N/m² | W = J/s

KINEMATICS:
- Displacement (Δx): vector. Distance: scalar (total path length)
- Velocity = Δx/Δt (vector). Speed = scalar. Acceleration = Δv/Δt (vector)
- Big 5 (constant acceleration only):
  - v = v₀ + at (missing Δx)
  - Δx = v₀t + ½at² (missing v)
  - v² = v₀² + 2aΔx (missing t)
  - Δx = ½(v₀+v)t (missing a)
- Free fall: a = g ≈ 10 m/s² downward
- Projectile motion: horizontal (a=0, x = v₀ₓt) and vertical (a=g) are independent; share only time
  - v₀ₓ = v₀cosθ, v₀ᵧ = v₀sinθ
  - Max range at 45°; complementary angles same range
  - At peak: vᵧ = 0 but vₓ ≠ 0 (object NOT at rest)

NEWTON'S LAWS:
- 1st: object stays at rest or constant v unless net external force acts
- 2nd: ΣF = ma (net force, not individual force)
- 3rd: equal and opposite forces on DIFFERENT objects (don't cancel)

FRICTION:
- Static: f_s ≤ μ_s·N (variable, at max only when on verge of sliding)
- Kinetic: f_k = μ_k·N (constant while sliding)
- μ_s > μ_k always

INCLINED PLANES:
- Parallel to slope: mg sinθ (down slope)
- Perpendicular to slope: mg cosθ (into surface), so N = mg cosθ
- Frictionless: a = g sinθ

CIRCULAR MOTION:
- Centripetal acceleration: a_c = v²/r (toward center)
- Centripetal force: F_c = mv²/r (not a new force — it's the net force toward center)
- Minimum speed at top of loop: v = √(gr) (set N = 0 → mg = mv²/r)

TORQUE AND EQUILIBRIUM:
- τ = rF sinθ (N·m, not Joules)
- CCW = positive, CW = negative
- Static equilibrium: ΣF = 0 AND Στ = 0 (both conditions required)
- Pick pivot at unknown force location to eliminate unknowns
- Center of mass: x_cm = Σ(mᵢxᵢ)/Σmᵢ

MOMENTUM & IMPULSE:
- p = mv (vector)
- Impulse: J = FΔt = Δp (airbags, padding: same Δp, more time → less force)
- Conservation: m₁v₁ᵢ + m₂v₂ᵢ = m₁v₁f + m₂v₂f (all collision types)
- Elastic: momentum AND KE conserved
- Perfectly inelastic: (m₁ + m₂)vf (stick together, max KE loss)

KEY TRAPS:
- Constant speed around a curve → acceleration IS present (centripetal)
- Normal force ≠ mg on inclines (N = mg cosθ)
- Static friction ≠ μ_s·N unless on verge of sliding (it's variable)
- Action-reaction pairs act on DIFFERENT objects
