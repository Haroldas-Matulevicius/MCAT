ZEROTH LAW:
- If A is in thermal equilibrium with B, and B with C → A and C are in thermal equilibrium
- Defines thermal equilibrium (T_A = T_B, no net heat transfer)
- Justifies thermometers
- Specific heat: Q = mcΔT (water: c = 4.18 J/g·K, very high → temperature buffer)

SYSTEMS:
- Open: exchanges matter AND energy with surroundings (living organisms)
- Closed: exchanges energy only (sealed gas cylinder)
- Isolated: exchanges neither matter nor energy (ideal thermos, universe)

FIRST LAW (ΔU = Q − W):
- ΔU = change in internal energy of system
- Q = heat added TO system (positive = heat in)
- W = work done BY system (positive = expansion)
- Key processes:
  - Isothermal (constant T): ΔU = 0 → Q = W
  - Adiabatic (Q = 0): ΔU = −W
  - Isochoric (constant V): W = 0 → ΔU = Q
  - Isobaric (constant P): W = PΔV

SECOND LAW:
- ΔS_universe ≥ 0 (entropy of isolated system always increases or stays same)
- Spontaneous processes increase entropy of the universe
- Heat flows spontaneously from hot to cold only
- No heat engine can be 100% efficient
- Entropy (S) = measure of disorder / number of available microstates

HEAT ENGINES:
- Efficiency: e = W_out/Q_H = 1 − (Q_C/Q_H)
- Carnot (maximum theoretical efficiency): e_Carnot = 1 − T_C/T_H (temperatures in Kelvin!)
- Real engines: always less than Carnot efficiency
- Refrigerator = reverse heat engine (requires work input)

THERMODYNAMICS ↔ CHEMISTRY CONNECTION:
- ΔG = ΔH − TΔS (connects first + second law to spontaneity)
- ΔG < 0 ↔ spontaneous ↔ ΔS_universe > 0
- ATP synthesis: couples spontaneous (proton gradient) to non-spontaneous (ADP + Pᵢ → ATP)
