CHARGE:
- Quantized: e = 1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹ C (smallest unit)
- Conservation: total charge of system never changes
- Conductors: electrons move freely; excess charge on outer surface; E = 0 inside at equilibrium
- Insulators: charge stays where placed

COULOMB'S LAW:
- F = kq₁q₂/r² (k = 9 × 10⁹ N·m²/C²)
- Inverse square: double r → F × ¼; triple r → F × 1/9
- Like charges repel, opposite attract
- Much stronger than gravity at atomic scale; both are inverse square

ELECTRIC FIELD:
- E = kQ/r² (from point charge; units: N/C = V/m)
- Vector: points away from positive charges, toward negative
- Between parallel plates (uniform field): E = V/d (favorite MCAT setup)
  - Field uniform in magnitude and direction; points from + plate to − plate
- Gauss's law key results:
  - Inside conductor at equilibrium: E = 0
  - All excess charge on outer surface of conductor
  - Outside spherical charge: E = kQ/r² (as if all charge at center)

ELECTRIC POTENTIAL:
- V = kQ/r (from point charge; SCALAR, not vector)
- Add contributions from multiple charges algebraically (no trig needed)
- Positive charges → positive potential; negative → negative potential
- Potential energy: U = qV = kq₁q₂/r (sign matters)
- Positive charge moves from high V to low V (like ball rolling downhill)
- Negative charge moves from low V to high V (both move to lower PE)
- Equipotential surfaces always ⊥ to electric field lines; no work done moving along them

CAPACITORS (parallel plate):
- Capacitance: C = ε₀A/d (F = farads; bigger plates or closer spacing → more C)
- Charge: Q = CV
- Energy: U = ½CV² = Q²/2C = ½QV
- Dielectric (insulating material between plates): C' = κC (κ ≥ 1)
- Dielectric with battery connected (V fixed): Q↑, C↑, E same, U↑
- Dielectric with capacitor isolated (Q fixed): V↓, C↑, E↓, U↓
- Capacitors in series: 1/C_total = 1/C₁ + 1/C₂
- Capacitors in parallel: C_total = C₁ + C₂ (opposite of resistors!)

MAGNETISM:
- Force on moving charge: F = qvB sinθ (right-hand rule; zero if v parallel to B)
- Magnetic force always ⊥ to velocity → does ZERO work → can change direction but never speed
- Charged particle in uniform B field → circular motion (constant speed, changing direction)
- If particle speeds up in B field → must be electric field doing the work
- Force on current-carrying wire: F = BIL sinθ (right-hand rule with current direction)
- Magnetic field from long straight wire: B = μ₀I/(2πr) (lower yield)

KEY TRAPS:
- Potential (V) is scalar — just add algebraically when multiple charges present
- Dielectric: always ask "battery connected (V fixed) or isolated (Q fixed)?"
- Magnetic force does NO WORK — cannot change KE, only direction
- Capacitors: series/parallel rules are REVERSED compared to resistors
