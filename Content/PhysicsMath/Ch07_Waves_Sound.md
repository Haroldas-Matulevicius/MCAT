# CP — Physics Ch 7: Waves & Sound

Kaplan Physics Ch 7 | FC4D

---

WAVE PROPERTIES:
- λ = wavelength (m) | f = frequency (Hz) | A = amplitude | T = period (s) | v = speed (m/s)
- T = 1/f | v = fλ (wave equation)
- Fixed wave speed: increasing f → decreasing λ (inversely proportional)
- Transverse: oscillation ⊥ to travel (light, string waves; can be polarized)
- Longitudinal: oscillation ∥ to travel (sound, compressions/rarefactions; cannot be polarized)

SUPERPOSITION & INTERFERENCE:
- Constructive: waves in phase (crest + crest); amplitudes add; path diff = mλ
- Destructive: waves ½λ out of phase (crest + trough); amplitudes subtract; path diff = (m+½)λ

STANDING WAVES (HIGH YIELD):
- Nodes: zero displacement (permanent destructive interference)
- Antinodes: maximum displacement (permanent constructive interference)
- String fixed both ends / open pipe (both ends):
  - λ_n = 2L/n (n = 1, 2, 3, ...) → ALL harmonics
  - f₁ = v/2L (fundamental)
- Closed pipe (one end closed, one end open):
  - λ_n = 4L/n (n = 1, 3, 5, ...) → ODD harmonics ONLY
  - f₁ = v/4L (fundamental; LOWER pitch than open pipe of same length)
  - Why odd only: must have node at closed end AND antinode at open end → only odd multiples of λ/4 fit

RESONANCE:
- External vibration matches natural frequency → maximum energy transfer, large amplitude
- MRI (radio waves), ear canal amplification (~3-4 kHz), tuning forks

SOUND:
- Longitudinal mechanical wave (requires medium)
- Speed: solids > liquids > gases (OPPOSITE of light; sound speeds up in denser media)
  - Air ≈ 340 m/s | water ≈ 1500 m/s | steel ≈ 5000 m/s
  - Speed increases with temperature (in gases)

INTENSITY & DECIBELS:
- I = P/A (W/m²) | I₀ = 10⁻¹² W/m² (threshold of hearing)
- β = 10 log(I/I₀) (dB)
- ×2 intensity = +3 dB | ×10 = +10 dB | ×100 = +20 dB | ×1000 = +30 dB
- Break large factors into 2s and 10s: ×200 = ×2 × ×10 × ×10 = +3 +10 +10 = +23 dB
- Inverse square law: I = P/(4πr²) → double distance → ¼ intensity → −6 dB

BEATS:
- Two slightly different frequencies → pulsating volume
- f_beat = |f₁ − f₂|
- Unknown fork: "512 Hz fork beats at 3 Hz" → unknown = 509 or 515 Hz

DOPPLER EFFECT:
- Source/observer approaching → perceived f increases (higher pitch)
- Source/observer receding → perceived f decreases (lower pitch)
- f' = f × (v ± v_obs)/(v ∓ v_src) [toward = together = higher]
- Moving source ≠ moving observer at same speed (slightly different f')
- Medical: Doppler ultrasound measures blood flow velocity from frequency shift

ULTRASOUND:
- >20,000 Hz; no ionizing radiation (safe for fetuses)
- Higher f = better resolution but less penetration
- Reflection at tissue boundaries; Doppler for blood flow

KEY TRAPS:
- Closed pipe: ODD harmonics only (not both)
- Closed pipe fundamental LOWER than open pipe of same length (λ₁ = 4L vs 2L)
- Sound faster in denser media; light slower in denser media (opposite!)
- dB is logarithmic: doubling intensity = +3 dB only (not +100%)
- Doppler only changes perceived frequency, not emitted frequency
