PHOTOELECTRIC EFFECT:
- KE_max = hf − φ (φ = work function; threshold f₀ = φ/h)
- Below f₀: NO electrons ejected regardless of intensity
- Intensity ↑ (same f): more electrons (higher current), same KE_max per electron
- Frequency ↑ (above f₀): same number of electrons, higher KE_max
- Ejection is instantaneous (no energy accumulation) → proved particle nature of light
- Trap: intensity ≠ energy per photon; frequency = energy per photon

WAVE-PARTICLE DUALITY:
- de Broglie: λ = h/p = h/(mv) (matter has wave properties)
- Increasing momentum → decreasing wavelength
- Relevant for electrons (macroscopic objects: negligibly small λ)

BOHR MODEL (hydrogen and hydrogen-like only):
- E_n = −13.6 eV/n² (n = 1, 2, 3, ...)
- Lower n = closer to nucleus = more stable (more negative energy)
- Ground state: n = 1; ionization: n = ∞, E = 0
- Transition energy: ΔE = −13.6(1/n_f² − 1/n_i²) eV
  - Electron drops → emits photon (ΔE negative)
  - Electron jumps → absorbs photon (ΔE positive)
  - E_photon = |ΔE| = hf = hc/λ

SPECTRAL SERIES:
- Lyman: transition to n = 1 → UV (largest energy gaps)
- Balmer: transition to n = 2 → visible
- Paschen: transition to n = 3 → IR
- Bigger jump → higher E photon → shorter λ

EMISSION vs ABSORPTION:
- Emission: bright lines on dark background (excited atoms release photons)
- Absorption: dark lines on continuous spectrum (atoms absorb specific λ from white light)
- Same line positions for a given element — they are complementary

NUCLEAR DECAY:
| Decay | Particle | ΔA | ΔZ |
|-------|----------|-----|-----|
| Alpha | ⁴₂He nucleus | −4 | −2 |
| Beta-minus | electron (β⁻) | 0 | +1 (neutron → proton; neutron-rich nuclei) |
| Beta-plus | positron (β⁺) | 0 | −1 (proton → neutron; proton-rich nuclei) |
| Electron capture | inner e⁻ captured | 0 | −1 (same result as β⁺) |
| Gamma | photon | 0 | 0 (nucleus drops from excited state) |

Penetration: α < β < γ | Ionization: α > β > γ (inverse tradeoff)
β⁺ → positron annihilates with electron → 2 gamma rays at 180° → PET scan

HALF-LIFE:
- N = N₀ × (½)^(t/t₁/₂)
- Count half-lives: 80 g → after 4 half-lives → 80/16 = 5 g
- Recognize fractions: 12.5% = (½)³ = 3 half-lives; 6.25% = 4 half-lives
- Activity also halves every half-life

MASS DEFECT & BINDING ENERGY:
- Δm = (Z·m_p + N·m_n) − m_nucleus (nucleus lighter than its parts)
- Binding energy = Δm·c² (energy released when nucleus formed)
- Binding energy/nucleon peaks at ⁵⁶Fe and ⁶²Ni (most stable)
- Fusion (light → heavier): products have higher BE/nucleon → releases energy; sun
- Fission (heavy → lighter): products have higher BE/nucleon → releases energy; reactors
- Both release energy because products are more stable (higher BE/nucleon) than reactants

MEDICAL APPLICATIONS:
- PET scan: ¹⁸F-FDG (glucose analog); positron emission → annihilation → two 511 keV γ at 180°; detects metabolically active tissue (cancer, brain)
- Radiation therapy: gamma, X-ray, or proton beams damage cancer DNA
- Radioactive tracers: ¹³¹I for thyroid (iodine concentrates there)

KEY TRAPS:
- Alpha: A−4, Z−2 (not just Z−2 or A−2)
- Beta-minus: Z+1 (neutron→proton). Beta-plus: Z−1 (proton→neutron)
- Photoelectric: intensity changes count; frequency changes KE per electron
- Half-life is constant — unaffected by temperature, pressure, chemical form
- Fission AND fusion both release energy (different mechanisms, both increase BE/nucleon)
