# CP — Physics Ch 10: Mathematics

Kaplan Physics Ch 10 | Cross-cutting (all CP questions)

> No calculator on the MCAT. These skills make or break timing.

---

SCIENTIFIC NOTATION:
- Multiply: multiply coefficients, ADD exponents
- Divide: divide coefficients, SUBTRACT exponents
- Add/subtract: match exponents first
- If coefficient ≥ 10 after multiply → shift (2.4 × 10¹⁰, not 24 × 10⁹)

LOGARITHMS (heavily tested — pH, pKa, dB, Nernst):
- log(2) = 0.3 | log(3) ≈ 0.5 | log(5) = 0.7 | log(10) = 1.0
- Derive others: log(4) = 2×log(2) = 0.6 | log(6) = log(2)+log(3) = 0.78 | log(8) = 3×log(2) = 0.9
- Rules: log(AB) = logA + logB | log(A/B) = logA − logB | log(Aⁿ) = n·logA
- pH = −log[H⁺] = n − log(a) where [H⁺] = a × 10⁻ⁿ
  - [H⁺] = 3.5×10⁻⁴ → pH = 4 − log(3.5) ≈ 4 − 0.54 = 3.46
  - [H⁺] = 5.0×10⁻⁸ → pH = 8 − 0.7 = 7.3
- Reverse: pH = 4.7 → [H⁺] = 10⁻⁴·⁷ = 10⁻⁵ × 10⁰·³ = 2.0×10⁻⁵ (10⁰·³ = 2)
- Decibels: β = 10 log(I/I₀) | ×10 intensity = +10 dB | ×2 = +3 dB
- ln = 2.303 × log | ln appears in first-order kinetics, Nernst, radioactive decay

TRIGONOMETRY:
- sin/cos at standard angles:
  - 0°: sin=0, cos=1 | 30°: sin=0.5, cos=0.87 | 45°: sin=cos=0.71 | 60°: sin=0.87, cos=0.5 | 90°: sin=1, cos=0
- SOH-CAH-TOA: sin = opp/hyp | cos = adj/hyp | tan = opp/adj
- Inclined plane: parallel = mg sinθ; perpendicular = mg cosθ
- Projectile: v₀ₓ = v₀cosθ; v₀ᵧ = v₀sinθ
- Vector at angle θ: x-comp = magnitude × cosθ; y-comp = magnitude × sinθ

METRIC PREFIXES:
- pico(p) 10⁻¹² | nano(n) 10⁻⁹ | micro(μ) 10⁻⁶ | milli(m) 10⁻³ | centi(c) 10⁻²
- kilo(k) 10³ | mega(M) 10⁶ | giga(G) 10⁹
- Key conversions: 1 atm = 760 mmHg = 10⁵ Pa | 0°C = 273 K | 1 eV = 1.6×10⁻¹⁹ J
  - 1 cal = 4.2 J | c = 3×10⁸ m/s | R = 8.314 J/mol·K = 0.0821 L·atm/mol·K

PROPORTIONAL REASONING (most valuable MCAT math skill):
- Direct (y = kx): x doubles → y doubles
- Inverse (y = k/x): x doubles → y halves
- Square (y = kx²): x doubles → y quadruples
- Inverse square (y = k/x²): x doubles → y becomes ¼
- Key examples:
  - KE = ½mv²: v doubles → KE × 4
  - Coulomb F: r doubles → F × ¼
  - Poiseuille Q: r doubles → Q × 16 (r⁴!)
  - Intensity I = P/4πr²: r doubles → I × ¼
  - Pendulum T = 2π√(L/g): L × 4 → T × 2 (square root)

GRAPHICAL ANALYSIS:
- Slope = rise/run = (y₂−y₁)/(x₂−x₁)
- Key linearized plots: ln[A] vs time (slope = −k, first-order kinetics); 1/V vs 1/[S] (Lineweaver-Burk)
- Area under curve: F vs d → work | F vs t → impulse | v vs t → displacement
- Semi-log plot: linearizes exponential (y = Aeᵇˣ)
- Log-log plot: linearizes power law (y = Axⁿ); slope = exponent n

ESTIMATION:
- √2 ≈ 1.4 | √3 ≈ 1.7 | π ≈ 3 | π² ≈ 10 | e ≈ 2.7
- Round aggressively, adjust direction (over vs underestimate)
- Order-of-magnitude reasoning: get power of 10 right when choices are spread by 10×
- Use answer choices to decide precision needed
- Always do a sanity check after calculating
