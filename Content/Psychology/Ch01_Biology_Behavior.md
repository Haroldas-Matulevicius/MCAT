Scope: Nervous system organization; brain regions and functions; endocrine system; influences on behavior (genetics, environment); prenatal and postnatal neural development.

AAMC FC mapping: FC6A (parts of nervous system) + FC7A (biological bases of behavior)

Kaplan: Behavioral Sciences Ch 1

HISTORY:
- Phrenology (Gall) → discredited but introduced localization of function
- Flourens — extirpation/ablation in animals (first to experimentally prove localization)
- Wundt (1879) — first psych lab (Leipzig); structuralism via introspection; "father of experimental psychology"
- William James — functionalism (US); *Principles of Psychology* (1890); stream of consciousness
- Dewey — extension to education; 1896 reflex arc paper = formal start of US functionalism
- Broca — speech production localized (left frontal)
- Helmholtz — speed of nerve impulse (psychology as quantifiable)
- Sherrington — inferred synapses

NERVOUS SYSTEM ORGANIZATION:
- CNS (brain + spinal cord) vs PNS (everything else)
- PNS: somatic (voluntary, skeletal muscle) vs autonomic
- ANS: sympathetic (fight/flight, NE) vs parasympathetic (rest/digest, ACh)
- Vagus nerve (CN X) — major parasympathetic nerve, ~75% of parasympathetic fibers

BRAIN ORGANIZATION:
- Hindbrain: medulla (vital reflexes — breathing, HR, BP, vomiting), pons (sensory/motor relay), cerebellum (coordination, balance, motor learning), reticular formation (arousal, sleep-wake)
- Midbrain: superior colliculi (visual reflexes), inferior colliculi (auditory reflexes), substantia nigra (dopamine; Parkinson's)
- Forebrain: thalamus, hypothalamus, limbic, basal ganglia, cerebrum

FOREBRAIN PARTS:
- Thalamus — sensory relay (all senses except olfaction)
- Hypothalamus — homeostasis (Four F's: Feeding, Fighting, Fleeing, Sex)
  - LH = hunger ("Lateral = Lunch")
  - VMH = satiety ("Ventromedial = Very full")
  - SCN = circadian pacemaker
- Limbic system — emotion + memory
  - Amygdala (fear, emotional memory)
  - Hippocampus (new explicit memory; HM case)
  - Fornix (white matter tract)
  - Septal nuclei (pleasure, addiction)
  - Cingulate cortex (emotion regulation, attention)
- Basal ganglia — caudate, putamen, globus pallidus (smooth voluntary movement; Parkinson's, Huntington's)
- Cerebrum — 4 lobes:
  - Frontal: primary motor cortex, executive function, Broca's area (left, speech production), personality
  - Parietal: primary somatosensory cortex, spatial reasoning
  - Temporal: primary auditory cortex, Wernicke's area (left, comprehension), memory
  - Occipital: primary visual cortex (V1)
- Aphasias: Broca's (expressive, "Broken speech"), Wernicke's (receptive, "Wordy but Wrong"), conduction (arcuate fasciculus damage)
- Lateralization: left = language/logic; right = spatial/music/face/emotion
- Corpus callosum — connects hemispheres
- Split-brain (Sperry) — severed corpus callosum studies

INFLUENCES ON BEHAVIOR:
- Heritability — proportion of trait variation due to genes
- Twin studies (MZ vs DZ), adoption studies, family studies
- Gene-environment interaction (epigenetics)
- Endocrine — hypothalamus → pituitary → other glands
- Anterior pituitary mnemonic FLAT PEG: FSH, LH, ACTH, TSH, Prolactin, Endorphins, GH
- Posterior pituitary: oxytocin (bonding, contractions, milk letdown), ADH (water retention)
- HPA axis: hypothalamus → CRH → ant. pituitary → ACTH → adrenal cortex → cortisol
- Adrenal cortex: cortisol, aldosterone, sex hormones; medulla: epinephrine, NE
- Thyroid: T3/T4 (metabolism), calcitonin
- Gonads: testosterone (testes), estrogen/progesterone (ovaries)
- Pineal: melatonin (circadian)

DEVELOPMENT — PRENATAL (NEURULATION):
- Notochord induces overlying ectoderm → neural plate
- Neural plate edges rise as neural folds flanking the neural groove
- Folds meet at midline → neural tube (rostral closes first, caudal last)
- Neural tube → 3 primary vesicles → 5 secondary vesicles → adult brain
- Neural tube defects: anencephaly (rostral failure, lethal), spina bifida (caudal); folic acid prevents
- Alar plate (dorsal, sensory → dorsal horn; "Alar = Afferent = Above")
- Basal plate (ventral, motor → ventral horn; "Basal = Below")
- Sulcus limitans divides them; pattern extends into brainstem cranial nerve nuclei
- Neural crest cells pinch off at fold crest and migrate away (derivative list in BB/BB_Organ_Systems.md §7.4)

PLACENTA (brief — detail in BB):
- Fetal trophoblast + maternal endometrium
- Key roles: gas exchange, hCG hormone production, immune barrier (IgG crosses)
- Detailed shunts + hormone handoff timing + notochord germ-layer trap: BB/BB_Organ_Systems.md §3, §7

DEVELOPMENT — POSTNATAL:
- Infant reflexes: rooting, sucking, Moro, Babinski (normal in infants, abnormal in adults), grasp (palmar)
- Fixed action patterns — innate stereotyped behavior triggered by sign stimulus
- Critical period (narrow, must occur) vs sensitive period (broader, optimal)
- Imprinting (Lorenz) — geese to first moving object (critical period)

METHODS TO STUDY BRAIN:
- EEG — electrical, poor spatial / excellent temporal (ms)
- CT — structure, X-ray
- MRI — structure, magnetic, excellent spatial
- fMRI — function (blood O2), good spatial / moderate temporal
- PET — function (metabolism), moderate spatial / poor temporal
- Lesion studies — behavior after damage
- Tradeoff: high temporal sacrifices spatial and vice versa

NEUROTRANSMITTERS (high yield — dual-home with BB_Nervous_Endocrine §4):
- ACh — memory, muscle, parasympathetic; Alzheimer's (low)
- Dopamine — reward, movement; schizophrenia (high mesolimbic), Parkinson's (low nigrostriatal)
- NE — alertness, arousal, fight/flight; depression (low), anxiety (high)
- Serotonin — mood, sleep, appetite; depression (low), OCD
- GABA — main inhibitory; anxiety (low); enhanced by alcohol/benzos
- Glutamate — main excitatory; LTP, learning; NMDA hypofunction in schizophrenia
- Endorphins — pain reduction; opioids mimic
