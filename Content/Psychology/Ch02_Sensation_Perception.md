# PS Chapter 2 — Sensation and Perception

Scope: Transduction and thresholds; signal detection theory; vision; hearing and vestibular sense; other senses; object recognition and Gestalt principles.

AAMC FC mapping: FC6B (sensation and perception)

Kaplan: Behavioral Sciences Ch 2

---

SENSATION VS PERCEPTION:
- Sensation = detection (transduction)
- Perception = interpretation
- Absolute threshold (50% detection)
- Difference threshold = JND
- Weber's law: ΔI/I = k (constant ratio)
- Subliminal stimuli (below absolute threshold)
- Signal Detection Theory (SDT) 2x2: hit, miss, false alarm, correct rejection
  - Sensitivity vs criterion (response bias)
- Sensory adaptation (decreased firing with constant stimulus)
- Pain does NOT adapt (exception)

VISION:
- Anatomy: cornea (most refraction) → pupil/iris → lens (accommodation) → retina
- Rods: dim light, periphery, rhodopsin, scotopic, no color
- Cones: bright light, fovea (highest acuity, 1:1 ganglion ratio), photopic, color
- Trichromatic (Young-Helmholtz, retinal level, R/G/B cones)
- Opponent-process (Hering, ganglion/LGN, R-G/B-Y/B-W)
- Pathway: retina → optic nerve → optic chiasm → LGN (thalamus) → V1 (occipital)
- Feature detectors (Hubel & Wiesel)
- Parallel processing (color, motion, form, depth simultaneously)

HEARING AND VESTIBULAR:
- Outer (pinna, canal, tympanic membrane)
- Middle (ossicles: malleus, incus, stapes; oval window)
- Inner (cochlea, basilar membrane, hair cells)
- Pathway through MGN of thalamus to auditory cortex (temporal lobe)
- Place theory: high freq → base, low freq → apex
- Frequency theory + volley principle (low frequencies)
- Conductive vs sensorineural hearing loss
- Vestibular: semicircular canals (rotation, 3 planes), otolith organs (utricle/saccule, linear/gravity)

OTHER SENSES:
- Somatosensation receptors:
  - Meissner (light touch)
  - Pacinian (deep pressure/vibration)
  - Merkel (sustained pressure)
  - Free nerve endings (pain/temp)
- Gate control theory of pain (Melzack & Wall) — large fibers close gate; endorphins close gate
- Taste: 5 basics (sweet, sour, salty, bitter, umami); CN VII, IX, X
- Olfaction: bypasses thalamus → directly to cortex/limbic (emotional memory connection)

PERCEPTION:
- Bottom-up (data-driven) vs top-down (knowledge-driven)
- Gestalt principles: proximity, similarity, closure, continuity, figure-ground, common fate
- Depth cues:
  - Binocular: convergence, retinal disparity
  - Monocular: relative size, interposition, linear perspective, texture gradient, motion parallax, aerial perspective
