# PS Chapter 6 — Identity and Personality

Scope: Self-concept, self-esteem, self-efficacy, locus of control; looking-glass self; Erikson's 8 stages; Kohlberg moral development; Freud's psychosexual stages; personality theories.

AAMC FC mapping: FC8A (self-concept), FC8B (identity), FC8C (personality)

Kaplan: Behavioral Sciences Ch 6

---

SELF-CONCEPT AND IDENTITY:
- Self-concept (overall understanding of self)
- Self-schema (organized cognitive structure for one aspect)
- Self-esteem (evaluation of self-worth)
- Self-efficacy (Bandura, specific situation)
- Locus of control (Rotter): internal vs external
- Social identity, ethnic identity, national identity, gender/sex identity

FORMATION OF IDENTITY (symbolic interactionism):
- Looking-glass self (Cooley) — identity from how we think others see us
  - 3 steps: imagine appearance, imagine judgment, develop feeling
- Mead — I (spontaneous) and Me (socialized)
  - Role-taking stages: preparatory (imitation), play (specific roles), game (multiple roles, generalized other)
- Reference groups
- Total institutions (Goffman) — prisons, military, monasteries; resocialization

ERIKSON'S 8 STAGES:
1. Trust vs Mistrust (0-1)
2. Autonomy vs Shame/Doubt (1-3)
3. Initiative vs Guilt (3-6)
4. Industry vs Inferiority (6-12)
5. Identity vs Role Confusion (12-18) [most tested]
6. Intimacy vs Isolation (young adult)
7. Generativity vs Stagnation (middle adult)
8. Integrity vs Despair (late adult)

KOHLBERG MORAL DEVELOPMENT:
- Preconventional (1: punishment; 2: self-interest)
- Conventional (3: good boy/girl; 4: law and order)
- Postconventional (5: social contract; 6: universal ethics)
- Heinz dilemma — reasoning matters, not answer
- Gilligan critique (gender bias)

FREUD PSYCHOSEXUAL:
- Oral (0-1) — fixation: smoking, overeating
- Anal (1-3) — fixation: retentive (orderly) or expulsive (messy)
- Phallic (3-6) — Oedipus/Electra complex
- Latency (6-12)
- Genital (12+)

VYGOTSKY (sociocultural):
- ZPD, scaffolding, MKO

PERSONALITY — PSYCHOANALYTIC (Freud):
- Id (pleasure principle)
- Ego (reality principle)
- Superego (morality)
- Defense mechanisms:
  - Repression
  - Denial
  - Projection
  - Displacement
  - Sublimation (most mature)
  - Rationalization
  - Reaction formation
  - Regression

NEO-FREUDIANS:
- Jung: collective unconscious, archetypes
- Adler: inferiority complex
- Horney: basic anxiety

HUMANISTIC:
- Rogers: unconditional positive regard, conditions of worth, incongruence
- Maslow: self-actualization

TRAIT THEORIES:
- Allport (cardinal/central/secondary)
- Eysenck PEN model
- Big Five OCEAN (Openness, Conscientiousness, Extraversion, Agreeableness, Neuroticism) — factor analysis

SOCIAL COGNITIVE (Bandura):
- Reciprocal determinism (behavior ↔ cognition ↔ environment)
- Self-efficacy

BIOLOGICAL:
- Temperament (innate behavioral style)
- Twin studies (MZ vs DZ)

BEHAVIORIST:
- Skinner (personality = patterns of behavior shaped by reinforcement)
