# PS Chapter 7 — Psychological Disorders

Scope: DSM-5 classification; major disorder categories; biological basis of NS disorders (Alzheimer's, Parkinson's, Huntington's, ALS); psychotherapy and biomedical treatments.

AAMC FC mapping: FC9A (psychological disorders)

Kaplan: Behavioral Sciences Ch 7

---

UNDERSTANDING DISORDERS:
- DSM-5 classification (descriptive criteria)
- Biomedical vs biopsychosocial (MCAT-preferred) models
- Rates of disorders (~50% lifetime)
- Deinstitutionalization

ANXIETY DISORDERS:
- GAD (6+ months excessive worry)
- Specific phobia
- Social anxiety disorder
- Panic disorder + agoraphobia
- Separation anxiety disorder

OCD (own DSM-5 category, not anxiety):
- Obsessions + compulsions

TRAUMA-RELATED:
- PTSD: intrusion, avoidance, negative cognition/mood, hyperarousal

SOMATIC SYMPTOM:
- Somatic symptom disorder
- Illness anxiety disorder

DEPRESSIVE:
- MDD (5+ symptoms, 2+ weeks): anhedonia, sleep, appetite, concentration, SI
- Persistent depressive disorder (dysthymia, 2+ years)
- Premenstrual dysphoric disorder (PMDD)
- Biology: serotonin/NE deficiency

BIPOLAR:
- Bipolar I (full mania + depression)
- Bipolar II (hypomania + depression)

SCHIZOPHRENIA:
- Positive: hallucinations (usually auditory), delusions (paranoid/grandiose/referential), disorganized speech/behavior
- Negative: flat affect, avolition, alogia, anhedonia, social withdrawal
- Cognitive: working memory, executive function deficits
- Dopamine hypothesis (mesolimbic ↑ for positive; mesocortical ↓ for negative)
- Glutamate hypothesis (NMDA hypofunction; PCP mimics)

DISSOCIATIVE:
- DID
- Dissociative amnesia
- Depersonalization/derealization

PERSONALITY DISORDERS:
- Cluster A (odd/eccentric): paranoid, schizoid, schizotypal
- Cluster B (dramatic/emotional): antisocial, borderline, histrionic, narcissistic
- Cluster C (anxious/fearful): avoidant, dependent, OCPD (≠ OCD)

EATING DISORDERS:
- Anorexia nervosa
- Bulimia nervosa

NEURODEVELOPMENTAL:
- ADHD (stimulants paradoxically calming)
- Autism spectrum

BIOLOGICAL BASIS OF NS DISORDERS:
- Alzheimer's: amyloid plaques, tau tangles, ACh deficit; cholinesterase inhibitors
- Parkinson's: dopamine loss in substantia nigra; tremor/rigidity/bradykinesia; L-DOPA
- Huntington's: autosomal dominant, caudate/putamen degeneration, chorea
- ALS: motor neuron degeneration

TREATMENT:
- Psychotherapy types:
  - CBT (most empirical support)
  - Psychoanalytic / psychodynamic
  - Humanistic (Rogerian)
  - Behavioral therapy (exposure, systematic desensitization)
  - Group therapy
  - Family therapy
- Biomedical:
  - SSRIs (1st line for depression, anxiety)
  - SNRIs
  - Tricyclic antidepressants (TCAs)
  - MAOIs (tyramine restriction)
  - Antipsychotics: typical (D2; EPS) vs atypical (D2 + 5-HT2A; metabolic SE)
  - Mood stabilizers — lithium (bipolar)
  - Anxiolytics — benzodiazepines (GABA enhancement)
  - ECT (treatment-resistant depression, severe mania, catatonia)
- Combination therapy strongest evidence base
