# PS Chapter 8 — Social Processes, Attitudes, and Behavior

Scope: Group psychology; conformity (Asch) and obedience (Milgram); socialization agents and types; deviance theories; attitudes (ABC model, ELM, cognitive dissonance); persuasion and compliance techniques.

AAMC FC mapping: FC8D (group psychology), FC9B (socialization), FC8E (attitudes)

Kaplan: Behavioral Sciences Ch 8

---

GROUP BEHAVIOR:
- Social facilitation (Zajonc): easy improved, hard impaired
- Social loafing (less effort in groups)
- Deindividuation (loss of self-awareness)
- Bystander effect (diffusion of responsibility, pluralistic ignorance)
- Group polarization (extremes amplified)
- Groupthink (Janis): conformity suppresses dissent
- Peer pressure

CONFORMITY AND OBEDIENCE:
- Asch line experiments (~33% conformity rate; 75% conform at least once)
- Normative (to fit in) vs informational (think they're right) influence
- Milgram (65% to 450V; obedience to authority)

REFERENCE GROUPS

NORMS:
- Folkways (informal)
- Mores (moral)
- Taboos (strongly prohibited)
- Laws (codified)

SANCTIONS:
- Positive vs negative
- Formal vs informal

AGENTS OF SOCIALIZATION:
- Family, peers, media, education, workplace, religion

TYPES OF SOCIALIZATION:
- Primary (early)
- Secondary (group/institution-specific)
- Anticipatory (future role)
- Resocialization (boot camp, prison, conversion)

DEVIANCE THEORIES:
- Differential association (Sutherland) — learned through interaction
- Labeling theory (Becker) — primary vs secondary deviance, stigma, master status
- Strain theory (Merton): conformity, innovation, ritualism, retreatism, rebellion
- Control theory (social bonds prevent deviance)
- Conflict theory (powerful define deviance)

ATTITUDES — ABC:
- Affective, behavioral, cognitive
- Attitude-behavior consistency limited (LaPiere study)

ELABORATION LIKELIHOOD MODEL (Petty & Cacioppo):
- Central route (careful, lasting)
- Peripheral route (superficial, temporary)

COGNITIVE DISSONANCE (Festinger):
- Attitude-behavior conflict → discomfort → attitude change
- $1 vs $20 boring task study (insufficient justification → attitude shift)

PERSUASION/COMPLIANCE:
- Foot-in-the-door
- Door-in-the-face
- Lowball
- That's-not-all
- Role of social media (echo chambers, viral persuasion)
