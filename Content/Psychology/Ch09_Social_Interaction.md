# PS Chapter 9 — Social Interaction

Scope: Status (achieved, ascribed, master); roles (conflict, strain, exit); groups; social networks and social capital; organizations and bureaucracy; self-presentation and Goffman's dramaturgical approach; communication and emotion detection.

AAMC FC mapping: FC9C (social interaction), FC8F (self-presentation)

Kaplan: Behavioral Sciences Ch 9

---

STATUS:
- Achieved (earned) vs ascribed (birth)
- Master status (dominates other statuses)

ROLES:
- Role conflict (between different roles)
- Role strain (within one role)
- Role exit (Ebaugh) — 4 stages: doubt, search, action, new identity

GROUPS:
- Primary (close, intimate, lasting)
- Secondary (formal, task-oriented)
- In-group / out-group
- Reference groups

NETWORKS / SOCIAL CAPITAL:
- Social network
- Weak ties (Granovetter) — best for info flow
- Social capital (resources via relationships)

ORGANIZATIONS / BUREAUCRACY:
- Bureaucracy (Weber's ideal type): division of labor, hierarchy, formal rules, impersonality, qualifications
- Iron law of oligarchy (Michels)
- McDonaldization (Ritzer): efficiency, calculability, predictability, control
- Characteristics of formal organizations (explicit goals, formal structure, division of labor)

SELF-PRESENTATION:
- Impression management (ingratiation, self-promotion, intimidation, exemplification, supplication)
- Goffman dramaturgical: front stage vs back stage

COMMUNICATION:
- Verbal vs nonverbal
- Eye contact (cultural variation)
- Body language, proxemics
- Animal communication (signals, gestures, displays, song)

EXPRESSING/DETECTING EMOTION:
- Universal facial expressions (Ekman)
