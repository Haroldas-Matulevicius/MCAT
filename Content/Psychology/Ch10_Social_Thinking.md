# PS Chapter 10 — Social Thinking and Behavior

Scope: Attraction, aggression, and attachment (Bowlby, Ainsworth); altruism; attribution theory (FAE, actor-observer bias, self-serving bias); stereotypes, prejudice, and discrimination; stereotype threat; contact hypothesis.

AAMC FC mapping: FC9D (social behavior), FC9E (social perception and behavior), FC10A (stereotypes/prejudice)

Kaplan: Behavioral Sciences Ch 10

---

ATTRACTION:
- Proximity / mere exposure (Zajonc)
- Similarity
- Reciprocity
- Physical attractiveness (halo effect)

AGGRESSION:
- Biological: testosterone, amygdala, low serotonin, genetics
- Frustration-aggression hypothesis (Dollard)
- Social learning theory (Bandura, Bobo doll)

ATTACHMENT:
- Bowlby — innate, monotropy, secure base, contact comfort (Harlow), critical period
- Ainsworth Strange Situation:
  - Secure (~70%) — distressed at separation, comforted by return
  - Avoidant (~20%) — little distress, ignores caregiver
  - Anxious-ambivalent (~10%) — very distressed, not easily comforted
  - Disorganized (~5%) — confused/contradictory, fearful

ALTRUISM:
- Empathy-altruism hypothesis (Batson)
- Kin selection (Hamilton)
- Reciprocal altruism (Trivers)
- Inclusive fitness
- Social exchange theory
- Social support types: emotional, instrumental, informational, appraisal

ATTRIBUTION THEORY (Heider):
- Dispositional (internal) vs situational (external)
- Fundamental attribution error (overattribute others to disposition)
- Actor-observer bias (self → situation, others → disposition)
- Self-serving bias (success internal, failure external)
- Halo effect

CULTURAL DIFFERENCES:
- Individualistic (more FAE, dispositional)
- Collectivistic (more situational)

STEREOTYPES, PREJUDICE, DISCRIMINATION:
- Stereotype = cognitive
- Prejudice = affective
- Discrimination = behavioral
- Individual vs institutional discrimination
- Stereotype threat (Steele & Aronson) — anxiety impairs performance
- Self-fulfilling prophecy (Merton; Pygmalion — Rosenthal & Jacobson)
- Ethnocentrism vs cultural relativism
- In-group bias, out-group homogeneity
- Scapegoat theory
- Contact hypothesis (Allport): equal status, common goals, cooperation, authority support reduces prejudice
