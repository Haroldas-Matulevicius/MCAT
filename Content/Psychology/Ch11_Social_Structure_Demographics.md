# PS Chapter 11 — Social Structure and Demographics

Scope: Core sociological theories; social institutions (education, family, religion, government, economy, healthcare); culture; demographic transition; Malthusian theory; population pyramids; fertility/mortality measures; migration; social movements; globalization; urbanization.

AAMC FC mapping: FC10B (social institutions), FC10C (culture), FC10D (demographics) — major Kaplan gap, supplement with Khan Academy and 300-page P/S doc

Kaplan: Behavioral Sciences Ch 11

---

SOCIOLOGICAL THEORIES:
- Functionalism (Durkheim, Parsons): society as organism
  - Manifest vs latent functions (Merton)
  - Dysfunctions
  - Mechanical (similar) vs organic (specialized) solidarity
  - Anomie (norm breakdown)
- Conflict theory (Marx): class struggle
  - Bourgeoisie vs proletariat
  - Class consciousness vs false consciousness
  - Weber extension: class + status + power/party + rationalization
- Symbolic interactionism (Mead, Blumer): meaning through interaction, symbols
- Social constructionism (Berger & Luckmann): reality socially constructed (race, gender, mental illness as constructs)
- Feminist theory: gender inequality, intersectionality
- Exchange / rational choice theory: cost-benefit

SOCIAL INSTITUTIONS:
- Education:
  - Manifest (knowledge, credentials) vs latent (childcare, networks, marriage market)
  - Hidden curriculum
  - Pygmalion / teacher expectancy (Rosenthal & Jacobson)
  - Tracking
  - Educational stratification
- Family:
  - Kinship
  - Forms: nuclear, extended, single-parent, blended
  - Endogamy / exogamy
- Religion:
  - Types: church, sect, cult/NRM
  - Secularization
  - Fundamentalism
  - Religiosity
- Government / politics:
  - Weber's authority types: traditional, charismatic, rational-legal
  - Power
- Economy:
  - Capitalism vs socialism
  - Division of labor (Durkheim)
- Healthcare:
  - Medicalization (normal conditions become medical)
  - Sick role (Parsons): rights (exempt, not blamed) + obligations (try to recover, seek help)
  - Illness experience
  - Social epidemiology (full treatment in Ch 12)

CULTURE:
- Elements: symbols, language, values, beliefs, norms, rituals, material objects
- Material vs symbolic/non-material culture
- Culture lag (Ogburn) — tech > norms
- Culture shock (honeymoon, frustration, adjustment, acceptance)
- Assimilation
- Multiculturalism / pluralism
- Subcultures (don't oppose dominant) vs countercultures (oppose)
- Cultural transmission
- Evolution of culture

DEMOGRAPHIC TRANSITION:
1. Pre-industrial: high birth, high death
2. Transitional: high birth, declining death (rapid growth)
3. Industrial: declining birth, low death
4. Post-industrial: low birth, low death
- Possible Stage 5 (decline)

MALTHUSIAN THEORY:
- Population grows exponentially
- Food grows arithmetically
- Positive checks (famine, war, disease) vs preventive checks
- Failed to predict tech advances + transition

POPULATION PYRAMIDS:
- Expansive (wide base, developing)
- Constrictive (narrow base, aging)
- Stationary (stable)

FERTILITY/MORTALITY:
- Crude birth rate (per 1000/year)
- Total fertility rate (TFR; replacement ~2.1)
- Age-specific fertility rate
- Crude mortality rate
- Infant mortality rate (key health indicator)
- Age-specific mortality
- Life expectancy

MIGRATION:
- Push (war, poverty) vs pull (opportunity) factors
- Immigration, emigration, internal migration

SOCIAL MOVEMENTS:
- Relative deprivation
- Reform, revolutionary, reactionary, new social movements

GLOBALIZATION:
- Positive (growth, exchange) vs negative (homogenization, exploitation, inequality)

URBANIZATION:
- Urbanization
- Suburbanization (post-WWII, white flight)
- Gentrification (displacement)
- Urban decline / blight
