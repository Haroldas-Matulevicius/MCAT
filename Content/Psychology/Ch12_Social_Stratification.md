# PS Chapter 12 — Social Stratification and Health Disparities

Scope: Stratification theories; social class and SES; social mobility; poverty; cultural and social capital; intersectionality; epidemiology measures; health and healthcare disparities; social determinants of health; environmental justice; global health; second sickness.

AAMC FC mapping: FC10E (social class), FC10F (health disparities) — Kaplan gap, growing in MCAT emphasis

Kaplan: Behavioral Sciences Ch 12

---

STRATIFICATION:
- Davis-Moore (functional necessity)
- Conflict theory (exploitation)
- Weber: class + status + party (independent dimensions)

SOCIAL CLASS:
- Working / middle / upper

SES = income + education + occupation
- Single best predictor of life outcomes

SOCIAL MOBILITY:
- Intergenerational (across generations) vs intragenerational (within lifetime)
- Vertical vs horizontal
- Meritocracy vs structural barriers

POVERTY:
- Absolute (basic needs unmet) vs relative
- Social exclusion

CULTURAL CAPITAL (Bourdieu):
- Embodied (knowledge, skills, dispositions)
- Objectified (books, art, instruments)
- Institutionalized (degrees, credentials)
- Reproduces class inequality

SOCIAL CAPITAL:
- Resources via networks/relationships

CLASS CONSCIOUSNESS vs FALSE CONSCIOUSNESS (Marx)

INTERSECTIONALITY (Crenshaw):
- Overlapping identities create unique experiences

EPIDEMIOLOGY MEASURES:
- Incidence (NEW cases / population at risk × time)
- Prevalence (TOTAL cases / population at one time)
- Prevalence = Incidence × duration
- Mortality (death rate)
- Morbidity (disease state/rate)

SOCIAL EPIDEMIOLOGY:
- How social factors influence disease distribution

HEALTH DISPARITIES:
- By class (lower SES → worse outcomes)
- By gender (women: depression, autoimmune; men: CVD, suicide)
- By race (Black Americans: hypertension, infant mortality, maternal mortality — persists after SES control)

HEALTHCARE DISPARITIES:
- Lack of insurance
- Geographic barriers (rural, "healthcare deserts")
- Language barriers
- Implicit bias
- Lack of culturally competent care
- Cost barriers

SOCIAL DETERMINANTS OF HEALTH:
- SES (dominant)
- Education
- Neighborhood / physical environment (food deserts, pollution)
- Discrimination and racism (weathering hypothesis — Geronimus, accelerated aging)
- Social support / networks

ENVIRONMENTAL JUSTICE:
- Environmental racism (pollution sources in low-income communities of color)

GLOBAL HEALTH:
- Wealthy: chronic disease (CVD, cancer, diabetes)
- Poor: infectious, maternal/child mortality, undernutrition
- Middle-income: "double burden"

SECOND SICKNESS:
- Illness from inadequate societal/healthcare response (stigma, financial ruin, isolation, provider discrimination)
- Particularly mental illness, HIV, addiction
