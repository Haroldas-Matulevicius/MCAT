# Cell Division Mnemonics

## Cell Cycle Phases: "Go Sally Go! Make Children!"
- G1, S, G2, M, Cytokinesis (see Heredity_Evolution file for detail)

---

## Mitosis Phases: "PMAT" or "I Pee on the MAT"
- Prophase, Metaphase, Anaphase, Telophase (see Heredity_Evolution file for detail)

---

## Cyclin/CDK Pairs: "DEAB 4221"
- Cyclin **D** - CDK**4** = drives G1 progression past restriction point
- Cyclin **E** - CDK**2** = drives G1/S transition
- Cyclin **A** - CDK**2** = drives S phase and S/G2 transition
- Cyclin **B** - CDK**1** = drives entry into M phase (= MPF, maturation-promoting factor)

---

## Oncogenes vs. Tumor Suppressors: "Gas Pedal vs. Brake"
- **Oncogenes** = gas pedal stuck ON (gain of function, dominant -- one mutant allele is enough)
  - Examples: Ras, Myc, Src
- **Tumor suppressors** = brakes that fail (loss of function, recessive -- need both alleles knocked out)
  - Examples: p53 ("guardian of the genome"), Rb (retinoblastoma protein)
  - p53 acts at G1/S checkpoint; Rb binds E2F to prevent S phase entry

---

## Knudson's "Two-Hit Hypothesis"
- Tumor suppressors need **two hits** (both alleles lost)
  - Inherited: one hit inherited + one somatic
  - Sporadic: both hits somatic
- Oncogenes need only **one hit** (dominant gain of function)
- Originally described for retinoblastoma (Rb gene)

---

## Apoptosis Pathways: "Intrinsic = Internal, Extrinsic = External"
- **In**trinsic = **In**side the cell (mitochondrial pathway)
  - DNA damage -> Bax/Bak permeabilize outer mitochondrial membrane -> cytochrome c released -> apoptosome (with Apaf-1) -> caspase-9 -> executioner caspases (3, 6, 7)
  - Anti-apoptotic: Bcl-2, Bcl-XL (inhibit intrinsic pathway)
- **Ex**trinsic = **Ex**ternal signal (death receptor pathway)
  - Fas ligand binds Fas receptor -> caspase-8 -> executioner caspases
