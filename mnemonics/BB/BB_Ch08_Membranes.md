## Fluid Mosaic
- **Fluid** (lipids/proteins move laterally) + **Mosaic** (patchwork of proteins)
- Asymmetric: **PS (phosphatidylserine) on INNER leaflet** normally → flips to outer in apoptosis ("eat me")

## What Crosses the Bilayer?
- **Yes**: small nonpolar (O2, CO2, N2), small polar (H2O — slowly, urea, ethanol)
- **No**: ions, glucose, amino acids (need transporters)

## Cholesterol — Fluidity BUFFER (not simple up/down)
- High T → REDUCES fluidity (rings restrict motion)
- Low T → PREVENTS crystallization (disrupts tight packing)
- Net: broadens functional temperature range

## Fluidity Factors
- Unsaturated FAs → MORE fluid (cis kinks)
- Shorter chains → MORE fluid (fewer vdW)
- Higher T → MORE fluid
- Saturated FAs → LESS fluid

## Na+/K+ ATPase — "3 Out, 2 In"
- **3 Na+ out, 2 K+ in per ATP** — electrogenic (-1 inside)
- Contributes to resting membrane potential; powers all secondary active transport

## Transport Types
- **Primary active** = direct ATP (Na/K, SERCA, H/K in stomach)
- **Secondary active** = Na+ gradient drives cotransport (SGLT = symport; NCX = antiport)
- **Facilitated** = protein-mediated, no ATP, down gradient (GLUT)
- **Simple diffusion** = bilayer only

## Tonicity
- **Hyper**tonic → cell **shrinks** (crenation)
- **Hypo**tonic → cell **swells** (lyses if RBC)
- "Hypo = low solute = cell swells in the hypO"

## Endocytosis Types
- **Phago**cytosis = cell **eating** (large solid particles: bacteria, dead cells)
- **Pino**cytosis = cell **drinking** (non-selective fluid uptake)
- **Receptor-mediated** = **selective** uptake (e.g., LDL via clathrin-coated pits)
- Greek roots: phagein = to eat; pinein = to drink

## Cell Junction Proteins
- **T**ight junctions = **C**laudins + **O**ccludins (seal the space)
- **A**dherens + **D**esmosomes = **C**adherins
- **G**ap junctions = **C**onnexins (6 connexins = 1 connexon; 2 connexons = 1 channel)
- **H**emidesmosomes = **I**ntegrins (to basement membrane)

Cytoskeletal attachments:
- **A**dherens junctions anchor **A**ctin
- **D**esmosomes anchor interme**D**iate filaments

## Cell Junction Order (Apical to Basal): "Tight on Top, Adherens After, Desmosomes Down, Gap Go anywhere"
1. Tight junctions (most apical -- seal)
2. Adherens junctions (belt of actin)
3. Desmosomes (spot welds, anchor IFs)
4. Gap junctions (anywhere -- communication channels)

## SNARE proteins
- **v-SNARE** on **v**esicle; **t-SNARE** on **t**arget membrane
- Botulinum toxin cleaves SNAREs → flaccid paralysis (no ACh release)
