## Metabolic State Hormones
- **Fed** → **Insulin** (β-cells): STORE (glycogenesis, lipogenesis, protein synthesis)
- **Fasting** → **Glucagon** (α-cells): MOBILIZE (glycogenolysis, gluconeogenesis, β-ox)
- **Starvation** → Glucagon + **Cortisol**: BREAK DOWN (proteolysis, ketogenesis)
- **Stress/exercise** → **Epinephrine** (adrenal medulla): like glucagon + muscle effect

## Insulin vs Glucagon (Reciprocal)
"**Insulin In, Glucagon Gone**" — Insulin stores inside cells; Glucagon releases to blood

## Key Metabolic Switches
- **F-2,6-BP** = glycolysis vs gluconeogenesis switch (via PFK-2)
  - Insulin ↑ F-2,6-BP → activates PFK-1 → glycolysis ON
  - Glucagon ↓ F-2,6-BP → activates F-1,6-BPase → gluconeogenesis ON
- **Malonyl-CoA** = FA synthesis vs β-oxidation switch
  - Insulin → ACC ON → malonyl-CoA ↑ → blocks CPT-I → no β-ox
  - Glucagon → ACC OFF → malonyl-CoA ↓ → CPT-I free → β-ox ON
- **Citrate** = crossover signal (inhibits PFK-1 in mito; activates ACC in cytoplasm)
- **Acetyl-CoA** → activates pyruvate carboxylase (links fat breakdown to gluconeogenesis)

## Tissue Rules (MUST Know)
- **Liver**: metabolic hub; makes ketones, can't use them (no thiophorase); has G6Pase
- **Muscle**: no G6Pase → can't export glucose; stores glycogen for self
- **Brain**: obligate glucose normally; adapts to ketones after 3-5 days starvation
- **RBC**: glycolysis only (no mito); makes lactate
- **Adipose**: lacks glycerol kinase → glycerol goes to liver
- **Heart**: aerobic; prefers FAs > ketones > lactate > glucose

## Starvation Timeline
1. 0-4 h: fed
2. 4-18 h: glycogenolysis
3. 18-48 h: glycogenolysis ↓ + gluconeogenesis ↑
4. Days 2-7: peak AA gluconeogenesis + ketogenesis ramps
5. Weeks 2+: ketones dominant, protein spared

## ATP Accounting Per Glucose
- Glycolysis: 2 ATP + 2 NADH
- 2× PDC: 2 NADH
- 2× TCA: 6 NADH + 2 FADH2 + 2 GTP
- OxPhos (NADH×2.5, FADH2×1.5): ~26 more ATP
- **Grand total: ~30-32 ATP** (32 with malate-aspartate shuttle; 30 with glycerol-3-P)

## Thermo Rule
- ΔG = ΔH − TΔS
- "**Gibbs Gives Go**" — ΔG < 0 → spontaneous
- ATP hydrolysis ΔG° ≈ -7.3 kcal/mol (but cellular ~-12 due to ratios)

## Redox Carriers
- **NADH → ATP** (catabolic, to ETC)
- **FADH2 → ATP** (catabolic, lower yield)
- **NADPH → biosynthesis** (FA synth, cholesterol, glutathione, respiratory burst, P450)

## High-Yield "Exceptions" to Know
1. FAs → glucose? NO (except odd-chain via succinyl-CoA)
2. Muscle exports glucose? NO (no G6Pase)
3. Liver uses ketones? NO (no thiophorase)
4. Brain uses FAs? NO (BBB + no β-ox)
5. RBC uses anything but glucose? NO (no mito)
