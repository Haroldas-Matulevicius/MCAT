# Gene Expression Mnemonics

## RNA Polymerase Products: "RMT" (I, II, III)
- RNA Pol **I** = **r**RNA (most abundant RNA)
- RNA Pol **II** = **m**RNA (and some snRNAs)
- RNA Pol **III** = **t**RNA (and 5S rRNA)
- "RMT sounds like 'are empty'" -- the Roman numerals need R-M-T to fill them
- Note: Prokaryotes have a single RNA polymerase (no I/II/III distinction)

---

## Purines vs. Pyrimidines
- **"CUT the PY"** = Pyrimidines are **C**ytosine, **U**racil, **T**hymine (1 ring, smaller)
- **"Pure As Gold"** = Purines are **A**denine, **G**uanine (2 rings, larger)
- Counter-intuitive: pyrimidine is a longer word but has fewer rings
- Base pairing: A-T (2 H-bonds), G-C (3 H-bonds). In RNA: A-U

---

## Stop Codons: "U Are Annoying, U Are Gone, U Go Away"
- **UAA**, **UAG**, **UGA** -- all start with U
- No amino acid encoded; release factors bind instead
- Only start codon: **AUG** (methionine)

---

## Blotting: "SNoW DRoP"
- **S**outhern = **D**NA
- **N**orthern = **R**NA
- **W**estern = **P**rotein
- Southern named after Edwin Southern; Northern and Western are compass-direction puns

---

## Ribosome Sites: "APE"
- **A** = Aminoacyl site (charged tRNA enters)
- **P** = Peptidyl site (peptide bond forms, growing chain sits here)
- **E** = Exit site (empty tRNA leaves)
- "An ape swings through: Arrives, does Peptide work, Exits"

---

## DNA Replication Enzymes: "Happy Teens Party Partying Late"
- **H**elicase (unwind) -> **T**opoisomerase/SSBs (relieve supercoiling, protect) -> **P**rimase (lay RNA primer) -> DNA **P**ol III (synthesize, 5'->3', proofreads 3'->5') -> DNA Pol I (remove primers, fill gaps) -> **L**igase (seal nicks between Okazaki fragments)

---

## Gel Electrophoresis: "Small Runs Far" + "Run to Red"
- Smaller fragments migrate faster/farther
- DNA is negatively charged (phosphate backbone) -> runs toward positive electrode (anode, red)
- SDS-PAGE: SDS gives all proteins uniform negative charge -> separation by size only

---

## Operon Regulation
- **LAC** = "Lacking" = normally **OFF** (inducible). Allolactose removes repressor -> transcription ON
- **TRP** = "Tripping over product" = normally **ON** (repressible). Excess Trp activates repressor -> transcription OFF
- Rule: Catabolic pathways = inducible; Anabolic pathways = repressible

### Operon Structure: "POS"
- **P**romoter (RNA polymerase binds) -> **O**perator (repressor binds) -> **S**tructural genes (protein-coding)

---

## PCR Steps: "DAE" at 95-55-72
- **D**enature (~95C): separate strands
- **A**nneal (~55C): primers bind
- **E**xtend (~72C): Taq polymerase synthesizes
- Temperatures go DOWN then UP (valley shape)
- 2^n copies after n cycles
