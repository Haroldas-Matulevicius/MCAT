# Heredity & Evolution Mnemonics

## Cell Cycle: "Go Sally Go! Make Children!"
- **G**o = **G1** (first gap; cell grows)
- **S**ally = **S** phase (DNA synthesis/replication)
- **G**o = **G2** (second gap; prepares for mitosis)
- **M**ake = **M** phase (mitosis)
- **C**hildren = **C**ytokinesis (cytoplasm divides)
- G0 = quiescent phase (cells exit the cycle)

---

## Mitosis Phases: "PMAT" or "I Pee on the MAT"
- **P**rophase: chromosomes condense, spindle forms, nuclear envelope dissolves
- **M**etaphase: chromosomes align at the **M**iddle
- **A**naphase: chromatids move **A**part
- **T**elophase: cell is **T**orn in two, nuclear envelope reforms

---

## Meiosis: "PMAT x 2"
- Two rounds of PMAT. "Meiosis I is FUN, Meiosis II is the CLONE"
- **Meiosis I** (reductional, 2n -> n): unique events happen here
  - Prophase I: crossing over at chiasmata
  - Metaphase I: independent assortment (random orientation of bivalents)
  - Anaphase I: homologs separate
- **Meiosis II** (equational): basically mitosis -- sister chromatids separate

---

## Sources of Genetic Variation: "CIR"
- **C**rossing over (Prophase I)
- **I**ndependent assortment (Metaphase I) -- 2^23 = ~8.4 million combinations per parent
- **R**andom fertilization (any sperm + any egg)

---

## Mendelian Laws: "SID"
- **S**egregation: alleles separate during gamete formation (Anaphase I/II)
- **I**ndependent assortment: genes on different chromosomes sort independently (Metaphase I)
- **D**ominance: one allele can mask the other in heterozygotes
- Note: Linked genes (same chromosome) violate independent assortment unless crossing over occurs

---

## Non-Mendelian Inheritance (Word Roots)
- **IN**complete dominance = **IN**termediate phenotype (red x white = pink)
- **CO**-dominance = **CO**-expression (red x white = red AND white patches, e.g., AB blood type)
- **Epistasis** = one gene **SITS** on another (one gene masks a different gene's expression)
- **Pleiotropy** = **PLENTY** of effects (one gene -> many traits; e.g., sickle cell)
- **Polygenic** = **POLY** genes (many genes -> one trait; e.g., height, skin color)

---

## Hardy-Weinberg

### Equations
- Allele: **p + q = 1** (p = dominant, q = recessive)
- Genotype: **p^2 + 2pq + q^2 = 1** (p^2 = homozygous dominant, 2pq = het, q^2 = homozygous recessive)
- **Start with q^2** (recessive phenotype frequency) -> sqrt -> q -> p = 1-q -> solve

### Violations: "Maggie May Does Not Smoke" (MMDNS)
- **M**utations
- **M**igration (gene flow)
- **D**rift (genetic drift -- small populations)
- **N**on-random mating
- **S**election (natural selection)
- If ANY occur, allele frequencies change (evolution is happening)

---

## Natural Selection Types (Graph Shapes)
- **Stabilizing** = bell curve gets NARROWER (extremes selected against, mean stays same; e.g., birth weight)
- **Directional** = bell curve SHIFTS sideways (one extreme favored; e.g., antibiotic resistance)
- **Disruptive** = bell SPLITS into two humps (both extremes favored, middle selected against; e.g., beak size)

---

## Genetic Drift: "Bottleneck = Disaster, Founder = Departure"
- **Bottleneck**: catastrophic event kills most of population randomly -> survivors have reduced genetic diversity
- **Founder effect**: small group leaves and colonizes new area -> carries only a subset of gene pool
- Both are random (non-selective), strongest in SMALL populations, reduce genetic variation
