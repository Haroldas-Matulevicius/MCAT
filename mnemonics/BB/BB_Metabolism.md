# Metabolism Mnemonics

## Glycolysis Intermediates: "Goodness Gracious, Father Franklin Did Go By Picking Pumpkins (to) Prepare Pies"
- **G**oodness = **G**lucose
- **G**racious = **G**lucose-6-phosphate
- **F**ather = **F**ructose-6-phosphate
- **F**ranklin = **F**ructose-1,6-bisphosphate
- **D**id = **D**ihydroxyacetone phosphate (DHAP)
- **G**o = **G**lyceraldehyde-3-phosphate (G3P)
- **B**y = **1,3-B**isphosphoglycerate
- **P**icking = **3-P**hosphoglycerate
- **P**umpkins = **2-P**hosphoglycerate
- **P**repare = **P**hosphoenolpyruvate (PEP)
- **P**ies = **P**yruvate

## Glycolysis Enzymes: "Hungry Peter Pan And The Growling Pink Panther Eat Pies"
- **H**ungry = **H**exokinase
- **P**eter = **P**hosphoglucose isomerase
- **P**an = **P**hosphofructokinase (PFK-1)
- **A**nd = **A**ldolase
- **T**he = **T**riose phosphate isomerase
- **G**rowling = **G**lyceraldehyde-3-phosphate dehydrogenase
- **P**ink = **P**hosphoglycerate kinase
- **P**anther = **P**hosphoglycerate mutase
- **E**at = **E**nolase
- **P**ies = **P**yruvate kinase

---

## Irreversible Glycolysis Steps: Steps 1, 3, and 10
From the enzyme mnemonic: **H**ungry (1), **P**an (3), **P**ies (10) -- first, third, and last words.
- Hexokinase (glucose -> G6P)
- PFK-1 (F6P -> F1,6BP) -- rate-limiting step
- Pyruvate kinase (PEP -> pyruvate)
- These 3 are bypassed by different enzymes in gluconeogenesis.

## TCA Cycle Intermediates: "Can I Keep Selling Seashells For Money, Officer?"
- **C**an = **C**itrate
- **I** = **I**socitrate
- **K**eep = alpha-**K**etoglutarate
- **S**elling = **S**uccinyl-CoA
- **S**eashells = **S**uccinate
- **F**or = **F**umarate
- **M**oney = **M**alate
- **O**fficer = **O**xaloacetate

Alternative: "Citrate Is Krebs' Starting Substrate For Making Oxaloacetate"

## ETC Complex Names: "Never Say Q Cannot Achieve"
- Complex I = **N**ADH dehydrogenase (accepts electrons from NADH)
- Complex II = **S**uccinate dehydrogenase (accepts electrons from FADH2; same enzyme as in TCA)
- Complex III = ubi**Q**uinol oxidase / Cytochrome bc1
- Complex IV = **C**ytochrome c oxidase (final electron transfer to O2)
- Complex V = **A**TP synthase

---

## ETC Proton Pumping: "1, 3, 4 pump; 2 does not"
- Complex I: pumps 4 H+
- Complex II: pumps 0 H+ (the odd one out -- not a transmembrane pump)
- Complex III: pumps 4 H+
- Complex IV: pumps 2 H+
- FADH2 yields less ATP than NADH because it enters at Complex II, skipping Complex I's pumping.

---

## ATP Yield per Carrier
- NADH = **2.5** ATP (enters at Complex I, which pumps)
- FADH2 = **1.5** ATP (enters at Complex II, which doesn't pump)
- Total from 1 glucose: 10 NADH (25) + 2 FADH2 (3) + 4 substrate-level = **30-32 ATP**
- Range depends on shuttle: malate-aspartate (liver/heart) = 2.5; glycerol-3-phosphate (muscle/brain) = 1.5

---

## ETC Inhibitors: "Rot At Complex"
- **R**otenone -> Complex I
- **A**ntimycin A -> Complex III
- **C**yanide / CO / azide -> Complex IV
- **O**ligomycin -> ATP synthase (Complex V)
- 2,4-DNP = uncoupler (dissipates proton gradient, NOT an inhibitor)

---

## Gluconeogenesis Bypass Enzymes: "Pathway Produces Fresh Glucose"
- **P**athway = **P**yruvate carboxylase (pyruvate -> OAA, in mitochondria; requires biotin)
- **P**roduces = **P**EP carboxykinase (OAA -> PEP; requires GTP)
- **F**resh = **F**ructose-1,6-bisphosphatase (F1,6BP -> F6P; rate-limiting)
- **G**lucose = **G**lucose-6-phosphatase (G6P -> glucose; only in liver/kidney, NOT muscle)
- 3 irreversible steps need 4 bypass enzymes because pyruvate kinase reversal requires 2 enzymes.

---

## Beta-Oxidation Steps: "OHOT"
- **O**xidation: Acyl-CoA dehydrogenase (FAD -> FADH2)
- **H**ydration: Enoyl-CoA hydratase (adds water across double bond)
- **O**xidation: 3-hydroxyacyl-CoA dehydrogenase (NAD+ -> NADH)
- **T**hiolysis: Thiolase (cleaves 2C acetyl-CoA using CoA-SH)
- Each cycle yields: 1 FADH2 + 1 NADH + 1 acetyl-CoA

---

## PFK-1 Regulation (Rate-Limiting Enzyme of Glycolysis)
- **Activated** when energy is LOW: AMP, ADP, fructose-2,6-bisphosphate (made by PFK-2 under insulin)
- **Inhibited** when energy is HIGH: ATP, citrate (TCA backed up)
- Logic: Low energy -> turn ON glycolysis. High energy -> turn OFF.

---

## Pentose Phosphate Pathway: "PPP = Produces Pentoses and Provides NADPH"
- Oxidative phase: Glucose-6-phosphate -> 2 NADPH + ribulose-5-phosphate
- Non-oxidative phase: ribulose-5-phosphate -> ribose-5-phosphate (for nucleotide synthesis)
- NADPH used for: fatty acid synthesis, cholesterol synthesis, glutathione reduction
- Rate-limiting enzyme: Glucose-6-phosphate dehydrogenase (G6PD)
