# Prokaryotes & Viruses Mnemonics

## Gram Stain: "P = Purple, N = piNk"
- Gram **P**ositive = **P**urple (retain crystal violet; thick peptidoglycan)
- Gram **N**egative = pi**N**k/red (lose crystal violet, take up safranin; thin peptidoglycan + outer membrane)

---

## Gram-Positive vs Gram-Negative Features: "P = Phat peptidoglycan; N = has aN outer membrane"
**Gram-positive:**
- Thick peptidoglycan (~90% of cell wall)
- Teichoic acids
- No outer membrane
- Exotoxins

**Gram-negative:**
- Thin peptidoglycan
- Outer membrane with LPS (endotoxin = Lipid A)
- Periplasmic space
- Porins

---

## Lytic vs Lysogenic: "Lytic = Lyses; Lysogenic = Lying Low"
- **Lytic:** Virus hijacks cell -> replicates -> assembles -> lyses (bursts) host cell -> releases progeny
- **Lysogenic:** Viral DNA integrates as **prophage** -> replicates passively with host -> can be triggered to lytic by stress (UV, DNA damage)

---

## Naked DNA Viruses: "PAPP smear"
- **P**apillomavirus (warts, cervical cancer)
- **A**denovirus (respiratory, conjunctivitis)
- **P**arvovirus (smallest DNA virus, ssDNA)
- **P**olyomavirus (JC, BK viruses)
- All other DNA viruses (herpes, hepadna, pox) are enveloped.

---

## All DNA Virus Families: "HHAPPPy"
- **H**erpes (enveloped, dsDNA)
- **H**epadna (enveloped, partially dsDNA -- Hep B)
- **A**deno (naked, dsDNA)
- **P**ox (enveloped, dsDNA -- largest DNA virus, replicates in CYTOPLASM)
- **P**arvo (naked, ssDNA -- smallest DNA virus)
- **P**apova/Polyoma (naked, dsDNA)
- Rules: All dsDNA except Parvo (ssDNA). All replicate in nucleus except Pox (cytoplasm).

---

## Naked RNA Viruses: "Naked CPR"
- **C**alicivirus (includes norovirus)
- **P**icornavirus (poliovirus, rhinovirus, Hep A)
- **R**eovirus (rotavirus; dsRNA)
- Almost all other RNA viruses are enveloped.

---

## Positive vs Negative-Sense RNA
- **Positive-sense** = **Ready to translate** (acts directly as mRNA)
- **Negative-sense** = **Needs to be flipped first** (must be converted to + sense by RNA-dependent RNA polymerase)
- Negative-sense viruses must carry their own RNA-dependent RNA polymerase (host doesn't have one)

---

## Prion vs Viroid vs Virus
- **P**rion = **P**rotein only (no nucleic acid). Misfolded PrPSc converts normal PrPC (alpha-helix -> beta-sheet). Causes CJD, mad cow, kuru.
- **V**iroid = circular ss**R**NA only (no protein coat). Infects plants only.
- **V**irus = nucleic acid + protein capsid (+/- envelope)

---

## Retrovirus: "Retro = Backward"
- RNA -> DNA (reverse of Central Dogma)
- Key enzyme: **Reverse Transcriptase** (RNA -> dsDNA)
- Then **Integrase** inserts viral DNA into host genome
- Integrated form = **provirus** (analogous to prophage)
