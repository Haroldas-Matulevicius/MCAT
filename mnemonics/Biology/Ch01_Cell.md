# Cell Mnemonics — Kaplan Biology Ch 1

Covers eukaryotic cells (organelles, cytoskeleton, junctions, endocytosis), prokaryotes, and viruses.

## Cytoskeleton Size Order: "MIcrofilaments are the MIniest"
Size goes up: Microfilaments (7nm) < Intermediate filaments (10nm) < Microtubules (25nm)
- **Microfilaments** = actin (thinnest)
- **Intermediate filaments** = keratin/vimentin/desmin/GFAP/lamins (medium)
- **Microtubules** = alpha/beta tubulin (largest, hollow tubes)

---

## Cytoskeleton Functions: "MF Move Muscles; IF Increase Integrity; MT Move Materials"
- **Microfilaments (actin):** Cell movement, muscle contraction (with myosin), cytokinesis (cleavage furrow)
- **Intermediate filaments:** Structural support/mechanical strength ONLY -- do NOT participate in movement; resist tension
- **Microtubules (tubulin):** Intracellular transport (kinesin/dynein), mitotic spindle, cilia/flagella (9+2)

---

## Motor Protein Direction: "Kinesin = Kind = Positive"
- **Kinesin:** moves toward (+) end of microtubules (anterograde, center -> periphery)
- **Dynein:** moves toward (-) end of microtubules (retrograde, periphery -> center)
- Also: "Kin-away" -- kinesin walks away from the nucleus

---

## Intermediate Filament Types: "Keep Very Decent Grades, Now Learn"
- **K**eratin = epithelial cells
- **V**imentin = mesenchymal cells (connective tissue, fibroblasts)
- **D**esmin = muscle cells
- **G**FAP = glial cells (astrocytes)
- **N**eurofilaments = neurons
- **L**amins = nuclear lamina (all cells)

---

## Cell Junction Proteins
- **T**ight junctions = **C**laudins + **O**ccludins (seal the space)
- **A**dherens + **D**esmosomes = **C**adherins
- **G**ap junctions = **C**onnexins (6 connexins = 1 connexon; 2 connexons = 1 channel)

Cytoskeletal attachments:
- **A**dherens junctions anchor **A**ctin
- **D**esmosomes anchor interme**D**iate filaments

---

## Cell Junction Order (Apical to Basal): "Tight on Top, Adherens After, Desmosomes Down, Gap Go anywhere"
1. Tight junctions (most apical -- seal)
2. Adherens junctions (belt of actin)
3. Desmosomes (spot welds, anchor IFs)
4. Gap junctions (anywhere -- communication channels)

---

## Endocytosis Types
- **Phago**cytosis = cell **eating** (large solid particles: bacteria, dead cells)
- **Pino**cytosis = cell **drinking** (non-selective fluid uptake)
- **Receptor-mediated** = **selective** uptake (e.g., LDL via clathrin-coated pits)
- Greek roots: phagein = to eat; pinein = to drink

---

## Secretory Pathway: "Really Good Proteins"
- **R**ough ER -> **G**olgi -> **P**lasma membrane
- Rough ER: protein synthesis + initial folding + N-linked glycosylation
- Golgi: modification (O-linked glycosylation) + sorting + packaging
- Also: Smooth ER = lipid synthesis + detox + Ca2+ storage
- Lysosomes = degradation (pH ~5, "Lyse stuff")
- Peroxisomes = oxidation, H2O2 production, catalase

---

## Gram Stain: "P = Purple, N = piNk"
- Gram **P**ositive = **P**urple (retain crystal violet; thick peptidoglycan)
- Gram **N**egative = pi**N**k/red (lose crystal violet, take up safranin; thin peptidoglycan + outer membrane)

---

## Gram-Positive vs Gram-Negative Features: "P = Phat peptidoglycan; N = has aN outer membrane"
**Gram-positive:**
- Thick peptidoglycan (~90% of cell wall)
- Teichoic acids
- No outer membrane
- Exotoxins

**Gram-negative:**
- Thin peptidoglycan
- Outer membrane with LPS (endotoxin = Lipid A)
- Periplasmic space
- Porins

---

## Lytic vs Lysogenic: "Lytic = Lyses; Lysogenic = Lying Low"
- **Lytic:** Virus hijacks cell -> replicates -> assembles -> lyses (bursts) host cell -> releases progeny
- **Lysogenic:** Viral DNA integrates as **prophage** -> replicates passively with host -> can be triggered to lytic by stress (UV, DNA damage)

---

## Naked DNA Viruses: "PAPP smear"
- **P**apillomavirus (warts, cervical cancer)
- **A**denovirus (respiratory, conjunctivitis)
- **P**arvovirus (smallest DNA virus, ssDNA)
- **P**olyomavirus (JC, BK viruses)
- All other DNA viruses (herpes, hepadna, pox) are enveloped.

---

## All DNA Virus Families: "HHAPPPy"
- **H**erpes (enveloped, dsDNA)
- **H**epadna (enveloped, partially dsDNA -- Hep B)
- **A**deno (naked, dsDNA)
- **P**ox (enveloped, dsDNA -- largest DNA virus, replicates in CYTOPLASM)
- **P**arvo (naked, ssDNA -- smallest DNA virus)
- **P**apova/Polyoma (naked, dsDNA)
- Rules: All dsDNA except Parvo (ssDNA). All replicate in nucleus except Pox (cytoplasm).

---

## Naked RNA Viruses: "Naked CPR"
- **C**alicivirus (includes norovirus)
- **P**icornavirus (poliovirus, rhinovirus, Hep A)
- **R**eovirus (rotavirus; dsRNA)
- Almost all other RNA viruses are enveloped.

---

## Positive vs Negative-Sense RNA
- **Positive-sense** = **Ready to translate** (acts directly as mRNA)
- **Negative-sense** = **Needs to be flipped first** (must be converted to + sense by RNA-dependent RNA polymerase)
- Negative-sense viruses must carry their own RNA-dependent RNA polymerase (host doesn't have one)

---

## Prion vs Viroid vs Virus
- **P**rion = **P**rotein only (no nucleic acid). Misfolded PrPSc converts normal PrPC (alpha-helix -> beta-sheet). Causes CJD, mad cow, kuru.
- **V**iroid = circular ss**R**NA only (no protein coat). Infects plants only.
- **V**irus = nucleic acid + protein capsid (+/- envelope)

---

## Retrovirus: "Retro = Backward"
- RNA -> DNA (reverse of Central Dogma)
- Key enzyme: **Reverse Transcriptase** (RNA -> dsDNA)
- Then **Integrase** inserts viral DNA into host genome
- Integrated form = **provirus** (analogous to prophage)
