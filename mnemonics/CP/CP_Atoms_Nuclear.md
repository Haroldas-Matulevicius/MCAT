# Atoms & Nuclear Mnemonics

## Orbital Subshells: "Smart People Don't Fail" (SPDF)
- s (l=0), p (l=1), d (l=2), f (l=3)

---

## Periodic Trends: "BEAR"
- **B**asicity: increases UP and to the LEFT
- **E**lectronegativity, Electron affinity, ionization **E**nergy: increase UP and to the RIGHT
- **A**cidity: increases DOWN and to the RIGHT
- **R**adius: increases DOWN and to the LEFT

---

## Electronegativity Ranking: "FONClBrISCH" (fon-cle-brish)
- F > O > N > Cl > Br > I > S > C > H
- Bonus: first three (F, O, N) are also the H-bonding atoms
- Note: Cl and N are very close in Pauling values; this is the standard MCAT approximation

---

## Quantum Numbers: "Hotel Analogy"
- **n** = which floor (energy level/shell): 1, 2, 3...
- **l** = type of room (subshell shape): 0 to (n-1)
- **ml** = which specific room (orbital orientation): -l to +l
- **ms** = top or bottom bunk (electron spin): +1/2 or -1/2

---

## Radioactive Decay Changes (A = mass number, Z = atomic number)
- **Alpha**: A drops by 4, Z drops by 2 (emits helium nucleus 4/2)
- **Beta-minus**: A unchanged, Z increases by 1 (neutron -> proton + electron)
- **Beta-plus (positron)**: A unchanged, Z decreases by 1 (proton -> neutron + positron)
- **Gamma**: No change to A or Z (energy release only)
- "Beta Backwards" -- beta-plus moves you backward on the periodic table

# Ch 4: Stoichiometry, Reactions & Ions

## Redox Identification: "OIL RIG" (or "LEO the lion says GER")
- **O**xidation **I**s **L**oss (of electrons)
- **R**eduction **I**s **G**ain (of electrons)
- Alternative: **L**ose **E**lectrons = **O**xidation / **G**ain **E**lectrons = **R**eduction

---

## Balancing Equations Order: "Make Nice Hot Omelets" (MNHO)
- **M**etals (and unique elements)
- **N**onmetals other than H and O
- **H**ydrogen
- **O**xygen last
- Combustion variant: **CHO** -- Carbon, Hydrogen, Oxygen

---

## Oxyanion Count: "Per is More, Hypo is Low"
- **per-** = extra oxygen (think "super" -- MORE)
- **hypo-** = fewer oxygens (think "hypo" = below, like hypothermia)
- -ate is the reference; -ite has one less O
