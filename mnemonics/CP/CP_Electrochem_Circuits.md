# Electrochem & Circuits Mnemonics

## Oxidation/Reduction: "OIL RIG" or "LEO says GER"
- **O**xidation **I**s **L**oss (of electrons)
- **R**eduction **I**s **G**ain (of electrons)
- **L**ose **E**lectrons = **O**xidation; **G**ain **E**lectrons = **R**eduction

---

## Electrode Assignment: "AN OX, RED CAT"
- **AN**ode = **OX**idation
- **RED**uction = **CAT**hode
- Memory: Anode and Oxidation both start with vowels; Cathode and Reduction with consonants
- This is true for BOTH galvanic and electrolytic cells

---

## Electrode Signs (Galvanic vs. Electrolytic)
- **Galvanic** (spontaneous): Anode is NEGATIVE, Cathode is POSITIVE
- **Electrolytic** (non-spontaneous): Anode is POSITIVE, Cathode is NEGATIVE
- In BOTH: electrons always flow from anode to cathode

---

## Ohm's Law: "VIR Triangle"
- V = IR (cover what you want, remaining letters give the formula)
- I = V/R, R = V/I

---

## Resistors vs. Capacitors: "Opposite Rules"
- Resistors: Series = add directly (R = R1+R2); Parallel = reciprocal (1/R = 1/R1+1/R2)
- Capacitors: OPPOSITE -- Parallel = add directly; Series = reciprocal
- "Whatever rule applies to resistors, flip it for capacitors"
