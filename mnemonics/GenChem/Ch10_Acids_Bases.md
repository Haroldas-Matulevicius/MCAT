# Acids & Bases Mnemonics — Kaplan GChem Ch 10

Covers strong acids/bases, Henderson-Hasselbalch, and titration endpoints.

## Strong Acids: "So I Brought No Clean Clothes"
- **S**o = H2**S**O4 (sulfuric)
- **I** = H**I** (hydroiodic)
- **B**rought = H**Br** (hydrobromic)
- **N**o = H**N**O3 (nitric)
- **Cl**ean = H**Cl** (hydrochloric)
- **Cl**othes = H**Cl**O4 (perchloric)

---

## Strong Bases: "Strong bases live on the left"
- Group 1 hydroxides: LiOH, NaOH, KOH, RbOH, CsOH
- Heavy Group 2 hydroxides: Ca(OH)2, Sr(OH)2, Ba(OH)2
- Mg(OH)2 is NOT strong (insoluble)

---

## Henderson-Hasselbalch Key Fact
- At the **half-equivalence point**: [HA] = [A-], so pH = pKa
- From: pH = pKa + log([A-]/[HA]). When [A-] = [HA], log(1) = 0.

---

## Titration Equivalence Point pH
- Strong acid + strong base = pH **7**
- Weak acid + strong base = pH **> 7** (basic salt)
- Strong acid + weak base = pH **< 7** (acidic salt)
