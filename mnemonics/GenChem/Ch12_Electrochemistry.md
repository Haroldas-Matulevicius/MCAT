# Electrochemistry Mnemonics — Kaplan GChem Ch 12

Covers galvanic vs electrolytic cells, electrode assignment, and electrode signs.

## Electrode Assignment: "AN OX, RED CAT"
- **AN**ode = **OX**idation
- **RED**uction = **CAT**hode
- Memory: Anode and Oxidation both start with vowels; Cathode and Reduction with consonants
- This is true for BOTH galvanic and electrolytic cells

---

## Electrode Signs (Galvanic vs. Electrolytic)
- **Galvanic** (spontaneous): Anode is NEGATIVE, Cathode is POSITIVE
- **Electrolytic** (non-spontaneous): Anode is POSITIVE, Cathode is NEGATIVE
- In BOTH: electrons always flow from anode to cathode
