# Lab Techniques Mnemonics

## Blotting Types: "SNoW DRoP"
- **S**outhern = **D**NA
- **N**orthern = **R**NA
- **W**estern = **P**rotein
- (The "o" letters are fillers. Read the capital letters: S-N-W maps to D-R-P)

---

## PCR Steps: "DAE"
- **D**enature (~95C): heat separates dsDNA into single strands
- **A**nneal (~55-65C): primers bind to complementary sequences
- **E**xtend (~72C): Taq polymerase synthesizes new strand from primers
- Each cycle doubles DNA: 2^n copies after n cycles

---

## Size Exclusion Chromatography: "Big Boys Elute First"
- Large molecules EXCLUDED from pores -> travel AROUND beads -> elute FIRST
- Small molecules ENTER pores -> longer path -> elute LATER
- Note: opposite of gel electrophoresis (where small = faster)

---

## TLC Polarity Rule: "Polar is Lower and Slower"
- On polar (silica) stationary phase: polar compounds interact more with the plate
- Polar compounds = LOWER Rf value (travel less)
- Nonpolar compounds = HIGHER Rf value (travel more)
- Rf = distance compound traveled / distance solvent traveled
- Note: reverses in reverse-phase chromatography (but MCAT primarily tests normal-phase)

---

## SDS-PAGE: "SDS = Size-only Separation"
- SDS denatures proteins and coats them with uniform negative charge
- Eliminates charge and shape as variables -> separation by molecular weight ONLY
- Smaller proteins migrate FARTHER (toward positive anode)
- Native PAGE (no SDS) preserves shape/charge -> separates by size, shape, AND charge
