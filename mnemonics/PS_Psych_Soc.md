# Psych/Soc Mnemonics

## Developmental Stages

### Piaget's Cognitive Development: "Some People Can Fly"
- **S**ensorimotor (0-2): object permanence
- **P**reoperational (2-7): egocentrism, conservation failure
- **C**oncrete operational (7-11): conservation, logical thought about concrete things
- **F**ormal operational (12+): abstract/hypothetical reasoning

### Erikson's 8 Psychosocial Stages: "Trust Autonomy Initiates Industry; Identity Intimacy Generatively Integrates"
1. Trust vs. Mistrust (0-1)
2. Autonomy vs. Shame/Doubt (1-3)
3. Initiative vs. Guilt (3-6)
4. Industry vs. Inferiority (6-12)
5. Identity vs. Role Confusion (12-18)
6. Intimacy vs. Isolation (young adult)
7. Generativity vs. Stagnation (middle adult)
8. Ego Integrity vs. Despair (late adult)

### Freud's Psychosexual Stages: "Old Aged People Love Grandchildren"
- **O**ral (0-1)
- **A**nal (1-3)
- **P**hallic (3-6, Oedipus/Electra complex)
- **L**atency (6-12)
- **G**enital (12+)

### Kohlberg's Moral Development: "PCP"
- **P**re-conventional: Stage 1 = obedience/punishment, Stage 2 = self-interest
- **C**onventional: Stage 3 = social approval ("good boy/girl"), Stage 4 = law and order
- **P**ost-conventional: Stage 5 = social contract, Stage 6 = universal ethical principles

## Personality

### Big Five Traits: "OCEAN"
- **O**penness (to experience)
- **C**onscientiousness
- **E**xtraversion
- **A**greeableness
- **N**euroticism

### Personality Disorder Clusters: "Weird, Wild, Worried"
- Cluster A = "Weird" (odd/eccentric): Paranoid, Schizoid, Schizotypal
- Cluster B = "Wild" (dramatic/erratic): Antisocial, Borderline, Histrionic, Narcissistic
- Cluster C = "Worried" (anxious/fearful): Avoidant, Dependent, OCPD

---

## Brain & Language

### Broca's vs. Wernicke's
- **Broca's** = **B**roken speech (non-fluent/expressive aphasia, frontal lobe)
- **Wernicke's** = **W**ordy but meaningless (fluent/receptive aphasia, temporal lobe)

---

## Memory

### Proactive vs. Retroactive Interference
- **Pro**active = **P**ast blocks **P**resent (old memories interfere with encoding new info)
- **Retro**active = **R**ecent blocks **R**ecall of old (new memories interfere with retrieving old info)

---

## Sleep

### Sleep Stage Brain Waves: "BAT-D"
- **B**eta waves: Awake/Alert
- **A**lpha waves: Drowsy/Relaxed
- **T**heta waves: N1-N2 (light sleep; N2 adds sleep spindles + K-complexes)
- **D**elta waves: N3 (deep/slow-wave sleep)
- REM: returns to Beta-like waves (muscle atonia, dreaming)

---

## Stress

### General Adaptation Syndrome: "People with GAS ARE stressed"
- **A**larm: fight-or-flight activation, sympathetic arousal
- **R**esistance: body adapts, maintains elevated metabolic state
- **E**xhaustion: resources depleted, immune suppression, organ damage

---

## Social Psychology

### Merton's Strain Theory: "CIRRR"
- **C**onformity: accepts goals + accepts means
- **I**nnovation: accepts goals + rejects means (e.g., crime to get rich)
- **R**itualism: rejects goals + accepts means (going through the motions)
- **R**etreatism: rejects goals + rejects means (drops out)
- **R**ebellion: replaces both goals + means with new ones

---

## Attribution

### Three Key Biases
- **F**undamental **A**ttribution **E**rror: overattribute OTHERS' behavior to internal/dispositional factors
- **S**elf-serving bias: attribute OWN success to internal, OWN failure to external
- **A**ctor-observer bias: attribute OWN behavior to situation, OTHERS' to disposition

---

## Emotion Theories: "James, Cannon, Schachter"
- **James-Lange:** stimulus -> body arousal -> emotion (body first)
- **Cannon-Bard:** stimulus -> simultaneous arousal AND emotion (both at once)
- **Schachter-Singer (Two-Factor):** stimulus -> arousal + cognitive label -> emotion (arousal + thinking)

---

## Conditioning

### Operant Conditioning Grid
- Positive/Negative = Add/Remove (NOT good/bad)
- Reinforcement/Punishment = Increase/Decrease behavior

| | Add (Positive) | Remove (Negative) |
|---|---|---|
| **Increase (Reinforcement)** | Add pleasant stimulus | Remove aversive stimulus |
| **Decrease (Punishment)** | Add aversive stimulus | Remove pleasant stimulus |

### Extinction Resistance: "VR > FR > VI > FI"
- **V**ariable **R**atio: most resistant (e.g., gambling/slot machines)
- **F**ixed **R**atio: fast responding, brief post-reinforcement pause
- **V**ariable **I**nterval: slow, steady responding
- **F**ixed **I**nterval: scallop pattern, least resistant to extinction
- Rule: Variable > Fixed; Ratio > Interval
