# Statistics Mnemonics — Kaplan Physics & Math Ch 12

Covers test characteristics, hypothesis testing, statistical power, and distributions.

## Sensitivity/Specificity: "SnNout" and "SpPin"
- **Sn**N**out**: A highly **Se**n**sitive** test, when **N**egative, rules **out** disease (few false negatives)
- **Sp**P**in**: A highly **Sp**ecific test, when **P**ositive, rules **in** disease (few false positives)

---

## Type I vs. Type II Errors: "The N in Negative has II vertical lines"
- Type **I** = false **P**ositive (alpha error): rejecting a true null hypothesis (false alarm)
- Type **II** = false **N**egative (beta error): failing to reject a false null hypothesis (missed detection)
- Visual: the letter "N" has two vertical strokes (||) = Type **II**

---

## Statistical Power: "BEAN"
- **B**eta: as beta increases, power decreases (power = 1 - beta)
- **E**ffect size: larger effect = more power
- **A**lpha: higher alpha (more lenient threshold) = more power
- **N** (sample size): larger N = more power

---

## Skew Direction: "The Foot Trick"
- Look at your feet: LEFT foot shape = negative (left) skew; RIGHT foot shape = positive (right) skew
- **Positive skew:** tail goes RIGHT, mean > median > mode (mean pulled toward tail)
- **Negative skew:** tail goes LEFT, mean < median < mode
