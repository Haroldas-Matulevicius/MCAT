## 1. Nephron Structure

The nephron is the functional unit of the kidney. Each kidney contains ~1 million nephrons.

**Components (in order of fluid flow):**

1. **Glomerulus** -- A tuft of fenestrated capillaries (within Bowman's capsule). Blood enters via the **afferent arteriole** and exits via the **efferent arteriole**. High hydrostatic pressure forces fluid (filtrate) through the filtration barrier.
   - **Filtration barrier**: fenestrated capillary endothelium + basement membrane + podocyte foot processes (with filtration slits). Allows passage of water, ions, glucose, amino acids, urea, small molecules. Blocks cells, large proteins (albumin normally does NOT pass).

2. **Bowman's capsule** -- Cup-like structure that collects filtrate. The filtrate here is essentially protein-free plasma.

3. **Proximal convoluted tubule (PCT)** -- The workhorse of reabsorption. Reabsorbs ~65-70% of filtrate.
   - Reabsorbs: glucose (100%, via SGLT), amino acids, Na+ (Na+/K+ ATPase on basolateral side drives it), HCO3-, water (obligatory, follows solutes), urea (passive), phosphate, K+.
   - Secretes: H+, organic acids, drugs (penicillin), toxins.
   - Has **brush border** (microvilli) for high surface area and abundant **mitochondria** for active transport.
   - Water reabsorption here is NOT regulated by hormones -- it is obligatory and constitutive.

4. **Loop of Henle** -- Creates the **medullary concentration gradient** necessary for urine concentration.
   - **Descending limb**: Permeable to **water**, impermeable to solutes. Water leaves by osmosis as the tubule descends into the hypertonic medulla. Filtrate becomes more concentrated.
   - **Thin ascending limb**: Impermeable to water, passively permeable to NaCl (NaCl diffuses out).
   - **Thick ascending limb**: Impermeable to water, actively transports NaCl out (Na+/K+/2Cl- cotransporter, the target of **loop diuretics** like furosemide). Dilutes the filtrate while making the medulla more concentrated.

5. **Distal convoluted tubule (DCT)** -- Fine-tuning of reabsorption and secretion.
   - Reabsorbs Na+ and Ca2+.
   - **Aldosterone** acts here (and on collecting duct): stimulates Na+ reabsorption and K+ secretion.
   - **PTH** (parathyroid hormone) stimulates Ca2+ reabsorption here.
   - Secretes H+ and K+.
   - Contains the **macula densa** cells (part of juxtaglomerular apparatus) -- senses NaCl concentration in filtrate and signals JG cells to adjust renin release.

6. **Collecting duct** -- Final concentration of urine.
   - **ADH** (antidiuretic hormone, from posterior pituitary) inserts **aquaporin-2 channels** into the apical membrane, allowing water reabsorption. Without ADH, the collecting duct is impermeable to water, and dilute urine is produced (diabetes insipidus).
   - **Aldosterone** also acts here (Na+ reabsorption, K+ secretion).
   - Passes through the hypertonic medulla -- if ADH is present, water follows the osmotic gradient out of the duct, concentrating the urine.
   - Also involved in H+ secretion (acid-base balance) via intercalated cells.

## 2. The Three Renal Processes

| Process | Where | What Happens |
|---------|-------|--------------|
| **Filtration** | Glomerulus | Pressure-driven bulk flow of plasma (minus proteins and cells) into Bowman's capsule. GFR ~125 mL/min (~180 L/day). We reabsorb 99% of this. |
| **Reabsorption** | PCT (most), Loop, DCT, collecting duct | Useful substances move from tubular lumen back into peritubular capillaries. Can be passive (water, urea) or active (glucose, amino acids, Na+). |
| **Secretion** | PCT, DCT, collecting duct | Substances move from peritubular capillaries into the tubular lumen. Important for K+, H+, drugs, toxins. This is the mechanism for fine-tuning composition. |

**Amount excreted = Amount filtered - Amount reabsorbed + Amount secreted**

## 3. Countercurrent Multiplier Mechanism

This is conceptually tricky but very high-yield. The purpose is to create an **osmotic gradient** in the medulla (300 mOsm at cortex to ~1200 mOsm at inner medulla) that allows the collecting duct to produce concentrated urine.

**How it works:**
1. The thick ascending limb actively pumps NaCl out into the medullary interstitium (but is impermeable to water). This makes the medulla salty.
2. The descending limb is permeable to water -- water leaves into the now-hypertonic medulla, concentrating the filtrate inside.
3. As concentrated filtrate rounds the hairpin turn and enters the ascending limb, it has more NaCl to pump out.
4. This positive feedback loop "multiplies" the gradient along the length of the loop.
5. The **vasa recta** (peritubular capillaries running parallel to the Loop of Henle in a hairpin) act as **countercurrent exchangers** -- they follow the gradient passively, picking up solutes on the way down and losing them on the way up. This removes reabsorbed water without washing out the gradient.

**Urea recycling** also contributes: ADH increases urea permeability in the inner medullary collecting duct, allowing urea to be deposited in the medullary interstitium (accounts for ~50% of the medullary osmolality).

## 4. RAAS in Detail (Renal Perspective)

1. Decreased renal perfusion, decreased NaCl at macula densa, or sympathetic activation --> **juxtaglomerular (JG) cells** (modified smooth muscle of afferent arteriole) release **renin**.
2. Renin cleaves angiotensinogen (from liver) --> angiotensin I.
3. ACE (in lungs) converts angiotensin I --> **angiotensin II**.
4. Angiotensin II:
   - Constricts **efferent arteriole** preferentially --> maintains GFR despite low blood pressure.
   - Systemic vasoconstriction --> increases blood pressure.
   - Stimulates **aldosterone** from adrenal cortex --> Na+ reabsorption in DCT/collecting duct --> water follows --> blood volume increases.
   - Stimulates ADH release and thirst.
   - Stimulates Na+ reabsorption in PCT directly.

## 5. ADH Mechanism

- **Stimulus**: High plasma osmolarity detected by osmoreceptors in the hypothalamus, or low blood volume/pressure detected by baroreceptors.
- **Release**: From the **posterior pituitary** (synthesized in hypothalamus, transported to posterior pituitary).
- **Action**: Binds V2 receptors on collecting duct cells --> activates cAMP/PKA signaling --> **aquaporin-2 channels** are inserted into the apical membrane via exocytosis.
- **Result**: Water is reabsorbed from the collecting duct into the hypertonic medullary interstitium --> concentrated urine, diluted blood.
- **Alcohol inhibits ADH release** --> dilute urine --> dehydration (hangover mechanism).

## 6. Acid-Base Balance

The kidneys are critical for long-term pH regulation (the bicarbonate buffer system and respiratory compensation are faster but limited).

**Acidosis (blood pH too low):**
- Kidneys secrete more H+ into the tubular lumen (intercalated cells in collecting duct).
- Kidneys reabsorb more HCO3- (primarily in PCT, using carbonic anhydrase).
- Kidneys generate new HCO3- by metabolizing glutamine --> NH4+ (excreted) + HCO3- (returned to blood).

**Alkalosis (blood pH too high):**
- Kidneys secrete less H+.
- Kidneys excrete HCO3- (reduce reabsorption).

MCAT connection: Understand how the kidneys compensate for respiratory acid-base disorders. Respiratory acidosis (high CO2, low pH) --> kidneys retain HCO3- and secrete H+. Respiratory alkalosis (low CO2, high pH) --> kidneys excrete HCO3-.

## 7. Renal Clearance

**Clearance (C)** = the volume of plasma completely cleared of a substance per unit time.

**C = (U x V) / P**

Where:
- U = urine concentration of the substance (mg/mL)
- V = urine flow rate (mL/min)
- P = plasma concentration of the substance (mg/mL)

**Key benchmark: Inulin** (or creatinine as a clinical proxy)
- Inulin is freely filtered, not reabsorbed, not secreted.
- Therefore, **inulin clearance = GFR** (~125 mL/min).

**Interpreting clearance:**
- Clearance of substance X > GFR --> substance is filtered AND secreted (e.g., PAH at low concentrations).
- Clearance of substance X < GFR --> substance is filtered AND reabsorbed (e.g., glucose, amino acids, Na+).
- Clearance of substance X = GFR --> substance is only filtered (e.g., inulin).
- Clearance = 0 --> substance is completely reabsorbed (e.g., glucose at normal plasma levels).

**PAH (para-aminohippuric acid):**
- At low concentrations, PAH is completely cleared from plasma (filtered + secreted).
- PAH clearance approximates **renal plasma flow (RPF)** (~625 mL/min).
- **Filtration fraction (FF)** = GFR / RPF (~20%). About 20% of plasma is filtered at the glomerulus.

## 8. The Integumentary System (Skin)

**Layers (superficial to deep):**
- **Epidermis** -- Stratified squamous epithelium. Avascular (no blood vessels). Layers from deep to superficial:
  - Stratum basale (stem cells, melanocytes, Merkel cells)
  - Stratum spinosum (Langerhans cells = dendritic/immune cells)
  - Stratum granulosum (keratohyalin granules, lipid secretion)
  - Stratum lucidum (only in thick skin: palms, soles)
  - Stratum corneum (dead, keratinized cells -- main barrier)
- **Dermis** -- Connective tissue with blood vessels, nerves, hair follicles, sweat glands, sebaceous glands. Contains collagen and elastin. Two layers: papillary (loose CT, dermal papillae for fingerprints) and reticular (dense irregular CT).
- **Hypodermis (subcutaneous layer)** -- Adipose tissue for insulation and energy storage. Not technically part of the skin but tested alongside it.

**Thermoregulation:**
- **Heat dissipation (too hot):** Vasodilation of dermal blood vessels (more blood to surface = more radiative heat loss). Sweating (evaporative cooling).
- **Heat conservation (too cold):** Vasoconstriction of dermal blood vessels (shunts blood to core). Piloerection (goosebumps -- limited effectiveness in humans). Shivering (muscle contraction generates heat).
- Controlled by the **hypothalamus** (the body's thermostat).

**Other skin functions:** Physical barrier, UV protection (melanin from melanocytes), vitamin D synthesis (UV-B converts 7-dehydrocholesterol to cholecalciferol in the skin), sensation (mechanoreceptors, thermoreceptors, nociceptors), immune defense (Langerhans cells).

## High-Yield Summary: What to Memorize (Homeostasis)

- **Nephron flow:** glomerulus → Bowman's → PCT → loop of Henle (descending → ascending) → DCT → collecting duct.
- **PCT:** ~65-70% of everything reabsorbed (obligatory). 100% of glucose & amino acids.
- **Descending limb:** water out only. **Ascending limb:** NaCl out only (impermeable to water).
- **Aldosterone** (from adrenal cortex, RAAS-driven): Na+ in, K+ out at DCT/collecting duct.
- **ADH** (from posterior pit): aquaporin-2 → water in at collecting duct.
- **Inulin clearance = GFR ≈ 125 mL/min.** PAH clearance ≈ RPF ≈ 625 mL/min. FF ≈ 20%.
- **Acid-base:** lungs handle CO2 (fast); kidneys handle HCO3- and H+ (slow, sustained).
- **Skin layers:** epidermis (avascular, keratinized) → dermis (vessels, nerves, glands) → hypodermis (fat).
- **Thermoregulation:** vasodilation + sweat to cool; vasoconstriction + shiver to warm. Hypothalamus = thermostat.
